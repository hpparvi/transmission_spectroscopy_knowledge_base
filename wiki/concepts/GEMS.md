# GEMS

**Giant Exoplanets around M-dwarf Stars** — the class of Saturn- to Jupiter-mass planets transiting M dwarfs, named in part by the [[JWST-GO-3171]] "Red Dwarfs and the Seven Giants" survey (PIs Kanodia, Cañas, Libby-Roberts; gemsjwst.github.io). GEMS sit at the intersection of two challenging regimes: hot/warm-Jupiter atmospheric chemistry (CH₄, CO, CO₂, H₂O retrievals) and M-dwarf [[stellar-contamination]] (TLS effect — spots/faculae often degenerate with planetary features). Theoretically, GEMS are also harder to form via core accretion in low-mass-star protoplanetary disks, so their existence and atmospheric composition probe planet formation in disks unlike the Sun's.

## Open questions

- **Are GEMS preferentially metal-poor in their atmospheres?** Two wiki GEMS papers ([[2026_Ashtari_HATS75b]] HATS-75 b log [M/H] = −1.74; [[2025_Canas_TOI5205b]] TOI-5205 b log [M/H] = −1.92 (PLATON) or −0.76 (POSEIDON)) **both find sub-solar atmospheric metallicities** in TLS frameworks. Possibly degenerate with stellar contamination — but the pattern across 2 of 7 [[JWST-GO-3171]] targets is suggestive.
- **Atmosphere–bulk metallicity gap.** TOI-5205 b: atmospheric [M/H] ≈ −2 to −0.8 vs interior bulk metallicity ~10–20% (~+1.5 to +2.3 dex) — a **~100× decoupling**. Three explanations: (i) atmosphere accreted from low-metallicity disk gas not mixed with interior; (ii) heavy-element settling/sequestration; (iii) inefficient vertical mixing.
- **Super-solar C/O ≈ 1.2–1.3** on TOI-5205 b ([[2025_Canas_TOI5205b]]) — formation tracer or photochemistry artifact? Comparable analyses on more GEMS would test.
- How well can [[back-to-back-transit-correction]] or other contamination-mitigation strategies be applied to GEMS, where rocky companion-planet references are typically unavailable?

## Papers

- [[2026_Ashtari_HATS75b]] — first wiki paper on a GEMS target; HATS-75 b NIRSpec PRISM survey; sub-solar atmospheric metallicity.
- [[2025_Canas_TOI5205b]] — second [[JWST-GO-3171]] GEMS-JWST result; TOI-5205 b (1.08 M_J around M4 dwarf, one of the most extreme GEMS); CH₄ + H₂S detected; sub-solar atmospheric metallicity + super-solar C/O; >20σ TLS detection; ~100× decoupled from predicted bulk metallicity.

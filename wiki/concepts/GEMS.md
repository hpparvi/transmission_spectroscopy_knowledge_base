# GEMS

**Giant Exoplanets around M-dwarf Stars** — the class of Saturn- to Jupiter-mass planets transiting M dwarfs, named in part by the [[JWST-GO-3171]] "Red Dwarfs and the Seven Giants" survey (PIs Kanodia, Cañas, Libby-Roberts; gemsjwst.github.io). GEMS sit at the intersection of two challenging regimes: hot/warm-Jupiter atmospheric chemistry (CH₄, CO, CO₂, H₂O retrievals) and M-dwarf [[stellar-contamination]] (TLS effect — spots/faculae often degenerate with planetary features). Theoretically, GEMS are also harder to form via core accretion in low-mass-star protoplanetary disks, so their existence and atmospheric composition probe planet formation in disks unlike the Sun's.

## Open questions

- Are GEMS preferentially metal-poor in their atmospheres? [[2026_Ashtari_HATS75b]] reports log [M/H] = −1.74 on HATS-75 b (sub-solar) — but possibly degenerate with stellar contamination.
- How does the bulk-vs-atmospheric metallicity gap (Z_p ≈ 0.20 vs log [M/H] ≈ −1.74 on HATS-75 b) generalize across the GEMS class? Inefficient vertical mixing or formation history?
- How well can [[back-to-back-transit-correction]] or other contamination-mitigation strategies be applied to GEMS, where rocky companion-planet references are typically unavailable?

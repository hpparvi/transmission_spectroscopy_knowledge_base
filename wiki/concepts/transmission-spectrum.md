# Transmission spectrum

The wavelength-dependent apparent planet radius Rp(λ) (or transit depth (Rp/R⋆)²(λ)) measured through [[transmission-spectroscopy]]. Features encode absorption and scattering by the day–night terminator atmosphere, modulated by cloud/haze opacity and — for M-dwarf hosts especially — potential [[stellar-contamination]] from unocculted heterogeneities.

## Papers
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — four-visit coadded GJ 1132 b transmission spectrum best fit by a flat line; demonstrates [[multi-visit-stacking]] as a rocky-planet detection discriminator.
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — first broadband 3–12 μm (NIR + MIR) transmission spectrum of a rocky exoplanet; featureless.
- [[2025_Alderson_TOI-776b]] — two-visit NIRSpec G395H transmission spectrum of TOI-776 b; flat after detector-offset correction.
- [[2024_Scarsdale_L98-59c]] — two-visit NIRSpec G395H transmission spectrum of L 98-59 c; featureless at 22/36 ppm in R~200.
- [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] — single-visit L 98-59 d transmission spectrum with a ~3.3–4.8 μm feature driven by [[H2S]] / [[SO2]].
- [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] — retrieval-focused analysis of the same L 98-59 d transmission spectrum; favors high-MMW secondary atmosphere.
- [[2024_Alderson_TOI-836b]] — two-visit NIRSpec G395H transmission spectrum of TOI-836 b; combined 25 ppm precision in R~200.
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — two G395H transmission spectra of GJ 1132 b that disagree between visits at 2.8–5.2 μm.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — two-visit G395H transmission spectrum of GJ 486 b with a blueward slope; water-rich atmosphere and starspot contamination scenarios fit equally well.
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — featureless two-visit transmission spectrum of LHS 475 b; demonstrates no NIRSpec noise floor above 5 ppm.

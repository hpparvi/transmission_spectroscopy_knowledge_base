# SO₂ photochemical shoreline

A proposed photochemical "shoreline" in the (Teq, atmospheric metallicity) plane separating hot-Jupiter atmospheres in which photochemical [[SO2]] production from H₂S / H₂O is observable from those in which it is suppressed (Crossfield et al. 2025; not yet ingested). Built from grid models of dayside / terminator photochemistry. The first observational benchmark target was [[WASP-39b]] (Teq ≈ 1100 K) from the JWST ERS data; [[2026_Radica_WASP96b]] adds [[WASP-96b]] (Teq ≈ 1300 K) as a second on-shoreline candidate at 10–20× solar metallicity.

## Open questions

- How robust is the SO₂ inference to data-reduction choice? [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]] documented up to 2 dex variation in retrieved abundances on the same WASP-39 b ERS PRISM data.
- What is the upper Teq limit of the shoreline before thermal dissociation suppresses SO₂?
- Can H₂S (the SO₂ precursor) be co-detected to test the photochemistry directly?

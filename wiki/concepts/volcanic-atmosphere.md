# Volcanic atmosphere

An atmosphere whose composition is primarily set by **volcanic outgassing** balancing atmospheric loss — rather than primordial accretion or impact-delivered volatiles. The wiki's first inference of a volcanic atmosphere is [[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]] on the sub-Earth [[L98-59b]], where an SO₂-dominated atmosphere is preferred over a flat line at 3.3–3.8σ in JWST NIRSpec G395H. Diagnostic species are typically **SO₂** (oxidized mantle), **H₂S** (reduced mantle), **CO₂**, and **H₂O** — with the SO₂/H₂S ratio probing mantle oxygen fugacity.

## Physics

The presence and composition of a volcanic atmosphere are set by three competing rates:
1. **Volcanic outgassing rate** Ṁ_volc = M_volc · x, where x is the volatile (e.g., S) content in the magma (~100–200 ppm bulk-silicate-Earth value) and M_volc is the extrusive volcanic rate. Strongly dependent on tidal-heating rate per unit mass; on Io, Ṁ_volc ≈ 7 × 10⁶ kg/s.
2. **Atmospheric escape rate** Ṁ_escape = energy-limited XUV-driven (with efficiency ~1%) ≈ 2 × 10⁵ kg/s for L 98-59 b.
3. **Steady state**: Ṁ_volc = Ṁ_escape requires ≥8× Io's tidal heating per unit mass for L 98-59 b.

If volcanism is driven by **runaway mantle melting** (Peale & Cassen 1978; Peale et al. 1979, applied to Io 1979; D. Z. Seligman et al. 2024; all not ingested), the inferred tidal heating constrains the **tidal quality factor Q** and the size of the **subsurface magma ocean** (typically R_m ≈ 0.6–0.9 R_p for eccentric M-dwarf rocky planets).

## Discriminating observables

- **SO₂ (oxidized mantle, f_O₂ > IW + 2.7)** vs **H₂S/H₂ (reduced mantle, f_O₂ < IW − 4)** — SO₂/H₂S ratio is the primary oxygen-fugacity proxy.
- **Sulfur-allotrope inversion**: photochemical S₈, S₃ produce a moderate temperature inversion (~50–100 K) in the upper atmosphere via visible-band absorption.
- **CO₂ and H₂O secondary tracers** — present in most volcanic outgassed atmospheres but at lower abundances; H₂O can be lost via XUV escape in long-evolution scenarios.

## Cosmic-shoreline implications

A confirmed volcanic atmosphere on a planet inside the canonical [[cosmic-shoreline]] (i.e., on the "airless" side) implies that **secondary outgassing can replenish atmospheres lost to early high-energy stellar activity**. This challenges the standard escape-velocity-vs-insolation shoreline. [[L98-59b]] joins [[TOI-270b]], [[2025_Teske_TOI561b]], and [[HD3167b]] as wiki shoreline challengers.

# Cosmic shoreline

The hypothesized boundary in (planetary escape velocity) × (cumulative stellar XUV irradiation) space that separates rocky bodies that retain atmospheres from those that do not (Zahnle & Catling 2017). For close-in M-dwarf rocky planets — where stellar XUV histories are intense and prolonged — the shoreline is expected to shift relative to the solar-system expression, making "does this planet host an atmosphere?" a first-order JWST-era question. The observational test is typically transit and/or eclipse spectroscopy of targets bracketing the shoreline.

## Papers
- [[2025_Coulombe_TOI-270b]] — **possibly challenges the cosmic shoreline**: TOI-270 b sits firmly on the airless side (next to TRAPPIST-1 b/c, GJ 486 b, GJ 1132 b, LTT 1445 A b) but JWST/NIRSpec G395H shows a tentative H₂O absorption feature (lnB = 0.3–3.2); coupled atmosphere–interior modeling ([[PACMAN-P]]) demonstrates that a sufficiently large initial volatile inventory could sustain an H₂O-, O₂-, or H₂-dominated atmosphere over Gyr timescales despite high cumulative XUV.
- [[2025_Connors_MIRI-15um]] — uniform FN-PCA reanalysis of MIRI F1500W eclipses on five rocky planets places three (TRAPPIST-1 b, LHS 1140 c, possibly TOI-1468 b) on the airless side; TRAPPIST-1 c moves modestly toward the atmospheric side; LHS 1478 b inconclusive. A useful methodology baseline for the [[JWST-DD-9235]] Rocky Worlds DDT survey ahead.
- [[2026_Coy_HD3167b]] — **challenges the cosmic shoreline**: HD 3167 b (USP, P = 0.96 d, Teq ≈ 1786 K, K-dwarf host) shows a MIRI/LRS eclipse depth ~5σ below bare-rock; extends the atmospheric-retention anomaly to true USP super-Earths.
- [[2025_Teske_TOI561b]] — **challenges the cosmic shoreline**: TOI-561 b (USP, P = 0.446 d, Teq ≈ 2300 K, ancient K-dwarf host ~10 Gyr) lies well above the expected atmosphere-retention boundary; JWST G395H secondary eclipses indicate a dayside temperature far below the bare-rock prediction, consistent with a thick volatile-rich atmosphere surviving over the system lifetime.
- [[2025_Redai_GJ357b]] — COMPASS NIRSpec G395H follow-up of GJ 357 b; extends the atmospheric exclusion to MMW < 8 g/mol; combined with Taylor et al., provides the strongest current multi-wavelength transmission constraint for a target near the shoreline.
- [[2025_Taylor_GJ357b]] — one of the most physically detailed shoreline analyses in the wiki: models the full XUV luminosity evolution of GJ 357 using MORS tracks and finds the star has been sub-threshold for transonic escape since ~10³ Myr, making GJ 357 b a favourable candidate for secondary-atmosphere retention despite its present-day shoreline position.
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — argues GJ 1132 b is a bare rock and therefore sits on the atmosphere-less side of the shoreline.
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — photoevaporation modeling shows L 168-9 b could not have retained a primordial H/He atmosphere beyond the first ~200 Myr for initial atmospheric mass fractions of 2–100%.
- [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]] — MIRI/LRS emission constraint places Gl 486 b clearly on the airless-body side of the shoreline; one of the most precise shoreline-relevant constraints to date.
- [[2024_Wachiraphan_LTT1445Ab]] — MIRI/LRS emission rules out thick atmospheres on LTT 1445 A b; joint Bond-albedo / heat-redistribution analysis consistent with the airless-body side.
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — invokes the shoreline as the theoretical motivation for the CHAMPs program's target selection.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — title explicitly invokes the shoreline ("High Tide or Riptide on the Cosmic Shoreline?"); places GJ 486 b just above the expected H₂-atmosphere cutoff, but the transmission-vs-contamination degeneracy prevents a clean shoreline placement in the paper itself (later resolved by [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]]).
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — CHAMPs target; first JWST transmission spectrum of an Earth-sized rocky planet at the shoreline edge.

# KELT-7 b

A hot Jupiter (1.39 M_J, 1.60 R_J, T_eq ≈ 2048 K, 2.73-day orbit) discovered by Bieryla et al. 2015 (not yet ingested) around the F2 host [[KELT-7]]. Notable for its **aligned, prograde orbit** (obliquity λ = −10.55° ± 0.27°; Tabernero et al. 2022, not ingested) — making it a prime BOWIE-ALIGN target for testing the alignment-vs-formation history hypothesis. Pre-JWST HST work (Pluriel et al. 2020, Gascón et al. 2025; not ingested) reported H₂O and tentative H⁻ continuum opacity. JWST NIRSpec G395H ([[2025_Ahrer_KELT7b]]) shows weak features compatible with either a high-altitude cloud deck or a low-metallicity atmosphere; equilibrium chemistry slightly preferred at C/O = 0.43–0.74 and metallicity 1–16× solar.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Tentative:** [[H2O]] (Δ ln Z = 1.0–1.9, weak by Sellke 2001 mapping), [[CO2]] (Δ ln Z = 0.9–1.5).
- **Ruled out / not detected:** [[CO]] (Δ ln Z = −0.7), CH₄, NH₃, HCN, [[H2S]]; high-T species SiO, VO, TiO, AlO, SH disfavored on physical grounds despite formal Bayes-factor preference.
- **Atmospheric metallicity:** 1–16× solar (equilibrium chemistry); single-digit × solar to genuinely low (free chemistry).
- **C/O:** 0.43–0.74; consistent with stellar (0.41–0.89 from Polanski 2022 / Kolecki & Wang 2022).
- **Cloud deck:** preferred at log P_cloud ≈ 0.6 bar (high-altitude); SiO₂ or Al₂O₃ condensates plausible at T_eq ≈ 2050 K.

## Observations to date

- **JWST NIRSpec G395H** ([[JWST-GO-3838]] BOWIE-ALIGN; [[2025_Ahrer_KELT7b]]) — single 7.51-h transit on 2024 February 3; mirror tilt event at ~one-third transit (smaller than [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]]'s WASP-39 b event); modeled as step function.
- **HST WFC3/G141** (Pluriel et al. 2020; not yet ingested) — first H₂O detection.
- **HST UVIS/G280** (Gascón et al. 2025; not ingested) — featureless 0.19–0.8 μm; confirms H⁻ at the H/He continuum level.

# GJ 436 b

The **archetype warm Neptune** (M_p = 25.43 M⊕ = 0.08 M_J, R_p = 4.03 R⊕ = 0.36 R_J, ρ ≈ 2.1 g/cm³) on an eccentric (e = 0.16) 2.64-day orbit around the bright (J = 6.07) nearby M3 dwarf [[GJ436]] (Butler et al. 2004; Gillon et al. 2007; not ingested). The first transiting exo-Neptune. JWST/MANATEE 8-eclipse panchromatic 2.4–12 μm thermal-emission spectrum ([[2025_Mukherjee_GJ436b]]) **overturns the Spitzer-era inference of extreme metallicity + hot interior**: the JWST 3.6 μm flux is significantly fainter than Spitzer reported, allowing **[M/H] ≥ 80–300× solar** (depending on chemistry assumption) and **T_int ~ 60 K** instead of the Spitzer-era ≥ 300 K tidal-heating-driven inference. Day-side T_day = 662.8 ± 5.0 K — consistent with zero-albedo equilibrium temperature; uniquely (with [[GJ1214b]]) GJ 436 b has T_day / T_eq ≤ 1.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Tentatively detected (emission):**
  - [[CO2]] at 2σ; log VMR ≈ −3.23⁺¹·⁶⁶₋₃·₃₀ ([[2025_Mukherjee_GJ436b]]).
- **No firm detection of:** CH₄, H₂O, NH₃, CO, HCN, C₂H₂, OCS, H₂S, SO₂ (all 2σ upper limits, log VMR < −4 to −5).
- **HST/WFC3 transmission (Knutson et al. 2014a; not ingested):** featureless — consistent with the cloudy-equilibrium model with [M/H] ≥ 500× solar.
- **CRIRES+ transmission (Grasser et al. 2024; not ingested):** rules out cloud-free metal-poor; consistent with cloudy/metal-rich.

## Bulk properties (revised by JWST)

- Day-side T_day = 662.8 ± 5.0 K (consistent with T_eq = 675 K).
- Bond albedo A_B = 0.43⁺⁰·²³₋₀·₄₀ (3σ upper limit ≤ 0.66).
- Heat-recirculation efficiency ε = 0.62⁺⁰·³⁸₋₀·₄₂ (unconstrained).
- **Intrinsic temperature T_int ~ 60 K** in disequilibrium-chemistry fits (5× lower than Spitzer-era 300–400 K).
- **Atmospheric [M/H] ≥ 80–500× solar** depending on chemistry / cloud assumptions:
  - Clear disequilibrium: ≥ 80× solar (2σ); ≥ 130× (1σ).
  - Cloudy equilibrium: ≥ 130× solar (2σ); ≥ 300× (1σ).
  - Clear equilibrium: ≥ 500× solar (1σ).

## Observations to date

**JWST MANATEE survey ([[JWST-GO-1177]] + [[JWST-GO-1185]]; PI: Schlawin):**
- 3× [[JWST-NIRCam]] F322W2 grism (2.4–4.0 μm) eclipses; 3× NIRCam F444W (3.9–5.0 μm); 2× [[JWST-MIRI]] LRS (5–12 μm) eclipses.
- All 8 eclipses collected 2022-12-07 → 2023-12-21.
- Two independent reductions: [[Tshirt]] (primary) and [[Eureka]]; agreement < 1σ.

**Pre-JWST:**
- Spitzer IRAC photometry (Stevenson et al. 2010; Lanotte et al. 2014; Morley et al. 2017; all not ingested) — the 3.6 μm flux of ~120–200 ppm is now overturned by JWST (~50 ppm).
- HST/WFC3 transmission (Knutson et al. 2014a; not ingested) — featureless; cloud + high-MMW consistent.

## Open questions

- **Is the metal-rich atmosphere cloudy or driven by disequilibrium chemistry?** Two scenarios give 2× different metallicity inferences; an unambiguous CO or H₂O detection at 5σ would settle.
- **Where does the tidal heating go?** Eccentricity (0.16) demands dissipation, but T_int ≈ 60 K is too low for the heating energy to be photospheric. Tidal heating may sink into the deep interior or escape via atmospheric circulation.
- **Phase-curve constraint** could break the high-albedo vs high-metallicity degeneracy.
- **Why JWST < Spitzer at 3.6 μm?** Spitzer pointing/sweet-spot artifact most likely; Mukherjee et al. refit unpublished 2018 Spitzer phase-curve eclipses and find 46 ± 43 ppm — consistent with JWST.

# HD 189733 b

A 1.15 M_J / 1.16 R_J hot Jupiter on a 2.2-day orbit around the active K1V dwarf [[HD189733]] (Bouchy et al. 2005; not yet ingested). Together with [[HD209458b]], the most extensively observed transiting exoplanet in the pre-JWST era — the historical archetype against which **pM/pL-class hot-Jupiter dichotomy** ([[2013_Pont_HD189733b]]; Fortney et al. 2008, not yet ingested) and **panchromatic transmission-spectrum interpretation** were calibrated. JWST NIRCam F322W2 + F444W transmission spectrum reported in Fu et al. 2024 (not ingested) and included in the population-level analysis of [[2025_Fu_statistical-trends]].

## Basic properties
M_p = 1.15 M_J, R_p ≈ 1.156 R_J (R_p/R_⋆ ≈ 0.156), P = 2.21857567 d, a = 0.0314 AU, T_eq ≈ 1200 K (irradiation T_eq ≈ 1700 K depending on heat-redistribution efficiency), atmospheric scale height ≈ 150 km. The host is a bright (V ≈ 7.7), photometrically active K1V dwarf rotating in ~12 d — its starspot variability dominates the transmission-spectrum systematics budget.

## Atmospheric composition

The wiki's currently-ingested constraints are pre-JWST. **No species are yet detected at unambiguous high significance** in the wiki's papers; instead, two competing scenarios fit the dataset:

- **Scenario A (Pont et al. dust):** Pervasive haze of submicrometre condensate grains spanning ≥5 atmospheric scale heights ([[2008_Pont_HD189733b]], [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]]); narrow [[Na]] D core at the 4σ level (Huitson 2012; not ingested) and tentative [[K]] core at 2.5σ ([[2013_Pont_HD189733b]]); broad alkali wings absent; broad H₂O bands muted to ~0–1 scale height.
- **Scenario B (McCullough clear+spots):** Solar-composition clear atmosphere with [[H2O]] ≈ 5×10⁻⁴ at T ≈ 700 K ([[2014_McCullough_HD189733b]]; 1.4 μm feature at ~200 ppm, ~8σ at 75 nm binning); UV-to-visible "Rayleigh slope" produced by unocculted starspots (T_spot ≈ 3 700 K, δ ≈ 5.6 %) on the active K-dwarf host; alkali features removed from the observable atmosphere by Na/K condensation onto KCl/Na₂S sulfides at the cool terminator (Fortney et al. 2005).

A high-altitude cloud deck (≥ 0.1 bar) is *not* excluded in either scenario.

From the JWST NIRCam population-level analysis ([[2025_Fu_statistical-trends]]): SO₂-L = −0.90 ± 0.09 (among the lowest SO₂-L values in the 8-planet sample), CO₂-L = 1.74 ± 0.09, CO-L = 0.43 ± 0.08. Bulk Z fraction 0.13 ± 0.03.

## Observations to date

The wiki currently captures the foundational pre-JWST panchromatic story:

- **2008** — [[2008_Pont_HD189733b]] — first three HST/ACS G800L transits (550–1050 nm); featureless visible spectrum with a 472 ± 42 km redward slope; first claim of an aerosol detection in an exoplanet atmosphere.
- **2013** — [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — full HST + Spitzer panchromatic 0.3–24 μm spectrum (STIS + ACS + WFC3 + NICMOS + IRAC + MIPS); coherent unocculted-spot AC-component correction via 5+ years of APT photometry + Gaussian-process interpolation; near-monotonic Rayleigh-slope from UV through near-IR plus narrow Na D / tentative K cores. Establishes the "dusty" hot-Jupiter scenario and explains four otherwise-puzzling phase-curve and emission features (large 3.6 μm phase amplitude, small hot-spot offset, near-blackbody dayside, 8 μm/4.5 μm opacity inversion).
- **2014** — [[2014_McCullough_HD189733b]] — single HST/WFC3 G141 spatial-scan transit (1.1–1.7 μm); detects 200 ± 47 ppm 1.4 μm H₂O feature + 83 ± 53 ppm 1.15 μm feature; reinterprets the panchromatic slope of [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] as **unocculted starspot contamination plus a clear atmosphere**, with Na/K condensation explaining the missing alkali wings. The two interpretations remain unresolved on this dataset.
- **JWST NIRCam (Fu et al. 2024; not ingested)** — F322W2 + F444W transmission; population entry in [[2025_Fu_statistical-trends]] establishes its place in the SO₂-L color-color framework.

## Open questions

- **Dust vs. starspots on the panchromatic slope.** The defining methodological question raised by this target — directly resolved only by joint TLS + dust retrieval at JWST precision, which has not been performed on this target.
- **Composition of the haze (if real).** Silicates (enstatite, forsterite, corundum), Fe, photochemical hydrocarbons all candidates; no observational discriminant yet.
- **Cloud-deck pressure.** Not constrained below ~0.1 bar in either scenario.
- **JWST-era transmission spectrum with modern TLS modeling.** A direct NIRSpec/NIRISS observation is the natural way to settle Pont vs. McCullough; not yet ingested in the wiki.

## Tensions

- **Panchromatic slope: dust-haze (Pont 2008/2013) vs. unocculted starspots (McCullough 2014).** The same 0.3–1 μm STIS+ACS+WFC3 spectrum is attributed to two physically distinct mechanisms of comparable Bayesian merit. **Driver of the difference:** the unocculted-spot DC component (steady minimum spot dimming) — Pont et al. apply only the variability-driven AC component, McCullough et al. add a steady δ ≈ 5.6 % background at T_spot ≈ 3 700 K. Two later theoretical lines of work bear on the disagreement:
  - [[2018_Rackham_TLS]] — formalizes the [[stellar-contamination|TLS]] effect for M dwarfs; demonstrates square-root scaling of variability with spot fraction (variability monitoring underestimates true spot coverage).
  - [[2019_Rackham_TLS-FGK]] — extends to K dwarfs and explicitly cites the McCullough HD 189733 b reinterpretation; shows that K-dwarf unocculted spots produce visible blueward slopes at the right magnitude. **Vindicates the McCullough mechanism as quantitatively viable**, without proving it correct over Pont et al.'s dust scenario. As of this writing, the wiki preserves both readings as live hypotheses.

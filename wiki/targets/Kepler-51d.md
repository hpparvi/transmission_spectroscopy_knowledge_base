# Kepler-51 d

Cool [[super-puff]] (M = 5.6 ± 1.2 M⊕, R = 9.32 ± 0.18 R⊕ → ρ = 0.038 ± 0.009 g/cm³, P = 130.2 d, T_eq ≈ 350 K) — one of the lowest-density exoplanets known — orbiting the 500 Myr Sun-like star [[Kepler-51]]. **The only known planet with a featureless sloped JWST transmission spectrum spanning 0.6–5.3 μm** ([[2025_LibbyRoberts_Kepler51d]]). The slope is best explained by a **high-altitude photochemical haze layer at 1–100 μbar** above an extended H/He envelope; tholin / soot / sulfur compositions equally viable. A thin tilted ring of porous submicron particles is a physically plausible alternative — but its Pointing–Robertson lifetime (∼ 0.1 Myr) makes a steady-state ring implausible in a 500 Myr system unless the result of a recent collision. Slow rotation (>33 hr; Lammers & Winn 2024 and Liu et al. 2024, not ingested) further constrains formation history.

## Mass / radius / class note

Class assigned as `neptune` to honor the Jupiter-units convention (M = 5.6 M⊕ ≈ 0.0176 MJ; R = 9.32 R⊕ ≈ 0.831 RJ). The planet is a *super-puff* in the dynamical sense (extreme low density, near-resonant orbit) rather than a sub-Neptune; the `neptune` class is the closest match in the current taxonomy.

## Atmospheric composition

- [[CH4]] — tentative at 2.2σ; ppb-level mixing ratio; interpreted as upwelling from the deeper atmosphere, NOT bulk abundance ([[2025_LibbyRoberts_Kepler51d]])

## Open questions

- **Haze composition**: tholin / soot / sulfur Mie continua are degenerate in the sloped 0.6–5.3 μm spectrum; MIRI/LRS may discriminate.
- **Tilted-ring transient scenario**: cannot be ruled out as a recent post-collisional event; repeated transits would test variability.
- **Slow rotation (>33 hr)**: implies unusual angular-momentum history during gas accretion (Ormel et al. 2015 expectation of near-breakup rotation violated).
- **System-wide haze hypothesis**: Kepler-51 b and c need similar JWST observations to test whether high-altitude hazes are universal in this super-puff system.

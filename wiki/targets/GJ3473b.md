# GJ 3473 b

Highly-irradiated rocky super-Earth (Rp = 1.264 R⊕, Mp = 1.86 M⊕, Teq ≈ 773 K) on a 1.198-day orbit around the mid-M dwarf [[GJ3473]] (Kemmer et al. 2020; not yet ingested). Four MIRI F1500W eclipses under the Hot Rocks Survey ([[JWST-GO-3730]]) yield a joint depth of 186 ± 45 ppm and R = 0.82 ± 0.12 — between bare-rock and atmospheric expectations. Both scenarios remain compatible; thick CO₂ atmospheres above ~1.2–6.5 bar are excluded ([[2026_Holmberg_GJ3473b]]). Tentative visit-to-visit eclipse-depth variability flagged.

# HD 189733

Active K1V (sometimes K2V) dwarf (T_eff ≈ 5050 K, R⋆ ≈ 0.755 R☉, M⋆ ≈ 0.806 M☉, [Fe/H] = −0.03; Bouchy et al. 2005, not yet ingested) hosting the hot Jupiter [[HD-189733b]]. The brightness (V ≈ 7.7) made this system second only to [[HD-209458]] for pre-JWST atmospheric characterization. Photometrically variable at the few-percent level on its ~12-day rotation period (Henry & Winn 2008; Croll et al. 2007 MOST campaign; not yet ingested), with ubiquitous occulted-spot crossings during HST visible transits. The active-K-dwarf nature is central to the [[2014_McCullough_HD189733b]] reinterpretation of the panchromatic transmission-spectrum slope as [[stellar-contamination]] (transit-light-source-effect) rather than [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]]'s Rayleigh-scattering haze.

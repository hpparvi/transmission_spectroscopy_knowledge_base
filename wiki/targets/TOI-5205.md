# TOI-5205

M4 dwarf (T_eff = 3430 K, M_⋆ = 0.394 M_⊙, R_⋆ = 0.392 R_⊙, [Fe/H] = 0.56) hosting the [[GEMS]]-class gas-giant [[TOI-5205b]]. Bright (J = 11.93). Active host showing visible-band rotational modulation; the JWST NIRSpec PRISM observations reveal spot-crossing events in all 3 visits ([[2025_Canas_TOI5205b]]). Spot T_spot ≈ 3156⁺⁴⁵₋₅₆ K (~270 K cooler than photosphere); spot covering fraction f_spot ≈ 0.13; faculae T_fac ≈ 4263 K, f_fac ≈ 0.015. Stellar-contamination (TLS) drives a strong blueward slope in the transmission spectrum below 3 μm.

The high metallicity ([Fe/H] = 0.56) measured here from JWST data is consistent with Kanodia et al. 2023 (not ingested) and helps refine the bulk-disk-mass argument that motivates GEMS formation puzzles around the smallest M-dwarfs.

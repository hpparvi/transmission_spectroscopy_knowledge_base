# L 98-59

A bright (J=7.93) nearby M3V dwarf (Teff=3415 K, R=0.303 R☉, [Fe/H]=-0.46) hosting three transiting rocky planets and a nontransiting fourth candidate (Cloutier et al. 2019; Kostov et al. 2019; Demangeon et al. 2021; not yet ingested). The system is a favored JWST small-planet target due to the host's brightness and the planets' favorable transmission spectroscopy metric.

The system offers the rare opportunity to compare atmospheres of *multiple* rocky planets around the *same* star: JWST observations to date show **[[L98-59b]]** as a [[volcanic-atmosphere]] candidate (SO₂ at 3.3–3.8σ; [[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]]), **[[L98-59c]]** as featureless ([[2024_Scarsdale_L98-59c]]), and **[[L98-59d]]** with sulfur-species hints ([[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]], [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]]) — a system-level divergence plausibly driven by differences in tidal heating and bulk composition (L 98-59 d is a water-world candidate). The Bello-Arufe inference of widespread mantle melting on L 98-59 b adds a system-scale tidal-heating + outgassing context: if confirmed, similar mechanisms may operate on c (most likely; no atmospheric signature observed) and d (sulfur-bearing, consistent with shared mantle-volatile inventory).

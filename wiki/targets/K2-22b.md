# K2-22 b

An ultra-short-period rocky planet (P = 9.1457 hr = 0.381 days, Teq ≈ 3830 K) actively disintegrating around the K-type dwarf [[K2-22]], discovered by Sanchis-Ojeda et al. 2015 (not yet ingested) in K2 mission data. Transit depth varies stochastically between 0% and ~1.3% in optical light — the defining signature of the [[disintegrating-planet]] class — as episodic dust and gas clouds transit the star. JWST MIRI/LRS mid-infrared transit observations have detected iron-rich silicate dust and a tentative NO gas feature in the highest-activity epoch ([[2025_Tusay_K2-22b]]).

Note: planet mass and radius are uncertain due to the disintegrating nature; values above are nominal estimates for the surviving body; the escaping dust/gas contributes the observed transit depth.

# GJ 1132 b

A rocky super-Earth (1.66 M⊕, 1.30 R⊕; Teq ≈ 529 K) on a 1.63-day orbit around the nearby M4.5 dwarf [[GJ1132]] (Berta-Thompson et al. 2015; not yet ingested). One of the most heavily observed rocky exoplanets in the wiki — five HST transits and six JWST transits/eclipses across four programs — and a textbook example of how multi-epoch JWST data can retire a tentative atmospheric inference. Current working conclusion: **bare rock**.

## Atmospheric composition

No species detected; bare-rock interpretation across all current data.

- **Ruled out:**
  - **H₂-dominated atmospheres** (NIRSpec, [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] → reinforced by [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]).
  - **HCN-rich atmospheres** as proposed by Swain et al. 2021 (not yet ingested) — directly contradicted by the HST [[2021_Mugnai_GJ1132b]] re-reduction.
  - **Pure CH₄** at >10σ (coadded four-visit NIRSpec, [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]); retires the Visit-1 ~1% CH₄ inference of [[2023_May_GJ1132b]].
  - **Pure CO₂** at >3σ (transmission); joint with MIRI/LRS emission, CO₂ <10 mbar ([[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]] + [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]).
  - **Earth-like and Venus-like atmospheres** (MIRI/LRS, [[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]]).
- **No remaining signal:** The flat-line model is preferred over all atmospheric scenarios for the four-visit coadded spectrum.

## Observations to date

**Pre-JWST HST context.** Five HST WFC3 G141 transits (1.125–1.650 μm, R ≈ 130) observed in 2017 under HST 14758. [[2021_Mugnai_GJ1132b]] reduced the data within the ARES survey and found a flat spectrum with no molecular absorption, directly contradicting Swain et al. 2021 (not yet ingested), which had claimed HCN and CH₄ features from the same dataset. The ARES result was independently re-reduced and aligns with all subsequent JWST findings.

**JWST [[JWST-GO-1981-CHAMPs]] (NIRSpec G395H).** Two 2023 visits ([[2023_May_GJ1132b]]) produced discrepant spectra — Visit 1 tentatively consistent with an H₂O + ~1% CH₄ atmosphere *or* unocculted starspots, Visit 2 featureless. The paper itself flagged the discrepancy as a cautionary tale.

**JWST [[JWST-GTO-1274]] (MIRI/LRS).** Single secondary eclipse ([[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]]); T_day = 709 ± 31 K, R = 0.95 ± 0.04 — bare-rock consistent. Independently rules out Earth-like and Venus-like atmospheres.

**JWST follow-up (NIRSpec G395M).** Two 2024 G395M visits coadded with the earlier G395H transits ([[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]); four-visit spectrum is flat. Leave-one-out analysis plus flux-calibrated out-of-transit stellar modeling reattributes the Visit-1 deviation to evolving stellar heterogeneity (a large cool spot that rotated out of view before Visit 2). First wiki demonstration of combining G395H + G395M to fill the NRS1/NRS2 gap.

## Open questions

- **Stellar CO bands as a residual systematic.** [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] speculates that spatially variable stellar molecular bands could mimic planetary CO/CO₂ features in NIRSpec G395H/G395M; not yet definitively ruled out as a source of any future tentative detection on M-dwarf planets.
- **Why does GJ 1132 b sit cleanly on the airless side of the [[cosmic-shoreline]]** while comparable-irradiation [[TOI-561b]] and [[HD3167b]] do not? The combination of stellar XUV history, planetary mass, and bulk composition driving the difference is not yet quantified.

## Tensions

- **Visit 1 vs Visit 2 atmospheric inference (resolved).** [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] left open whether Visit 1's H₂O + ~1% CH₄ feature was real atmosphere or unocculted starspots. [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] resolves: the four-visit coadded spectrum is flat; Visit 1 is an outlier driven by transient stellar heterogeneity.
- **Swain et al. 2021 HCN/H₂ vs ARES flat (resolved).** Two independent reductions of the same HST WFC3 dataset reached opposite conclusions. [[2021_Mugnai_GJ1132b]] and the four-visit JWST result confirm the flat ARES result.

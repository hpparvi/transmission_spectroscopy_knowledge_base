# GJ 1132 b

A rocky super-Earth (1.66 M⊕, 1.30 R⊕; Teq ≈ 529 K) on a 1.63-day orbit around the nearby M4.5 dwarf [[GJ1132]], discovered by Berta-Thompson et al. 2015 and a long-standing priority target for atmospheric characterization because of its favorable planet–star radius ratio.

## Observations to date
The JWST/CHAMPs program ([[JWST-GO-1981-CHAMPs]]) observed GJ 1132 b in four transits. The two 2023 NIRSpec G395H visits ([[2023_May_GJ1132b]]) produced discrepant spectra — Visit 1 tentatively consistent with an H₂O + ~1% CH₄ atmosphere *or* unocculted starspots, Visit 2 featureless. Two follow-up NIRSpec G395M visits in 2024 ([[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]) were coadded with the earlier data; the four-visit spectrum is best fit by a flat line, and a leave-one-out analysis together with flux-calibrated out-of-transit stellar modeling reattribute the Visit-1 deviation to evolving stellar heterogeneity (a large cool spot that rotated out of view before Visit 2). Combined with MIRI/LRS emission constraints ([[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]]), which measured T_day = 709 ± 31 K (R = 0.95 ± 0.04) and found a featureless 5–12 μm spectrum consistent with an airless body, the current working conclusion is that **GJ 1132 b is a bare rock**.

# HD 80606 b

A highly eccentric hot Jupiter (e = 0.93183, P = 111.44 d, M = 4.16 M_J, R = 1.03 R_J) discovered by Naef et al. 2001 (not yet ingested) on a 111-day orbit around the bright (J = 7.7, V ≈ 9) Sun-like star [[HD80606]]. Apoastron-to-periapse distance varies from 0.88 → 0.03 au — the star-planet flux scales by a factor of ~860, driving an irradiation-temperature swing from ~300 K → ~1500 K over the 17-hour periapse passage. One of the most extreme eccentricity cases known. The first JWST atmospheric characterization is [[2025_Sikora_HD80606b]] (NIRSpec G395H, 20.9-hour partial phase curve centered on the eclipse).

## Atmospheric composition

- **Detected** ([[2025_Sikora_HD80606b]]):
  - [[CH4]] — 4.1–10.7σ in NRS1 post-periapse (dependent on whether NRS1 detector offset is included); strongest at 3.0–3.5 μm.
  - [[H2O]] — 4.2–5.5σ in NRS2 post-periapse.
  - [[CO2]] — 3.7–4.4σ in NRS2 post-periapse.
- **Chemical equilibrium retrievals preferred over free-chemistry** (Bayes factors at all post-periapse phases); CO present in equilibrium retrievals.
- **No strong temperature inversion** detected at the IR photosphere despite GCM predictions (Tsai et al. 2023b; not ingested) of a transient ~400 K inversion at periapse.

## Observations to date

- **JWST NIRSpec G395H** ([[JWST-GO-2488]] — PI: Sikora; [[2025_Sikora_HD80606b]]) — 20.9-hour partial phase curve on 2022 November 1, capturing the secondary eclipse and the periapse passage. T_bright = 892 K (pre-eclipse) → 1313 K (peak post-periapse) → 1077 K (5.4 hr post-periapse).
- **Spitzer IRAC 4.5 μm** (de Wit et al. 2016; not yet ingested) — 80-hour partial phase curve at periapse; 738 ± 52 ppm NRS2-comparable amplitude.
- **Spitzer IRAC 8 μm** (G. Laughlin et al. 2009; not yet ingested) — 30-hour first photometric eclipse detection (pre-JWST landmark).
- **HST/STIS / TESS / RVs** — orbit characterization (K. A. Pearson 2022 ephemeris; not yet ingested).

## Open questions

- **Rotation period.** de Wit et al. 2016 (not ingested) energy-conserving model gives P_rot ≈ 93 hr; pseudo-synchronous prediction is ~40 hr. Untested.
- **Tsai 2023b GCM photochemistry.** This paper's chemical-equilibrium preference rejects the bulk of the disequilibrium predictions; quantitative comparisons of CO/CH₄ ratio remain to be made.
- **Cloud composition.** Lewis et al. 2017 (not ingested) predict MgSiO₃, MnS, Na₂S clouds during periapse — not yet directly observed.
- **Repeat periapse passage observations.** Single-visit data leaves variability questions open.

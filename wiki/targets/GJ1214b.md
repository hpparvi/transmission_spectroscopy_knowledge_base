# GJ 1214 b

The **archetype thick-aerosol sub-Neptune** (Charbonneau et al. 2009; not ingested), discovered by the MEarth project. R_p = 2.73 R_⊕, M_p = 8.17 M_⊕ (Cloutier et al. 2021; not ingested), ρ = 2.2 g cm⁻³, on a 1.58-day orbit around the nearby M4.5 dwarf [[GJ1214]] (14.6 pc; T_eff = 3250 K). Famously **featureless** in HST/WFC3 (Kreidberg & Bean 2014; not ingested), attributed to extremely thick high-altitude aerosols. The first JWST observation (Kempton et al. 2023, MIRI/LRS thermal phase curve; not ingested) revealed a large amplitude of the phase curve indicating high atmospheric metallicity. The first JWST/NIRSpec G395H transmission (Schlawin et al. 2024a, not ingested) reported tentative CO₂ at 2.4σ and CH₄ at 2.0σ. **[[2025_Ohno_GJ1214b]]** combines all three (HST/WFC3 + NIRSpec/G395H + MIRI/LRS) and concludes the panchromatic spectrum requires **[M/H] ≈ 3.5–3.8 (~3000–6000× solar) — a metal-dominated atmosphere where H₂ is no longer the main constituent.**

# HAT-P-26 b

A low-density warm Neptune-mass exoplanet (M = 18.6 M⊕ ≈ 0.06 M_J, R = 6.33 R⊕ ≈ 0.57 R_J, T_eq ≈ 1000 K) discovered by Hartman et al. 2011 (not yet ingested) on a 4.234-day orbit around the K-dwarf [[HAT-P-26]]. Its low gravity and warm equilibrium temperature have made it one of the most studied low-mass exoplanets since launch — first H₂O detection in HST/WFC3 (Wakeford et al. 2017; not yet ingested), atmospheric metallicity refined by MacDonald & Madhusudhan 2019 (not yet ingested), and the first JWST NIRSpec G395H transmission spectrum reported in [[2025_Gressier_HATP26b]].

## Atmospheric composition

- **Detected:** [[H2O]] (lnB = 4.06, ~3σ), [[CO2]] (lnB = 85.64, ≫5σ), [[SO2]] (lnB = 13.46, ≥5σ).
- **Tentative:** [[H2S]] (~2σ, posterior peak log VMR ≈ −3.5).
- **Ruled out (3σ upper limits):** [[CO]] (log X < −5.33), NH₃, C₂H₂, C₂H₄, HCN.
- **Atmospheric metallicity:** ~10× solar (3–25× free retrieval); subsolar C/O ≈ 0.14.

## Observations to date

- **JWST NIRSpec G395H** ([[JWST-TST-GTO-1312]], [[2025_Gressier_HATP26b]]) — single 7 hr 56 min transit on 2023 June 15; SO₂ detection; first JWST result on the target. Forthcoming companion analyses: NIRISS/SOSS (R. J. MacDonald & DREAMS 2025, in prep) and the joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI panchromatic analysis (L. Alderson & DREAMS 2025, in prep).
- **HST WFC3 / STIS / Spitzer** (Wakeford et al. 2017, Stevenson et al. 2016, MacDonald & Madhusudhan 2019, Tsiaras et al. 2018; not yet ingested) — first H₂O detection; metallicity 4.8⁺²¹.⁵₋₄.₀ × solar (HST) refined to 18.1⁺²⁵.⁹₋₁₁.₃ × solar (re-analysis).

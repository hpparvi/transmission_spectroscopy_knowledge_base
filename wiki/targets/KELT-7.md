# KELT-7

F2 dwarf (T_eff = 6768 ± 7 K, R = 1.768 R☉, M = 1.76 ± 0.25 M☉, [Fe/H] = +0.139, age 1.3 ± 0.2 Gyr; Bieryla et al. 2015, not yet ingested) hosting the aligned hot Jupiter [[KELT-7b]]. Above the Kraft break (T_eff > 6100 K), so retains its primordial spin — making the planet's measured aligned obliquity (λ = −10.55° ± 0.27°) physically meaningful for formation-history inference: it is unlikely to have been damped post-formation. Stellar contamination is not a primary concern at this T_eff.

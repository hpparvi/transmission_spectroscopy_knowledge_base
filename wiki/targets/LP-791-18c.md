# LP 791-18 c

Temperate sub-Neptune (Rp = 2.438 R⊕, Mp = 7.1 M⊕, ρ = 2.69 g cm⁻³, T_eq ≈ 355 K at A_B = 0.3) on a transiting orbit around the M6 dwarf [[LP-791-18]]. JWST NIRSpec PRISM transmission ([[2025_Roy_LP791-18c]], [[JWST-GTO-1201]] NEAT) reveals a strong Rayleigh-scattering haze (5.4σ) overlaid with [[CH4]] absorption (4.5σ) and **no detectable [[CO2]]** (CH₄/CO₂ > 12 at 2σ). The haze enhancement is 2–3 orders of magnitude greater than on the comparable temperate sub-Neptunes [[K2-18b]] and [[TOI-270d]] — flagged as evidence for intrinsic atmospheric diversity in the temperate sub-Neptune class.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Detected:** [[CH4]] at 4.5σ; log X(CH₄) > −2.89 (2σ).
- **Strong haze Rayleigh slope** at 5.4σ (log c_Haze = 5.16); likely CH₄-derived tholins (Titan-like) or sulfur hazes.
- **Ruled out:** [[CO2]] (log X < −1.97 at 2σ), and as haze precursors: HCN, C₂H₂, C₂H₄, C₂H₆, SO₂, SO.
- **Metallicity:** 250–400× solar (chemically-consistent retrieval), consistent with chemical equilibrium.
- **Bulk composition** richer in H₂ than H₂O — hints at formation **inside** the water ice line.

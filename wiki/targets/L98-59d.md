# L 98-59 d

The outer of three transiting rocky super-Earths around the M3V dwarf [[L98-59]]. Radius 1.58 R⊕, mass 1.94 M⊕, Teq ≈ 416 K on a 7.45-day orbit. Classified as a "water world" by Luque & Palle 2022 (not yet ingested), consistent with a rock+ice bulk composition at roughly equal mass fractions. Potentially tidally heated via mean-motion resonance with L 98-59 b, c (2:4:7 period ratios; Seligman et al. 2024, not yet ingested).

## Observations to date
Two independent JWST NIRSpec/G395H analyses of a single transit from [[JWST-GTO-1224]] ([[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] and [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]]; "Paper II" and "Paper I" respectively) both find a transmission spectrum preferring an out-of-equilibrium atmosphere containing [[H2S]] and [[SO2]]. Quantitative significance spans 2.2σ–5.6σ depending on reduction + retrieval code. A stellar-contamination-only scenario cannot reproduce the observed ~3.3 μm and ~4.4 μm features. The sulfur species, if confirmed, hint at **active volcanism** on this tidally heated planet. Cycle 2 follow-up (second G395H + NIRISS SOSS) is planned.

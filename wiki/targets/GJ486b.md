# GJ 486 b (= Gl 486 b)

A hot rocky planet (Rp=1.289 R⊕, Mp=2.77 M⊕, Teq≈696 K) on a 1.47-day orbit around the M3.5 dwarf [[GJ486]]. Third-closest known transiting exoplanet (~8 pc). Originally a JWST CHAMPs target and since accumulated both transmission and emission observations.

## Observations to date
NIRSpec/G395H transmission spectroscopy by [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] (JWST CHAMPs, two transits) found the spectrum deviates from flat at 2.2σ–3.3σ and presented **two equally-Bayesian scenarios**: (a) a water-rich atmosphere with log X(H₂O) > 10%, or (b) unocculted starspot contamination from ~10% coverage of 2700 K spots on the 3343 K photosphere. The paper explicitly could not distinguish the two with G395H alone and flagged the already-scheduled MIRI/LRS follow-up as the tie-breaker. The subsequent MIRI/LRS emission campaign ([[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]]; JWST GO 1743) found dayside T = 865 K and temperature ratio R = 0.97 ± 0.01, consistent with an airless body and **inconsistent with the water-rich atmosphere interpretation** — resolving Moran's ambiguity in favor of the stellar-contamination scenario. On the [[cosmic-shoreline]] diagram GJ 486 b sits clearly on the **airless-body side**.

## Tensions
- **Water-rich atmosphere scenario ([[2023_Moran_GJ486b]], NIRSpec transmission) vs. airless body conclusion ([[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]], MIRI/LRS emission):** Moran 2023 presented the water-rich and stellar-contamination scenarios as equally Bayesian. Weiner Mansfield 2024's emission constraint (R = 0.97) is inconsistent with a water-rich atmosphere, so the transmission features must be attributed to stellar contamination. **Status: resolved in favor of stellar contamination.** The first case in the wiki where an emission observation selects between alternatives that a transmission paper had explicitly left open.

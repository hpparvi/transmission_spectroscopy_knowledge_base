# Kepler-51

500 Myr Sun-like G dwarf (M = 0.96 M☉, R = 0.87 R☉; Masuda et al. 2024, not yet ingested) hosting three transiting [[super-puff]] planets (Kepler-51 b, c, [[Kepler-51d]]) plus one non-transiting low-mass planet. Active host: spot-crossing events visible in JWST and Kepler transits. [[2025_LibbyRoberts_Kepler51d]] uses an in-transit spot crossing to extract a wavelength-dependent contrast, finding a hot-spot ΔT ∼ 300 K above the photosphere — > 1000 K hotter than solar sunspots, possibly a faculae/penumbra/hot+cold spot complex.

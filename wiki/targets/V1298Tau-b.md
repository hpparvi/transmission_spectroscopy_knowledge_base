# V1298 Tau b

Young (10–30 Myr) low-density sub-Neptune progenitor (M_p = 12 ± 1 M⊕, R_p = 9.85 R⊕, P = 24.14 d, T_eq ≈ 670 K) on a 24-day orbit around the weak-lined T Tauri star [[V1298Tau]]. The innermost of three transiting planets (with c, d) in a near 2:3:6 mean-motion resonance plus an outer planet candidate. The system is well-aligned (Gaidos et al. 2022; not ingested) and ~20 Myr old as a member of Group 29 in Taurus-Auriga. Stellar magnetic activity makes RV mass measurement difficult — the original RV mass (0.64 M_J ≈ 200 M⊕; Suárez Mascareño et al. 2021; not ingested) is 40σ inconsistent with the atmospheric-scale-height mass derived in [[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]]. The planet is in a transient mass-loss phase — could lose 50% of its primordial envelope by ~100 Myr.

## Atmospheric composition

From the panchromatic JWST/NIRSpec G395H + HST/WFC3 G141 transmission spectrum analyzed in [[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]] (clear, haze-free, scale height ~1500 km):

- [[CO2]] — detected at 35σ (~1000 ppm 4.3 μm feature) ([[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]])
- [[H2O]] — detected at 30σ (~600 ppm 2.7 μm feature) ([[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]])
- [[CO]] — detected at 10σ ([[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]])
- [[CH4]] — detected at 6σ; abundance **7σ lower than equilibrium expectation** — implies high T_int ≈ 500 K + K_zz ~ 10⁷–10⁸ cm² s⁻¹ ([[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]])
- [[SO2]] — detected at 4σ ([[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]])

Tentative OCS at 3.5σ (4.85–5 μm window; reduces to ~2σ in broader binning); first proposed onset of OCS at ~500 K (S. Mukherjee et al. 2025; not ingested).

# TOI-1130 b

Warm mini-Neptune (3.66 ± 0.04 R⊕, 19.8 ± 0.03 M⊕, T_eq ≈ 825 K, P = 4.07 d) — the inner planet of the unique 2:1 mean-motion-resonance system [[TOI-1130]], whose outer companion is the hot Jupiter [[TOI-1130c]]. The orbital architecture (mini-Neptune + outer hot Jupiter in 2:1 MMR) is essentially unique and **strongly constrains the formation history**: disc-driven migration, not high-eccentricity migration, must have brought both planets to their present orbits (Beaugé 2006 not ingested), and the outer hot Jupiter pebble-filters the inner disc, blocking volatile-rich material from beyond the snow line if the mini-Neptune formed in situ. The first JWST atmospheric characterization ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]]) reveals a **high mean molecular weight atmosphere** (μ = 5.5⁺¹·³₋₀·₈ amu, [M/H] ≈ 60× solar) with H₂O, CO₂, SO₂, and tentative CH₄ detected — strongly favoring **ex-situ formation beyond the water ice line + subsequent disc migration** for the inner mini-Neptune. TOI-1130 b sits at the **edge of the radius cliff** (orbital period < 100 d × radius > 3 R⊕ is sparsely populated; Dattilo 2023 not ingested; Fulton 2017 not ingested); a high-MMW atmosphere with low atmospheric escape rate (He I 1083 nm non-detection; mass loss < 10¹¹ g s⁻¹) is consistent with maintaining its position on the radius-period diagram. Joins [[TOI-270d]] (μ ≈ 5.5) and [[GJ3470b]] (μ ≈ 5.7) as a third "**miscible-envelope mini-Neptune**" — a class that may have heterogeneous formation history.

## Atmospheric composition
- [[H2O]] — detected (7.5σ; log mass fraction ≈ −0.6) ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]])
- [[CO2]] — detected (3.3σ; log mass fraction ≈ −1.2) ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]])
- [[SO2]] — detected (3.6σ; log mass fraction ≈ −3.0); photochemical product; independently confirms a high-metallicity atmosphere ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]]; cf. [[2025_Crossfield_SO2shoreline]])
- [[CH4]] — tentative (~2σ) ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]])
- [[He]] — 1083 nm escape signature **not detected**; mass-loss rate < 10¹¹ g s⁻¹ ([[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]])
- Mean molecular weight μ = 5.5⁺¹·³₋₀·₈ amu (equilibrium-chemistry retrieval); μ > 3.5 amu lower limit from free chemistry
- Atmospheric metallicity log Z/Z_⊙ = 1.8⁺⁰·⁴₋⁰·₃ (60⁺₁₅₀₋₃₀ × solar)
- C/O < 0.75 (3σ); median ~0.2 (consistent with low-C/O accretion of water-ice-line-rich planetesimals)
- Optical scattering slope (TESS + CHEOPS + NIRISS Order 1) modelled as Rayleigh + sulfur boost; Titan-tholin, soot, ice-tholin, and phosphorus hazes excluded at > 3–5σ

## Observations to date
JWST observations under [[JWST-GO-3385]] (PI: Barat) on 2024 Aug 20 (NIRSpec G395H) and 2024 Aug 24 (NIRISS SOSS). 12 [[CHEOPS]] transits + 20 [[TESS]] sectors + ground-based LCO + ASTEP + MINERVA refined the transit-timing solution to enable JWST scheduling against the system's ~2-hour TTV. The CHEOPS visit overlapping the NIRISS JWST transit (CH_PR149003, PI: Brandeker) provides simultaneous optical baseline. First (and to date only) JWST atmospheric characterization in [[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]].

## Open questions
- **Origin of volatiles**: ex-situ formation beyond the snow line + disc migration is the leading interpretation. Interior–atmosphere magma-ocean outgassing is a possible alternative source (Werlen 2025a/b not ingested; Lichtenberg 2025 not ingested) but TOI-1130 b's ~25 % envelope-mass-fraction puts it at the transition where magma solidifies, weakening this channel (Breza 2025; Calder 2025 — both not ingested).
- **Is CH₄ real?** Detected at 2σ tentative; would discriminate between strictly equilibrium chemistry (C/O = 0.3 produces some CH₄) and aggressively photochemistry-suppressed atmospheres. Higher-S/N follow-up needed.
- **TOI-1130 c composition**: does the outer hot Jupiter share TOI-1130 b's volatile enrichment, consistent with co-migration, or is it pure-H/He?
- **Position relative to the Brande 2024 A_H^H₂O quadratic trend** (not ingested): TOI-1130 b is below the quadratic, consistent with the silicate-condensation theory of Gao 2020 (not ingested).

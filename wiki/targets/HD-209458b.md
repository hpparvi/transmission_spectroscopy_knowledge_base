# HD 209458 b

The first transiting exoplanet (Charbonneau et al. 2000; Henry et al. 2000; not yet ingested) and the historical archetype for [[transmission-spectroscopy]] of inflated hot Jupiters: M_p ≈ 0.69 M_J, R_p ≈ 1.55 R_J, P ≈ 3.52 d, around the G0V star [[HD-209458]]. The 2000 / 2001 foundational theory papers ([[2000_Seager_TheoryTransmission]], [[2001_Brown_TransmissionDiagnostics]]) computed predicted spectra specifically for this target, and the prediction of [[Na]] absorption was confirmed by Charbonneau, Brown, Latham & Mayor 2002 (HST/STIS; not yet ingested) — **the first detection of an exoplanet atmosphere**. The wiki has no JWST-era observation of HD 209458 b ingested yet.

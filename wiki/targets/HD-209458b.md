# HD 209458 b

The first transiting exoplanet (Charbonneau et al. 2000; Henry et al. 2000; not yet ingested) and the historical archetype for [[transmission-spectroscopy]] of inflated hot Jupiters: M_p ≈ 0.69 M_J, R_p ≈ 1.39 R_J, P ≈ 3.52 d, around the G0V star [[HD-209458]]. The 2000/2001 foundational theory papers ([[2000_Seager_TheoryTransmission]], [[2001_Brown_TransmissionDiagnostics]]) computed predicted spectra specifically for this target; the [[Na]] D doublet prediction was confirmed by Charbonneau et al. 2002 (HST/STIS; not yet ingested) — **the first detection of an exoplanet atmosphere**.

## Atmospheric composition

From the panchromatic STIS+WFC3+NIRCam free-chemistry retrieval in [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] (consistent solar metallicity 0.8–2.1× and C/O ≈ 0.54):

- [[H2O]] — detected at 12.6σ (log VMR = −4.53⁺⁰·³²⁻⁰·²⁹) ([[2025_Verma_HD209458b]])
- [[CO2]] — detected at 7.8σ (log VMR = −7.52⁺⁰·³⁰⁻⁰·²⁶) ([[2025_Verma_HD209458b]])
- [[Na]] — detected at 6.9σ (log VMR = −5.67⁺⁰·⁵¹⁻⁰·⁴⁵) ([[2025_Verma_HD209458b]]); first robust JWST-era confirmation of the historic Charbonneau 2002 detection
- [[K]] — detected at 4.9σ (log VMR = −6.93⁺⁰·⁴⁶⁻⁰·⁴⁷) ([[2025_Verma_HD209458b]])

3σ upper limits on log VMR for **CO** (−5.35), **NH₃** (−5.79), **C₂H₂** (−6.17), **CH₄** (−7.30), **H₂S** (−5.69), **HCN** (−6.97); rejects MacDonald & Madhusudhan 2017's NH₃ tentative claim and Giacobbe et al. 2021's ground-based CH₄/HCN/NH₃/C₂H₂/CO claims.

## Observations to date

The wiki has theoretical predictions ([[2000_Seager_TheoryTransmission]], [[2001_Brown_TransmissionDiagnostics]]) and now its first JWST-era ingestion via [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — a methodology study using the SANSAR framework on a re-analysis of Xue et al. 2024 (not ingested) NIRCam F322W2 + F444W transmission combined with HST/STIS (Sing et al. 2016; not ingested) and HST/WFC3 (Deming et al. 2013; not ingested). The headline finding for HD 209458 b is that **NIRCam-only retrievals overestimate H₂O and CO₂ abundances by ~3 dex** relative to combined STIS+WFC3+NIRCam — the optical baseline anchors temperature and cloud properties. With the full coverage, HD 209458 b's atmosphere is consistent with solar metallicity and stellar C/O.

## Open questions

- Does the metallicity = solar / C/O = stellar result hold under disequilibrium chemistry treatment?
- 1D retrievals at HD 209458 b's 1450 K T_eq may be biased by limb asymmetry; [[2025_Fu_limb-asymmetry]] showed analogous targets exhibit > 5σ morning–evening contrasts.

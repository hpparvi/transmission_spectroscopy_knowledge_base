# TOI-776 c

A warm sub-Neptune (Rp = 2.02 ± 0.14 R⊕, Mp = 5.3 ± 1.8 M⊕, Teq ≈ 420 K, P = 15.67 d) orbiting the bright M1V dwarf [[TOI-776]] (LP 961-53), discovered by Luque et al. 2021 (not yet ingested) and characterised with updated masses by Fridlund et al. 2024 (not yet ingested). Its bulk density (ρ ≈ 4.4 g cm⁻³) is consistent with a ~50% water-mass-fraction composition, placing it among a small group of potential "water worlds" alongside TOI-270 d and GJ 9827 d. The first JWST transmission spectrum ([[2025_Teske_TOI776c]]; NIRSpec G395H, 3–5 μm) is featureless; metallicities ≲ 180–240× solar are ruled out at 3σ, though the inferred MMW limit varies strongly with the assumed atmospheric model (2.8–7.7 g mol⁻¹).

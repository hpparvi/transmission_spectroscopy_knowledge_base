# WASP-17 b

Inflated, low-density hot Jupiter (Mp ≈ 0.4 MJ, Rp ≈ 1.93 RJ, Teq ≈ 1700 K) on a retrograde 3.74-day orbit around the F6 dwarf [[WASP-17]]. The DREAMS program ([[JWST-TST-GTO-1353]]) has produced a multi-instrument suite covering the dayside (NIRISS SOSS — [[2025_Gressier_WASP17b]]) and nightside (NIRSpec G395H — [[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]]). Earlier MIRI/LRS work (Grant et al. 2023, Valentine et al. 2024; both not yet ingested) detected a SiO₂(s) cloud feature at 8.6 μm and mapped a hotspot offset of 18.7° E.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Detected:**
  - [[H2O]] — supersolar dayside abundance at 6.4σ ([[2025_Gressier_WASP17b]]).
- **Tentative:**
  - [[FeH]] — ~3σ on dayside under positive-depth priors ([[2025_Gressier_WASP17b]]).
  - [[SO2]] — 2.3σ on nightside; requires day-to-night transport at ~10× solar metallicity ([[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]]).
  - [[CH4]] — 1.5σ on nightside ([[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]]).
  - [[CO2]] — 0.9σ on nightside; underabundance relative to dayside is unexplained ([[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]]).
- **Bond albedo:** A_B = 0.42 +0.06/−0.10; recirculation f = 0.54 (inefficient redistribution; [[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]]).

## Limb asymmetry

[[2025_Fu_limb-asymmetry]] reports δA_H = 1.50 ± 0.25 H on the GTO 1353 NIRISS/SOSS transit — the third planet (after [[WASP-94Ab]] and [[WASP-39b]]) with > 5σ morning–evening water-band contrast. At T_eq = 1740 K, MgSiO₃ alone is sufficient to drive the divergence (no Na₂S/MnS needed). The Louie et al. 2025 free retrieval recovers a limb-averaged temperature of 1272 K — far below T_eq = 1755 K — likely a signature of unrecognized aerosol heterogeneity.

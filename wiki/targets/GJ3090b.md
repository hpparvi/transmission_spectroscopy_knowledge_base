# GJ 3090 b

Warm sub-Neptune (R = 2.13 R⊕, M = 3.34 M⊕, ρ = 1.89 g/cm³, T_eq ≈ 693 K) on a 2.85-day orbit around the M2V dwarf [[GJ3090]] (Almenara et al. 2022; not yet ingested). At the outer edge of the radius valley around mid-M dwarfs; the warmest sub-Neptune with a published JWST atmospheric spectrum (∼100 K warmer than GJ 9827 d). The first JWST sub-Neptune with a confident metastable [[He]] I detection (5.5σ at 1083 nm) — but the small amplitude empirically supports a high-metallicity, low-mass-loss atmosphere ([[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]]).

## Atmospheric composition

- [[He]] — detected (5.5σ; metastable 1083 nm; small amplitude → metal-enriched) ([[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]])
- [[CH4]] — confidently absent at ∼3.4 μm; drives the high-metallicity inference ([[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]])
- [[CO2]], [[SO2]] — tentative; sub-solar C/O preferred ([[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]])

## Open questions

- Metallicity precision: chemically consistent retrievals find >100× solar at 3σ for clouds at <μbar; >700× solar at 2σ irrespective of clouds; best-fit grid >1000× solar (MMW ∼27 amu). Free retrievals cannot constrain individual species.
- TLS-vs-atmosphere disentanglement: NIRISS/SOSS spectra dominated by stellar contamination — atmospheric inferences come solely from NIRSpec/G395H.
- Sub-solar C/O origin: ice-rich formation vs atmospheric evolution under chemical exchange with the interior and/or atmospheric mass loss?

# GJ 357 b

A super-Earth (Rp = 1.217 R⊕, Mp = 1.84 M⊕, Teq ≈ 525 K, P = 3.93 d) orbiting the exceptionally inactive M2.5V dwarf [[GJ357]] (Jenkins et al. 2019, not yet ingested; parameters from Luque et al. 2019, not yet ingested). Bulk composition is consistent with an Earth-like interior. The first JWST observation ([[2025_Taylor_GJ357b]]; NIRISS SOSS) yields a featureless transmission spectrum; atmospheres ≤100× solar metallicity with cloud-top pressure > 0.01 bar are ruled out at 3σ. The host star's XUV history makes volcanic revival of a CO₂-dominated secondary atmosphere physically plausible — distinguishable from a bare-rock scenario with 3–4 NIRSpec/G395H transits targeting the 4.3 μm CO₂ feature.

## Observations to date
GJ 357 b has been observed in two JWST transmission spectroscopy programs covering complementary wavelength ranges. The NEAT GTO program ([[2025_Taylor_GJ357b]], NIRISS SOSS, 0.85–2.8 μm) found a featureless spectrum and ruled out ≤100× solar metallicity atmospheres with cloud-top pressure > 0.01 bar at 3σ. The COMPASS survey ([[2025_Redai_GJ357b]], NIRSpec G395H, 2.87–5.14 μm) extended coverage into the 3–5 μm region, also yielding a featureless spectrum and excluding mean molecular weight < 8 g/mol and metallicity < 300–500× solar. Together, the two datasets cover 0.85–5.14 μm with no features detected, effectively ruling out all H/He-dominated primordial atmospheres and most moderately metal-enriched scenarios, while leaving CO₂-dominated high-metallicity secondary atmospheres unexcluded pending a MIRI/LRS emission measurement.

# K2-18 b

Habitable-zone sub-Neptune (R = 2.610 R⊕, M = 8.63 M⊕, T_eq ≈ 255 K) on a 32.9-day orbit around the M3 dwarf [[K2-18]] (Foreman-Mackey et al. 2015, Montet et al. 2015; not ingested). The most contested sub-Neptune in the wiki: a Hycean candidate (Madhusudhan et al. 2021, 2023; not ingested) versus a magma-ocean / volatile-rich primordial atmosphere (Wogan et al. 2024, Shorttle et al. 2024, Schmidt et al. 2025; none ingested) — with multiple JWST analyses giving subtly different chemical compositions on the same data. As of [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] the consensus is: **CH₄ robustly detected**; **CO₂ marginal at 2–3σ** depending on retrieval setup; **DMS retracted** under comprehensive retrievals (Bayes factor < 1); high C/O ratio (≈ 10–13×) supports a primordial H₂-He atmosphere with elevated O+C from carbon-soot-line accretion (Inside-Out Planet Formation).

## Atmospheric composition

- **Robust detection:**
  - [[CH4]] — 4.16σ–4.62σ across all configurations ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]]); log VMR ≈ −1.7 to −1.0; reproduces Madhusudhan et al. 2023 (not ingested) and Schmidt et al. 2025 (not ingested) detections.
- **Marginal / configuration-dependent:**
  - [[CO2]] — typically ~2σ in comprehensive retrievals (Bayes factor ~1); 2.0σ in fiducial; 3.7σ in Hu et al. 2025 (not ingested) joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI retrieval. log VMR ≈ −3.94⁺¹·⁶⁸₋₄·²⁰; long tail to lower values.
- **Retracted:**
  - [[DMS]] — Madhusudhan 2023, 2025 (not ingested) reported tentative detection; [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] and Schmidt et al. 2025 (not ingested) find Bayes factor < 1 in comprehensive retrievals; current consensus is **non-detection**.
- **Ruled out / upper limits (95%):** H₂O < 10⁻²·²⁹, CO < 10⁻²·⁸⁰, NH₃ < 10⁻⁴·⁴¹, HCN < 10⁻²·⁵⁴, H₂S < 10⁻²·⁸⁵, SO₂ < 10⁻³·²⁰, OCS < 10⁻⁴·⁶¹, CH₃Cl < 10⁻³·⁷², N₂O < 10⁻³·¹⁴, CS₂ < 10⁻⁵·³².
- **MMW** = 2.5–10 amu depending on retrieval complexity; comprehensive 13-species retrieval prefers high-MMW (~4.3 amu).
- **C/O** = 10.3–13.4 (super-stellar by ~10–20×); both C and O abundances elevated absolutely.

## Observations to date

**JWST [[JWST-GO-2722]] (PI: Madhusudhan):**
- **NIRISS SOSS** transit on 2023 January 20–21 (~5.3 hr; first-order 0.85–2.83 μm; spot-crossing event during ingress).
- **NIRSpec G395H** transit on 2023 June 1 (~4.9 hr; 2.8–5.2 μm with detector gap).

**JWST follow-up programs (papers not yet ingested):**
- MIRI/LRS observations (Madhusudhan et al. 2025, Hu et al. 2025) — extending coverage to 12 μm.

**Aerosol-inclusive reanalysis** (using literature-reduced spectra):
- [[2025_Jaziri_K2-18b]] joint analysis of Madhusudhan 2023 NIRISS+NIRSpec spectrum + Luque 2025 (not ingested) Eureka! MIRI/LRS reduction. **Photochemical CH₄-derived tholins (exo1 from PAMPRE)** — adding both 7 μm C–H bending absorption and shorter-wavelength scattering — improve the combined fit (Bayes factor 5.53), reduce the inferred CH₄ abundance by ~2 dex (log VMR -1.2 → -3.5), and lower metallicity from 20× → 0.3× solar; reinforces the metallicity-aerosol-composition degeneracy.

**Pre-JWST HST/WFC3** (Benneke et al. 2019, Tsiaras et al. 2019; not ingested) — first H₂O detection / methane reinterpretation.

## Open questions

- **Is CO₂ real?** Marginal at 2σ in [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] but 3.7σ in Hu et al. 2025 (joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI; not ingested). Multi-visit + multi-instrument synthesis needed.
- **Does the DMS retraction generalize?** Three independent reanalyses ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]], Schmidt 2025 not ingested; partly Welbanks 2025 not ingested) agree DMS is not statistically supported. Madhusudhan 2025 (MIRI; not ingested) maintains the detection at 6–12 μm. The 6–12 μm ν₂ band is the next test.
- **Hycean vs primordial-with-elevated-O-C.** [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] favors the latter via IOPF; Madhusudhan 2023, 2025 favor the former. The discriminator is the inferred C/O and absolute C/H abundance — but error bars span both regimes.
- **Comparator diversity.** [[K2-18b]] (CH₄ + tentative CO₂, no DMS), [[TOI-270d]] (CH₄ + CO₂ + tentative CS₂, no SO₂), [[LP-791-18c]] (CH₄ + haze, no CO₂) — three temperate sub-Neptunes with three distinct chemistries.

## Tensions

- **DMS detection (Madhusudhan 2023, 2025) vs non-detection ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]], Schmidt et al. 2025).** Same NIRISS+NIRSpec data; comprehensive retrievals retract DMS. Madhusudhan 2025 MIRI/LRS (not ingested) reasserts DMS at 6–12 μm — discriminator is the MIRI band shape. Status: **disputed**, with current methodological consensus tilting against DMS.
- **CO₂ at 2σ vs 3.7σ.** Same data analyzed by [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] (NIRISS+NIRSpec only) and Hu et al. 2025 (NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI; not ingested) gives different significances. Likely driven by MIRI inclusion + prior choice.
- **Madhusudhan 2023 published spectrum vs Fernández-Rodríguez reduction.** Even using Madhusudhan's published spectrum, a comprehensive retrieval finds CO₂ at 2.90σ and no DMS — narrower than Madhusudhan 2023 reported. The difference is **retrieval setup, not data reduction**.

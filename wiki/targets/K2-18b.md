# K2-18 b

Habitable-zone sub-Neptune (R = 2.610 R⊕, M = 8.63 M⊕, T_eq ≈ 255 K) on a 32.9-day orbit around the M3 dwarf [[K2-18]] (Foreman-Mackey et al. 2015, Montet et al. 2015; not ingested). The most contested sub-Neptune in the wiki: a Hycean candidate (Madhusudhan et al. 2021, 2023; not ingested; reaffirmed via MIRI in [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]) versus a magma-ocean / oxygen-poor primordial mini-Neptune (Wogan et al. 2024, Shorttle et al. 2024 not ingested; [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]]) — with multiple JWST analyses giving subtly different chemical compositions on the same data. As of mid-2025 the converging methodological consensus is: **CH₄ robustly detected** (4.2σ ensemble in [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]]); **CO₂ marginal at 2–3σ** depending on retrieval setup, with [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]] showing the apparent CO₂ feature is reduction-specific; **DMS/DMDS not robustly preferred** under comprehensive joint NIR+MIR retrievals or under multi-reduction NIR-only ensemble analysis ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]], [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]], [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]], [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]]) — though [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] maintains a 3σ MIRI-only DMS/DMDS preference; high C/O ratio (≈ 3 in Schmidt mini-Neptune scenario; ≈ 10–13× in Fernández-Rodríguez IOPF scenario) supports a primordial H₂-He atmosphere without invoking life or a liquid water surface.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Robust detection:**
  - [[CH4]] — 4.16σ–4.62σ across all configurations ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]]); log VMR ≈ −1.7 to −1.0; reproduced at higher abundance (~1 dex above two-offset case) under panchromatic exoTEDRF/SPARTA retrievals ([[2025_Luque_K2-18b]]).
- **Marginal / configuration-dependent:**
  - [[CO2]] — typically ~2σ in comprehensive retrievals (Bayes factor ~1); 2.0σ in fiducial ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]]); 3.7σ in Hu et al. 2025 (not ingested) joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI retrieval. log VMR ≈ −3.94⁺¹·⁶⁸₋₄·²⁰; long tail to lower values. Robust two-sided posterior under SPARTA+JExoRES reductions in [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] (log VMR ≈ −1.66 to −2.13); broader under exoTEDRF.
- **Contested:**
  - [[DMS]] — Madhusudhan 2023 (not ingested) reported tentative NIR detection; [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] reports independent MIRI evidence at 3σ (log X(DMS) ≈ −3.42⁺¹·¹⁶₋₁·⁴⁴). Retracted under comprehensive NIR-only retrievals ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]], Bayes factor < 1; [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]] ensemble max significance 2.2σ across 60 NIRISS+NIRSpec data combinations, with 95% upper limit log C₂H₆S < −3.70). Joint NIR+MIR panchromatic retrieval finds Δln Z = −0.3 to +2.8 — **never reaches the ≥5 strong-evidence threshold** ([[2025_Luque_K2-18b]]). Ethane (C₂H₆) and 10 other hydrocarbons fit the data comparably ([[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]]). **Model-independent Gaussian-feature analysis** of the MIRI/LRS spectrum ([[2025_Taylor_K2-18b-MIRI]]) shows the data **do not robustly prefer any spectral features** over a flat line: 5 of 6 Gaussian-feature tests give "no evidence" (Δln Z < 1); only the dual-Gaussian with positions fixed at 7 + 8.8 μm reaches Δln Z = 1.21 (weak evidence, ~2σ).
  - [[DMDS]] — claimed at 3σ in MIRI-only canonical retrieval ([[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]); not preferred in panchromatic retrieval ([[2025_Luque_K2-18b]]); confirmed at ≥2.5σ in MIRI only but not NIR ([[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]]); spectrally degenerate with DMS at the present S/N. [[2025_Taylor_K2-18b-MIRI]] shows the MIRI features themselves are not robust over a flat line.
- **Ruled out / upper limits (95%):** H₂O < 10⁻²·²⁹, CO < 10⁻²·⁸⁰, NH₃ < 10⁻⁴·⁴¹, HCN < 10⁻²·⁵⁴, H₂S < 10⁻²·⁸⁵, SO₂ < 10⁻³·²⁰, OCS < 10⁻⁴·⁶¹, CH₃Cl < 10⁻³·⁷², N₂O < 10⁻³·¹⁴, CS₂ < 10⁻⁵·³².
- **MMW** = 2.5–10 amu depending on retrieval complexity; comprehensive 13-species retrieval prefers high-MMW (~4.3 amu).
- **C/O** = 10.3–13.4 (super-stellar by ~10–20×); both C and O abundances elevated absolutely.

## Observations to date

**JWST [[JWST-GO-2722]] (PI: Madhusudhan):**
- **NIRISS SOSS** transit on 2023 January 20–21 (~5.3 hr; first-order 0.85–2.83 μm; spot-crossing event during ingress).
- **NIRSpec G395H** transit on 2023 June 1 (~4.9 hr; 2.8–5.2 μm with detector gap).

**JWST MIRI follow-up:**
- **MIRI/LRS** transit on 2024 April 25–26 (5.85 hr; 2.68 hr in-transit; 5095 integrations; ~5–12 μm; under [[JWST-GO-2722]]) — first MIRI observation, reported in [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]].
- Hu et al. 2025 (not ingested) joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI reanalysis — reports CO₂ at 3.7σ.

**Comprehensive ensemble reanalysis** of NIRISS+NIRSpec ([[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]]): two NIRISS reductions ([[FIREFLy]], [[exoTEDRF]]), four NIRSpec reductions ([[FIREFLy]], [[exoTEDRF]], two [[Eureka]] variants), **first analysis of the NIRISS SOSS 2nd order**, multiple resolutions, two independent retrievals ([[POSEIDON]] + [[BeAR]]); >250 retrievals across 60 data combinations. The ensemble posterior method is explicitly novel for JWST atmospheric inference. Confirms CH₄ (4.2σ) but finds CO₂ and DMS reduction-and-resolution-dependent (max 2.3σ and 2.2σ respectively); detects 5⁺³₋₂% cool starspot coverage via the 2nd-order data. Argues an oxygen-poor mini-Neptune (C/O ≈ 3.25) with nitrogen depletion to a magma layer naturally explains observed CH₄ and the H₂O / CO₂ / NH₃ upper limits without invoking a liquid water ocean.

**Aerosol-inclusive reanalysis** (using literature-reduced spectra):
- [[2025_Jaziri_K2-18b]] joint analysis of Madhusudhan 2023 NIRISS+NIRSpec spectrum + [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] Eureka! MIRI/LRS reduction. **Photochemical CH₄-derived tholins (exo1 from PAMPRE)** — adding both 7 μm C–H bending absorption and shorter-wavelength scattering — improve the combined fit (Bayes factor 5.53), reduce the inferred CH₄ abundance by ~2 dex (log VMR -1.2 → -3.5), and lower metallicity from 20× → 0.3× solar; reinforces the metallicity-aerosol-composition degeneracy.

**Photochemical-Hycean self-consistency reanalysis** ([[2026_Fujisawa_K2-18b]]): applies the [[PROTEUS]] 1D photochemical model + Yoshida 2025 radiative-convective climate model to a 1-bar H₂ envelope over a liquid-water ocean (T_s = 328 K). Photodissociation of CH₄ drives **percent-level CO** in the upper atmosphere + a sharp **CH₄ depletion layer near 600 km altitude**, producing "photochemical saturation" plateaus in the 2.8–4.0 μm band that **better fit the data than vertically uniform mixing ratios** (R≈25 reduced χ² 1.95 vs 2.50 for exoTEDRF; 1.95 vs 2.38 for FIREFLy; 3.15 vs 4.26 for Madhusudhan reduction). The CO/CO₂ posterior is *strongly binning-dependent*: at R≈55 the Madhusudhan reduction favors X_CO ≲ 10⁻³, while at native R≈100 the exoTEDRF reduction prefers X_CO ~ 10⁻³–10⁻² — argues the Hycean scenario **cannot yet be ruled out** by current CO/CO₂ data. Mass-balance + ocean-buffering analysis allows CO₂ to be buffered at 10⁻³–10⁻² in CO₂-poor scenarios (β ~ 10²–10³ alkaline ocean). CH₄ photochemical lifetime is ~1.2×10⁷ yr — much shorter than the system age — so a Hycean K2-18 b atmosphere, if real, requires sustained interior CH₄/H₂ replenishment (~5×10³ kg s⁻¹), making it a *dynamic steady state* rather than a primordial configuration.

**Biosignature-perspective assessment** ([[2025_Seager_biosignature-prospects]]): the K2-18 b DMS claim fails all three of the Seager et al. **Detection / Attribution / Interpretation** key criteria — detection ~2.4σ tentative + offset-sensitive, attribution ambiguous (multiple sulfur gases share spectral bands), interpretation requires ~20× modern-Earth biological DMS flux. Framing converges with the Schmidt/Luque/Pica-Ciamarra/Fernández-Rodríguez retractions.

**Pre-JWST HST/WFC3** (Benneke et al. 2019, Tsiaras et al. 2019; not ingested) — first H₂O detection / methane reinterpretation.

## Open questions

- **Is CO₂ real?** Marginal at 2σ in [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] but 3.7σ in Hu et al. 2025 (joint NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI; not ingested). [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] finds two-sided posteriors under SPARTA+JExoRES, broader under exoTEDRF. Multi-visit + multi-instrument synthesis needed.
- **Are the MIRI features astrophysical or systematic?** [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] argues the MIRI feature amplitudes require T_p ≈ 422 K (per [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]) — inconsistent with the energy-balance bound of 380 K. ~26 stacked MIRI transits would be required for a 3σ rejection of a flat-line model; ~4 transits/year available.
- **Is DMS spectroscopically unique?** [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]] systematic 650-molecule search finds DMS is one of 11 species reaching ≥2.5σ in MIRI; diethyl sulfide, methacrylonitrile, propyne, and 8 other hydrocarbons fit comparably. [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] make the same point with C₂H₆ alone. Discriminator is now chemistry/plausibility rather than statistics.
- **Hycean vs primordial-with-elevated-O-C.** [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] favors the latter via IOPF; Madhusudhan 2023 + [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] favor the former. The discriminator is the inferred C/O and absolute C/H abundance — but error bars span both regimes.
- **Comparator diversity.** [[K2-18b]] (CH₄ + tentative CO₂, contested DMS), [[TOI-270d]] (CH₄ + CO₂ + tentative CS₂, no SO₂), [[LP-791-18c]] (CH₄ + haze, no CO₂) — three temperate sub-Neptunes with three distinct chemistries.

## Tensions

- **DMS detection (Madhusudhan 2023; reasserted in [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]) vs non-detection ([[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]], [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]], [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]], [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]], [[2025_Taylor_K2-18b-MIRI]], Welbanks-Nixon 2025 not ingested).** Same NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI data; comprehensive retrievals retract DMS. The MIRI claim ([[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]) hinges on a 2-molecule canonical model (CH₄+CO₂+X); broadening to all 20 molecules originally tested in M25, or to 650 ([[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]]), or to joint NIR+MIR ([[2025_Luque_K2-18b]]) reduces the statistical preference to ≤2.8σ — well below the 5σ standard. **Most directly**, [[2025_Taylor_K2-18b-MIRI]]'s model-agnostic Gaussian-feature analysis of the same MIRI/LRS spectrum finds 5 of 6 tests give "no evidence" over a flat line; only fixed-Gaussians at 7 + 8.8 μm reach weak evidence (~2σ). Status: **disputed**, with current methodological consensus tilting strongly against a robust DMS detection.
- **MIRI photospheric temperature inconsistent with energy balance.** [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] retrieves T_p ≈ 422⁺¹⁴¹₋₁₃₃ K at 1 mbar from MIRI alone — required to match the amplitude of MIRI features under self-consistent scale-height physics. [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] note this exceeds the zero-albedo no-atmosphere bound (380 K) and retrieve T_p ≈ 175⁺⁵⁰₋₄₀ K panchromatically. Discriminator: are MIRI feature amplitudes ~300 ppm planetary or systematic?
- **CO₂ at 2σ vs 3.7σ.** Same data analyzed by [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] (NIRISS+NIRSpec only) and Hu et al. 2025 (NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI; not ingested) gives different significances. Likely driven by MIRI inclusion + prior choice. [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] note CO₂ posterior shape is reduction-dependent (SPARTA/JExoRES tight; exoTEDRF broad).
- **DMS/DMDS opacity at 1 mbar is unknown.** Both [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] and [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] use Sharpe et al. 2004 cross-sections at 1 bar / 278 K — pressure-extrapolated to the actual ~1 mbar K2-18 b photosphere. Real DMS/DMDS absorption at sub-Neptune conditions could differ substantially; abundance estimates carry a systematic bias of unknown sign and magnitude.
- **Madhusudhan 2023 published spectrum vs Fernández-Rodríguez reduction.** Even using Madhusudhan's published spectrum, a comprehensive retrieval finds CO₂ at 2.90σ and no DMS — narrower than Madhusudhan 2023 reported. The difference is **retrieval setup, not data reduction**.

# LTT 1445 A b

A rocky planet (Rp=1.34 R⊕, Mp=2.73 M⊕, Teq=431 K) orbiting the primary of the nearby (6.86 pc) triple M-dwarf system LTT 1445. Bulk density 6.2±1.3 g/cm³ is consistent with an Earth-like rocky composition. Instellation (~5.77 S⊕) is Mercury-like. The **closest known transiting rocky exoplanet** at the time of the JWST observations. Discovery: Winters et al. 2019; parameters updated in Pass et al. 2023 (not yet ingested).

## Observations to date
MIRI/LRS thermal emission from [[JWST-GO-2708]] ([[2024_Wachiraphan_LTT1445Ab]]) yields a dayside temperature 525±15 K and temperature ratio R = 0.95±0.06 consistent with a dark rocky surface. The paper rules out thick atmospheres (100 bar CO₂ at >6σ) while leaving moderately thin atmospheres (Mars/Titan/Earth-like) unconstrained. Prior NIRSpec/G395H transmission (Diamond-Lowe et al. 2024; not yet ingested) ruled out clear H/He atmospheres. Working conclusion: **likely bare rock, possibly with a thin secondary atmosphere**.

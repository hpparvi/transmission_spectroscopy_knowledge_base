# L 98-59 b

A sub-Earth (R_p = 0.85 R⊕, M_p ≈ 0.40 M⊕, ρ ≈ 3.6 g/cm³) on a 2.25-day orbit around the bright nearby M3V dwarf [[L98-59]]. The **smallest exoplanet with a JWST transmission spectrum**. JWST NIRSpec G395H 4-transit data ([[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]]) detect an **SO₂-dominated volcanic atmosphere** at 3.6σ — first wiki target with a [[volcanic-atmosphere]] inference. The eccentric orbit (e = 0.103⁺⁰·¹¹⁷₋₀·₀₄₅) implies tidal heating sufficient to drive runaway mantle melting; the inferred outgassing rate requires ≥8× Io's tidal heating per unit mass; subsurface magma ocean spanning **60–90% of the planetary radius** is predicted. The detection contradicts L 98-59 b's position on the airless side of the [[cosmic-shoreline]] — a third independent wiki shoreline challenger.

## Atmospheric composition

- **Detected:**
  - [[SO2]] — volume mixing ratio > 90% at 68% confidence; primary spectral driver via the 3.9–4.5 μm and 2.8–3.1 μm bands ([[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]]).
- **Ruled out / upper limits (2σ):** log VMR(H₂O), CH₄, CO, NH₃, H₂S all consistent with non-detection; H₂ < 24%; CO₂ < 84%.
- **MMW** ≈ 64 amu (consistent with high SO₂); inconsistent with light primordial atmosphere (μ < 2.3 amu rejected at 2σ).

## Observations to date

**JWST [[JWST-GO-3942]] (PI: Damiano):**
- **NIRSpec G395H** four transit observations on 2024 January 30 / February 3, 6, 19 (each 0.97 hr transit + 1.87 hr baseline); 11,288 integrations total. Two independent reductions ([[Eureka]] + [[FIREFLy]]) and three retrieval codes ([[ExoTR]], [[Aurora]], [[POSEIDON]]) all converge on the SO₂-rich atmosphere preference at 3.3–3.8σ.

**Pre-JWST:**
- HST/WFC3 transmission spectrum (Damiano et al. 2022 + Zhou et al. 2022; not ingested) — featureless; rules out cloud-free H₂-rich atmosphere; high-MMW remains allowed (consistent with the SO₂-rich inference).

## Interior implications

- **Subsurface magma ocean R_m ≈ 0.6–0.9 R_p** required if SO₂ outgassing balances XUV-driven escape via runaway-melting (Peale & Cassen 1978; Seligman et al. 2024, not ingested) framework.
- **Tidal quality factor Q_L98-59b ≲ 1400 Q_Io.**
- **Oxidized mantle f_O₂ > IW + 2.7** (≥2.7 log units above iron-wüstite) required for SO₂ outgassing rather than H₂S or H₂O-dominated.

## Open questions

- **Multi-visit / multi-instrument confirmation.** Bello-Arufe estimate ~6 more transits would push the SO₂ detection past 5σ — feasible given the bright host (J=7.9) and short period.
- **MIRI/LRS follow-up for SO₂ ν₂ band at 7.5 μm.** Currently inferred from NIRSpec-only bands at 3.9–4.5 μm and 2.8–3.1 μm.
- **Tidal-Q calibration.** The 8× Io scaling is a zeroth-order estimate; detailed interior modeling with viscoelastic dissipation could refine.
- **System-scale context.** Why does L 98-59 b (rocky, volcanic) differ from [[L98-59c]] (featureless rocky) and [[L98-59d]] (water-world candidate, S-bearing tentative)?

## Tensions

- **Frequentist vs Bayesian preference.** Flat-line model gives p = 0.81 (cannot be rejected) but Bayesian evidence prefers SO₂ atmosphere at 3.3–3.8σ across codes. Standard metrics give different conclusions — characteristic of the marginal-feature S/N regime.
- **Cosmic shoreline.** L 98-59 b is on the "predominantly airless" side of the Zahnle & Catling 2017 (not ingested) shoreline; SO₂ detection contradicts the placement, joining [[TOI-270b]], [[2025_Teske_TOI561b]], and [[HD3167b]] in pressuring the original formulation.

# TOI-561 b

An ultra-short-period super-Earth (P = 0.446 d, R ≈ 1.45 R⊕, M ≈ 2.24 M⊕, ρ ≈ 4.3 g/cm³) orbiting an ancient (~10 Gyr) thick-disk K dwarf. JWST NIRSpec G395H secondary eclipse observations find a dayside temperature significantly cooler than predicted for a bare rock, providing one of the first detections consistent with a thick volatile-rich atmosphere on a rocky exoplanet and challenging the cosmic shoreline hypothesis.

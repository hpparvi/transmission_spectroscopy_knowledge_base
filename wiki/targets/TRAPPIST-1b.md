# TRAPPIST-1 b

The innermost of the seven TRAPPIST-1 transiting terrestrial planets (Gillon et al. 2016, 2017; not yet ingested), on a 1.51-day orbit around the M8V ultracool dwarf [[TRAPPIST-1]]. Prior JWST work (Greene et al. 2023; not yet ingested) is consistent with no thick atmosphere; the planet is treated as a near-airless reference body in [[2025_Rathcke_TRAPPIST1bc]], where it is used to correct the [[TRAPPIST-1c]] transit spectrum for [[stellar-contamination]] via [[back-to-back-transit-correction]].

## Observations to date
TRAPPIST-1 b has been observed across three JWST programs. **NIRISS/SOSS** ([[2023_Lim_TRAPPIST1b]], 0.6–2.8 μm, GO 2589) observed two transits in July 2022 and found the spectrum dominated by [[stellar-contamination]] from contrasting photospheric heterogeneities: cool spots in Visit 1 and warm faculae in Visit 2 at ~29% covering fractions each. After modeling the contamination, no planetary atmosphere was detected; H₂-dominated atmospheres are rejected at 16–29σ, but secondary atmospheres cannot be constrained. **NIRSpec/PRISM** ([[2025_Rathcke_TRAPPIST1bc]], 0.6–5.3 μm, GO 2420) uses TRAPPIST-1 b as a near-airless reference to empirically correct TRAPPIST-1 c's transmission spectrum — the first application of [[back-to-back-transit-correction]]. **MIRI F1500W** photometric secondary eclipses (5 visits, GTO 1177; original Greene et al. 2023 not yet ingested) are uniformly reanalyzed in [[2025_Connors_MIRI-15um]] with FN-PCA: joint depth 863 ± 90 ppm, T_day = 499 ± 24 K, consistent with a low-albedo bare rock.

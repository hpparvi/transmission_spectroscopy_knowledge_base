# H₂S

Hydrogen sulfide — a sulfur-bearing volatile whose formation in an atmosphere typically traces volcanic outgassing or magma-ocean equilibria. Absorption bands across the NIRSpec G395H range (notably near 3.3 μm and 4.4 μm) make it accessible to JWST transmission spectroscopy of small planets, though atmospheric detections on rocky exoplanets are a recent development.

## Papers
- [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] — primary opacity source for the ~5.6σ atmospheric detection on L 98-59 d; log mixing ratio near the prior upper edge.
- [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] — log X(H₂S) ≈ −0.62 retrieved in the preferred Stellar+Atmosphere scenario on L 98-59 d; individual detection significance ~2σ.
- [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]] — tentative detection in WASP-39 b in some reductions of the ERS PRISM dataset but not all; pipeline-dependent.
- [[2026_Heinke_HATP12b]] — detected at ~3.7σ on HAT-P-12 b via the 3.8 μm ν₁ band (TEATRO reduction); first JWST detection of H₂S in a sub-Saturn atmosphere; CASCADe vs TEATRO reduction choice affects significance.
- [[2025_Crouzet_HATP12b]] — marginal 3.2σ in CASCADe NIRSpec-only reduction; **absent** in TEATRO NIRSpec-only — pipeline-dependent in line with the panchromatic Heinke 2026 conclusion.

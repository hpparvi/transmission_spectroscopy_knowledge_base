# CH₄

Methane — expected in cool/reduced atmospheres in thermochemical equilibrium; its detection or non-detection constrains C/O ratio, metallicity, and disequilibrium processes (quenching, photochemistry). Strong 3.3 μm absorption feature accessible to JWST NIRSpec G395H.

## Papers
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — coadded four-visit GJ 1132 b spectrum rejects pure-CH₄ atmospheres at > 10σ and H₂O + 1–10% CH₄ mixtures; retires the tentative Visit-1 CH₄ inference from [[2023_May_GJ1132b]].
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — pure-CH₄ atmosphere forward models ruled out for L 168-9 b by the featureless NIR + MIR spectrum.
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — ~1% CH₄ tentatively inferred in Visit 1 of GJ 1132 b from the 3.3 μm region (Bayes factor 27 / 3.1σ vs no CH₄), not reproduced in Visit 2.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — pure-CH₄ atmospheres strongly disfavored on GJ 486 b (χ²ν = 2.1 in forward modeling); retrievals report stringent upper limits on CH₄ abundance.
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — pure-CH₄ atmospheres rejected at ≥5σ for LHS 475 b by the absence of the strong 3.3 μm band; 3σ feature upper limit of 38 ppm at 3.3 μm.

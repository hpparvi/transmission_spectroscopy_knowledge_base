# CS₂

Carbon disulfide — a sulfur-bearing organic species predicted by Moses et al. 2024 (in prep; not ingested) to be the dominant sulfur reservoir in **temperate sub-Neptune** atmospheres, formed through the H₂S → CH₃ → HCS → CS → CS₂ network at temperatures 300–500 K. Has a strong absorption band centered at 4.6 μm — accessible to [[JWST-NIRSpec]] G395H — but with an opacity profile **degenerate with CH₃Cl, CH₃F, and H₂CS** in this wavelength range. First proposed for an exoplanet atmosphere by [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]].

## Papers

- [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] — moderate evidence (Bayes factor 78 in fiducial sulfur model; log VMR ≈ −2.13⁺⁰·³⁷₋₀·⁵⁴) on temperate sub-Neptune TOI-270 d (T_eq = 360 K) NIRSpec G395H + NIRISS SOSS; cannot be statistically distinguished from CH₃Cl, CH₃F, or H₂CS at JWST/NIRSpec resolution; MIRI 8–14 μm needed to break the degeneracy.

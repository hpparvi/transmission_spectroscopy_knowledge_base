# SiO

Silicon monoxide — the simplest gas-phase silicon-bearing molecule and the dominant Si carrier in the very hot, low-pressure regimes where silicates have evaporated. Has long been suspected as the carrier of the NUV rise in ultra-hot-Jupiter transmission spectra (Lothringer et al. 2022 hypothesis): in atmospheres with T_eq > ∼2100 K, silicate clouds (MgSiO₃, Mg₂SiO₄) are vaporized, releasing gas-phase SiO that absorbs broadly in the NUV; in cooler hot Jupiters (T_eq < 2100 K), silicates condense out and the NUV rise vanishes. The 4.0–4.5 μm SiO bands are accessible to [[JWST-NIRSpec]] G395H. Constraining gas-phase SiO simultaneously with H₂O / CO is a path to **refractory-to-volatile abundance ratios (Si/O)**, which trace the rocky-material accretion fraction during planet migration.

## Papers

- [[2025_Gapp_WASP121b]] — **first definitive identification** of SiO via direct spectral signature in JWST NIRSpec G395H on [[WASP-121b]]; 5.2σ detection compatible with chemical equilibrium on the dayside; supports the Lothringer 2022 hypothesis distinguishing T_eq > 2100 K from cooler hot Jupiters.

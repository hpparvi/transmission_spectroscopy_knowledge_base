# H₂O

Water vapor — a dominant near-infrared opacity source in warm-to-hot planetary atmospheres, with broad absorption bands across 1–5 μm that make it the most commonly detected molecule in exoplanet transmission spectra.

## Papers
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — four-visit coadded GJ 1132 b spectrum: thin H₂O (steam) atmospheres remain marginally consistent with the data, but leave-one-out analysis shows the fit is driven entirely by the earlier Visit 1; retires the H₂O inference.
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — pure-H₂O atmosphere forward models tested on L 168-9 b; featureless NIR + MIR spectrum rules out cloudless H₂O-rich atmospheres at <100× solar metallicity.
- [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]] — MIRI/LRS emission of Gl 486 b rules out thick water-rich atmospheres; only atmospheres with <1% H₂O at ≤0.1 bar are still allowed, retiring the water-rich transmission interpretation of [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]].
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — tentatively inferred as the dominant gas in Visit 1 of GJ 1132 b (log X_{H2O} ≈ −0.01), but the detection is degenerate with unocculted starspots and not reproduced in Visit 2.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — [[POSEIDON]] retrievals favor H₂O as the dominant gas on GJ 486 b at log X(H₂O) > 10% (2σ); Bayes factors 133 / 8 (3.6σ / 2.6σ) in [[Eureka]] / [[FIREFLy]] reductions. Interpretation equally well explained by unocculted starspots — later resolved in favor of starspots by [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]].
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — 3σ upper limit of 61 ppm on any H₂O absorption feature at 2.8 μm in LHS 475 b's transmission spectrum; pure H₂O atmospheres remain consistent with the data at high MMW.

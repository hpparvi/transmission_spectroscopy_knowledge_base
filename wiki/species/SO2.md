# SO₂

Sulfur dioxide — a key photochemical product in atmospheres containing H₂O + sulfur species. Its first unambiguous exoplanet detection was on the hot Jupiter WASP-39 b (Alderson, Rustamkulov et al. 2023; not yet ingested), in which it is produced photochemically from H₂S + H₂O. The 4.05 μm band is accessible to NIRSpec/G395H; much stronger bands at 7–10 μm are accessible to MIRI/LRS. On rocky M-dwarf planets SO₂ may instead originate via direct volcanic outgassing.

## Papers
- [[2025_Fisher_TOI1685b]] — pure SO₂ secondary atmosphere ruled out for TOI-1685 b from five NIRSpec G395H transits; SO₂ also ruled out as a component of mixed secondary atmospheres (CO + CO₂ + SO₂).
- [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] — bimodal posterior on L 98-59 d; either log X(SO₂) > −1 (detected) or < −1.5 (upper limit), in either case a tentative contributor to the 4.4 μm feature.
- [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] — log X(SO₂) ≈ −2.35 in the preferred retrieval on L 98-59 d; individual detection significance ~2σ.

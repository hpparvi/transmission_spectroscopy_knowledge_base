# SO₂

Sulfur dioxide — a key photochemical product in atmospheres containing H₂O + sulfur species. Its first unambiguous exoplanet detection was on the hot Jupiter WASP-39 b (Alderson, Rustamkulov et al. 2023; not yet ingested), in which it is produced photochemically from H₂S + H₂O. The 4.05 μm band is accessible to NIRSpec/G395H; much stronger bands at 7–10 μm are accessible to MIRI/LRS. On rocky M-dwarf planets SO₂ may instead originate via direct volcanic outgassing.

## Papers
- [[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]] — **first wiki SO₂ on a rocky planet.** Sub-Earth L 98-59 b (R_p = 0.85 R⊕); JWST NIRSpec G395H 4-transit observations; SO₂-rich (>90% VMR at 68% confidence) atmosphere preferred at 3.3–3.8σ across three retrieval codes (ExoTR/Aurora/POSEIDON). Production via **volcanic outgassing** (≥8× Io tidal heating per unit mass) rather than photochemistry from H₂O + H₂S. Discriminator: oxidized mantle f_O₂ > IW + 2.7 vs reduced-mantle H₂S-dominated outgassing.
- [[2025_Gressier_HATP26b]] — **first SO₂ in a Neptune-mass planet** (HAT-P-26 b, M = 18.6 M⊕, T_eq ≈ 1000 K); lnB = 13.46 (≥5σ); log X(SO₂) ≈ −4.40; bridges hot-Jupiter and sub-Neptune SO₂ detections; population analysis of 11 JWST giants empirically validates the [[SO2-photochemical-shoreline]] (Crossfield 2023) trend.
- [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] — **3σ upper limit log X < −5** on temperate sub-Neptune TOI-270 d; **retracts** Benneke et al. 2024's tentative SO₂ inference on the same data; an independent-reanalysis null result.
- [[2025_Crossfield_SO2shoreline]] — theoretical mapping paper: 936-model HELIOS+VULCAN+petitRADTRANS grid characterizing where SO₂ is observable in (T_eq, [M/H]) space. SO₂ never the dominant sulfur species in any modeled atmosphere; observability is its only privilege. The 4.1 μm band saturates at high metallicity; 7.4/8.7 μm bands are more sensitive metallicity tracers but require MIRI.
- [[2025_Fisher_TOI1685b]] — pure SO₂ secondary atmosphere ruled out for TOI-1685 b from five NIRSpec G395H transits; SO₂ also ruled out as a component of mixed secondary atmospheres (CO + CO₂ + SO₂).
- [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] — bimodal posterior on L 98-59 d; either log X(SO₂) > −1 (detected) or < −1.5 (upper limit), in either case a tentative contributor to the 4.4 μm feature.
- [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] — log X(SO₂) ≈ −2.35 in the preferred retrieval on L 98-59 d; individual detection significance ~2σ.
- [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]] — confirmed detection in WASP-39 b across all four data-reduction pipelines of the ERS PRISM dataset; photochemical origin (as in original ERS papers, not yet ingested).
- [[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]] — tentative 2.3σ detection on the WASP-17 b **nightside** via iESR-PIE; abundance requires day-to-night transport from a ~10× solar metallicity dayside per 2D VULCAN photochemistry.
- [[2026_Radica_WASP96b]] — moderate evidence (lnB = 2.69) for SO₂ in WASP-96 b; abundance matched to Crossfield+2025 [[SO2-photochemical-shoreline]] grid at 10–20× solar; second wiki target on the proposed shoreline after WASP-39 b.
- [[2025_Murphy_WASP107b]] — first **limb-resolved SO₂ asymmetry** on a JWST target; WASP-107 b morning limb log VMR ≈ −4.7 vs. evening −5.4 (5×); photochemistry interpretation; consistent with 2D quench-photochemistry models predicting SO₂ buildup on the cooler morning limb.
- [[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]] — SO₂ at 4σ on the young (10–30 Myr) sub-Neptune progenitor V1298 Tau b (T_eq ≈ 670 K); first SO₂ in a sub-Neptune-mass planet (~12 M⊕); abundance is high relative to PICASO grid prediction (under-predicts), consistent with 100× solar UV irradiation from the active T Tauri host driving photochemistry.
- [[2025_Deka_WASP39b]] — robust detection in WASP-39 b NIRISS+NIRSpec PRISM+MIRI panchromatic data; log VMR ≈ −5.73 (free) to −6.0 (equilibrium); independent confirmation of the original ERS detection (Alderson et al. 2023; not ingested).
- [[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]] — tentative on warm sub-Neptune GJ 3090 b NIRSpec G395H; helps drive the chemically-consistent inference of sub-solar C/O atop high metallicity; not a free-retrieval detection.
- [[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]] — **first SO₂ detection on a warm mini-Neptune**: TOI-1130 b (3.66 R⊕, T_eq = 825 K), 3.6σ from the 4 μm band in combined NIRSpec G395H + NIRISS SOSS + TESS + CHEOPS transmission; log mass fraction ≈ −3.0. Photochemical origin (equilibrium chemistry does not produce SO₂ at this temperature). Joins [[2025_Crossfield_SO2shoreline]] empirical sample at [M/H] ≈ 60× solar.
- [[2026_Wogan_LTT1445Ab]] — climate-constrained 2σ upper limit log P_SO₂ < −4.0 bar for [[LTT1445Ab]]; most restrictive rocky-planet SO₂ upper limit to date.
- [[2025_Fu_statistical-trends]] — **first wiki population-level SO₂ study**. Defines a scale-height-normalized **SO₂-L band index** (3.95–4.1 μm SO₂ band minus 2.9–3.7 μm L-band) on 8 high-precision JWST transmission spectra. SO₂-L correlates negatively with T_eq (r = −0.64 ± 0.08) and planet mass (r = −0.41 ± 0.09): **SO₂ preferably exists at T_eq ≲ 1200 K and M_p ≲ 0.3 M_J**. Positive correlation with bulk Z fraction (r = 0.54 ± 0.10 for T_eq < 1400 K) — SO₂ traces metallicity. SO₂-L vs CO₂-L color-color diagram introduced as a population-characterization framework analogous to the HR diagram.

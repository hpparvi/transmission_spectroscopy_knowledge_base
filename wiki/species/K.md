# K

Atomic potassium; an alkali metal with a prominent doublet at ~770 nm; a companion detection to [[Na]] in hot-Jupiter transmission spectra. Detected in WASP-39 b by the original ERS team (not yet ingested) and confirmed across multiple independent reductions in [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]].

## Papers

- [[2026_RoyPerez_WASP39b]] — confirmed detection in WASP-39 b across independent reductions of the NIRSpec PRISM ERS dataset.
- [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — detected at 4.9σ on HD 209458 b from STIS+WFC3+NIRCam free-chemistry SANSAR retrieval; log VMR ≈ −6.93; companion to the Na detection and the first robust JWST-era K confirmation in HD 209458 b's atmosphere.
- [[2025_Deka_WASP39b]] — K constrained on WASP-39 b panchromatic NIRISS+PRISM+MIRI [[NEXOTRANS]] hybrid model; log VMR ≈ −8.30 to −8.74 across Bayesian and ML retrievals; consistent with low-K hot-Jupiter atmospheres; broadly compatible across all four chemistry options.
- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — tentative narrow K core at 2.5σ in HST/ACS pixel-by-pixel reanalysis of [[HD189733b]]; **broad wings absent**, consistent with the same alkali-condensation / aerosol-obscuration explanation as for Na in this target.
- [[2008_Pont_HD189733b]] — non-detection of broad K wings on HD 189733 b in HST/ACS G800L; flux excess in 15-nm K band: 1.6 ± 5.2 × 10⁻⁵.
- [[2001_Brown_TransmissionDiagnostics]] — Brown 2001 predictions for K resonance doublet at 767 nm on HD 209458 b; line cores ~10⁻³ relative to stellar continuum.
- [[2000_Seager_TheoryTransmission]] — foundational prediction of the K I resonance doublet at 767.0 nm as a companion transmission feature to Na I.

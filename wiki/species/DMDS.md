# DMDS — dimethyl disulfide ((CH₃)₂S₂)

A volatile organosulfur molecule (Domagal-Goldman et al. 2011; Seager et al. 2013; not ingested) proposed alongside [[DMS]] as a potential sub-Neptune biosignature. Structurally two methyl groups bridged by an S–S bond. Its MIRI absorption bands (broad ~6.8–8 μm shared with DMS; distinct ~10.5 μm methyl-bending feature) are reportedly present in the MIRI/LRS spectrum of [[K2-18b]] under [[JWST-GO-2722]] ([[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]]) at log VMR ≈ −3.5 (~10⁻³–10⁻⁴) — a putative inference that is **spectrally degenerate** with DMS and refuted in panchromatic joint NIR+MIR retrieval ([[2025_Luque_K2-18b]]) and broadened-candidate retrieval ([[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]]).

Like DMS, the opacity data are sparse: Sharpe et al. 2004 HITRAN cross-sections at 1 bar / 278 K only, extrapolated to sub-Neptune ~1 mbar photospheres at unknown bias. No firm DMDS detection exists in the wiki.

## Papers

- [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] — DMDS at 3.2σ (canonical DMDS-only retrieval); jointly with DMS at 3.4σ over a flat spectrum. Retrieved abundance log X(DMDS) = −3.48⁺¹·²⁴₋₂·²⁷.
- [[2025_Luque_K2-18b]] — DMS+DMDS not preferred (Δln Z = −0.3 to +2.8 across reductions; never reaches the ≥5 strong-evidence threshold). C₂H₆ provides an equally good fit.
- [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]] — DMDS reaches ≥2.5σ in MIRI but not in NIR; one of 11 candidate molecules confirmed in MIRI by all three retrieval codes. Not unique against diethyl sulfide, methacrylonitrile, propyne, etc.
- [[2025_Taylor_K2-18b-MIRI]] — model-agnostic Gaussian critique of MIRI features; no statistical preference for any feature except weak (~2σ) signal at fixed 7 + 8.8 μm positions. DMDS-distinguishing band at 10.5 μm is not preferred over a flat line under any test.

(Madhusudhan et al. 2023 NIRISS+NIRSpec; Welbanks, Nixon et al. 2025; not yet ingested. [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]] does not test DMDS directly but applies the same ensemble-retrieval logic to NIRISS+NIRSpec DMS — finding no robust preference and a 95% upper limit; the DMDS+DMS spectral degeneracy means similar conclusions extend to DMDS in NIR.)

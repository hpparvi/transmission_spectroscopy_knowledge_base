# Aurora

Aurora (Welbanks & Madhusudhan 2021; not yet ingested) — Bayesian transmission-spectrum retrieval code with multidimensional inhomogeneous-cloud / haze treatments and limb-asymmetry support. Designed for hot-Jupiter / sub-Saturn atmospheres.

## Papers

- [[2026_Radica_WASP96b]] — one of four free-chemistry retrieval frameworks cross-compared on WASP-96 b.
- [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — used as a benchmark reference for [[SANSAR]] forward-model and inverse-model agreement on WASP-96 b; cloud/haze and reference-pressure parameters agree within 1σ.
- [[2025_Murphy_WASP107b]] — Aurora-derived best-fit models from Welbanks et al. 2024a (limb-combined retrieval; not ingested) extended to the WASP-107 b panchromatic limb-resolved χ² grid; identifies SO₂/CO₂/cloud heterogeneity between limbs.
- [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] — AURA-family retrievals (AURA + Aurora cross-check) drive the canonical 16-parameter, 2-molecule (CH₄+CO₂+DMS/DMDS) K2-18 b MIRI/LRS analysis; 3.4σ flat-line rejection.
- [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]] — uses **VIRA** (latest in the Aurora family, Constantinou & Madhusudhan 2024) as one of three independent retrieval codes (with [[POSEIDON]] and [[petitRADTRANS]]) in the 650-molecule agnostic K2-18 b search.
- [[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]] — Aurora 14-parameter retrieval (8 species + isothermal T + cloud-top P + cloud fraction + reference P + R_p + NIRS1-NRS2 offset) on the L 98-59 b NIRSpec G395H 4-transit spectrum; also a 2-parameter simple "pure SO₂" retrieval. Detects SO₂-rich atmosphere over flat line at 3.6σ (simple) / 3.3σ (14-parameter).

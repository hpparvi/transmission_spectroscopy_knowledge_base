# PACMAN-P

Coupled atmosphere–interior geochemical evolution model (Krissansen-Totton et al. 2024). Simulates magma-ocean solidification jointly with multiphase atmosphere–interior chemical equilibrium for arbitrary C-, H-, O-, and Fe-bearing compositions. At each time step it solves for surface temperature via a radiative-convective climate model and drives atmospheric escape (XUV-limited or diffusion-limited) from a parameterized stellar XUV history; mantle convection is temperature-dependent and driven by accretion heat, radionuclides, and latent heat of solidification. Used to ask **whether a given (initial volatile inventory, planet bulk, host-star XUV) combination can sustain a secondary atmosphere over Gyr timescales** despite escape losses.

## Papers
- [[2025_Coulombe_TOI-270b]] — Monte Carlo over [[TOI-270b]] initial-volatile inventories yields three illustrative outcomes consistent with the planet's bulk constraints: H₂-dominated retention (large initial inventory), H₂O-dominated steam (intermediate), and O₂-dominated post-H-escape (oxidized inventory). Provides the theoretical underpinning for a long-lived secondary atmosphere on the airless side of the [[cosmic-shoreline]].

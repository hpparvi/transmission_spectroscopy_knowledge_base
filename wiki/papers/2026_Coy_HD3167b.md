# An Atmosphere on the Ultra-Short Period super-Earth HD 3167 b

**Authors:** Coy, B. N., Weiner Mansfield, M., et al. (2026, ApJ) · [arXiv:2604.11911]
**Targets:** [[HD3167b]] · **Instrument:** [[JWST-MIRI]] (LRS)

## TL;DR

A single JWST MIRI/LRS secondary eclipse of the ultra-short-period super-Earth HD 3167 b (Teq ≈ 1786 K), observed under the LAVA LAMPS survey ([[JWST-GO-4818]]) on 2025 June 25, yields a measured eclipse depth of 38 ± 11 ppm — far below the bare-rock blackbody expectation of 104 ± 3 ppm (≈5σ deficit). The T_day/T_max ratio R = 0.57 is inconsistent with an airless body. The paper interprets this as evidence for either a highly reflective surface (geometric albedo Aeff ≈ 0.72) or heat redistribution by a substantial volatile atmosphere. Three independent reductions ([[SPARTA]], [[Eureka]], [[exoTEDRF]]) agree on the eclipse depth. HD 3167 b becomes the USP super-Earth with the lowest observed R value among the lava-world sample, making it the most compelling rocky USP candidate for atmospheric retention to date.

## Key findings

- Eclipse depth 38 ± 11 ppm; bare-rock prediction 104 ± 3 ppm → ~5σ atmospheric evidence.
- R = T_day/T_max = 0.57; bare-rock silicate scenarios require R ≈ 1.
- Two interpretations: (1) high Bond albedo (Aeff ≈ 0.72), or (2) efficient heat redistribution by a thick volatile atmosphere; the data cannot distinguish between these.
- No individual molecular species detected; composition undetermined.
- First evidence for an atmosphere on a true USP super-Earth (P < 1 day); extends the lava-world atmospheric-detection class below P ≈ 1 day.
- All three independent reductions consistent; eclipse depth is robust against systematics.
- Challenges the [[cosmic-shoreline]] expectation that planets at such irradiation levels should be stripped bare.

## Methods

Single secondary eclipse observed with JWST MIRI/LRS on 2025 June 25 under [[JWST-GO-4818]] (LAVA LAMPS survey; PI: Weiner Mansfield). Primary reduction via [[SPARTA]]; independent cross-checks with [[Eureka]] and [[exoTEDRF]]. System parameters refined with [[EXOFASTv2]]. Atmospheric models generated with [[GENESIS]] (1D radiative-convective) combined with [[FastChem]] (chemical equilibrium abundances) to produce model eclipse spectra for comparison.

## Caveats & limitations

- Single-epoch eclipse; variability over repeated observations not characterized.
- High-albedo scenario (no atmosphere) cannot be ruled out.
- No species-level spectral constraint — the MIRI/LRS measurement provides a broadband thermal-emission signal rather than molecular fingerprints.
- Host star HD 3167 also hosts outer planets (b, c, d system) that could affect thermal modeling.

## Open questions / follow-ups

- Does a second eclipse observation confirm the depth and constrain phase-curve redistribution?
- Is the emission deficit due to reflectivity or redistribution? JWST phase curve or higher-precision eclipse could discriminate.
- If a genuine atmosphere, what is the composition? H₂O or CO₂ atmospheres are possible volatile candidates; SO₂ from volcanic outgassing is another.

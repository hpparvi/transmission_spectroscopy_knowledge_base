# Escaping Helium and a Highly Muted Spectrum Suggest a Metal-enriched Atmosphere on Sub-Neptune GJ 3090 b from JWST Transit Spectroscopy

**Authors:** Ahrer, Radica, Piaulet-Ghorayeb, Raul, Wiser, Welbanks et al. (2025, ApJL 985:L10) · arXiv:2412.11643
**Target:** [[GJ3090b]] · **Host:** [[GJ3090]] · **Instruments:** [[JWST-NIRISS]] (SOSS, 0.6–2.85 μm), [[JWST-NIRSpec]] (G395H, 2.7–5.2 μm)
**Program:** [[JWST-GO-4098]]

## TL;DR

First JWST spectroscopic study of the warm sub-Neptune [[GJ3090b]] (2.13 R⊕, T_eq ≈ 693 K, M2V host) — four transits (2× NIRISS/SOSS + 2× NIRSpec/G395H). Detects the metastable [[He]] I 1083 nm triplet at 5.5σ with amplitude 434 ± 79 ppm — the **first JWST sub-Neptune helium detection** — but the amplitude is an order of magnitude smaller than solar-metallicity forward-model predictions, supporting a metal-enriched atmosphere. NIRISS/SOSS spectra are dominated by the transit-light-source ([[stellar-contamination]]) effect with spot/faculae properties varying between two visits 6 months apart, precluding atmospheric inference at λ < 2.85 μm. NIRSpec/G395H retrievals find tentative evidence for muted features and **a confident absence of CH4 at ∼3.4 μm**, driving the inference of a high-metallicity atmosphere: >100× solar at 3σ (clouds at ∼μbar pressures) or >700× solar at 2σ irrespective of clouds. Best-fit grid models prefer >1000× solar (MMW ∼27 amu). Tentative SO₂ + CO₂ hints suggest a sub-solar C/O.

## Key findings

- **He I 1083 nm**: 5.5σ detection in NIRISS/SOSS pixel-level spectra; 434 ± 79 ppm amplitude, ~10× lower than solar-metallicity forward-model predictions — empirically consistent with a high-metallicity, low-mass-loss atmosphere.
- **NIRISS spectra dominated by [[stellar-contamination]]**: TLS retrievals strongly preferred over TLS+atmosphere; visit-to-visit changes in retrieved spot/faculae parameters prevent atmospheric inference at NIRISS wavelengths.
- **CH₄ absence at 3.4 μm**: confident non-detection drives the high-metallicity inference (chemically consistent retrievals + grid analysis converge).
- **Metallicity**: free-retrieval upper limit on individual species; chemically consistent retrievals find >100× solar at 3σ for cloud-top pressures < μbar; >700× solar at 2σ regardless of clouds; best-fit grid model >1000× solar (MMW ∼27 amu).
- **Tentative species hints**: SO₂ tentative (sub-solar C/O preferred); CO₂ tentative.
- **Sub-solar C/O** explained by ice-rich formation or atmospheric evolution under interior chemical exchange / mass loss.
- Independent agreement with prior CRIRES+ K-band non-detection (Parker et al. 2025; not yet ingested), which suggested >150× solar metallicity and MMW > 7.1 g/mol.
- Adds GJ 3090 b to the warm sub-Neptune sample (∼100 K warmer than GJ 9827 d) demonstrating diverse, generally high-metallicity atmospheres.

## Methods

- **Reductions**: [[exoTEDRF]] (nominal), [[NAMELESS]] (NIRISS), [[Eureka]] (NIRSpec), [[Tiberius]] — multi-pipeline cross-validation.
- **Atmospheric retrievals**: free-chemistry on NIRSpec only; chemically consistent retrievals; self-consistent forward grid using [[ScCHIMERA]] paired with [[VULCAN]] photochemistry.
- **Interior modeling**: [[GASTLI]] mass–radius curves with envelope metal mass fractions Z_env up to pure water; intrinsic temperature 20–70 K (3σ).
- **Stellar contamination**: out-of-transit spectrum fitting with `StellarFit` (PHOENIX 1/2/3-component); in-transit retrieval via [[stctm]]; both treatments prefer heterogeneous (spot+faculae) photosphere for visit 2 but disagree for visit 1.
- **Joint interior–atmosphere**: [[joint-interior-atmosphere-retrieval]] coupling [[GASTLI]] structure to atmospheric Z_env constraints.

## Caveats & limitations

- **Stellar contamination dominates NIRISS** — atmospheric inferences are NIRSpec-only.
- **Variable TLS realizations** between NIRISS/SOSS visits separated by 6 months (∼10 stellar rotations) and between NIRISS and NIRSpec epochs (~2 months apart) — 100 ppm offset between instruments cannot be removed without a single TLS realization assumption. Authors recommend that **future programs requiring NIRISS+NIRSpec schedule visits close enough in time** that a single TLS realization can be assumed.
- **Free-retrieval inability to constrain individual species** — the muted features prevent free chemistry from pinpointing molecules; chemically consistent and grid models do the heavy lifting.
- **3D effects (e.g., terminator-asymmetric chemistry, vertical mixing) not modeled** — alternative explanations for the CH₄ absence besides high metallicity (analogous to hot-Jupiter 3D effects in Zamyatina et al. 2023, 2024) cannot be ruled out.
- **Stellar models are imperfect fits** to GJ 3090's photosphere (χ²ν > 150 in absolute fits) — in-transit retrievals using model ratios may be more robust than absolute fits, but uncertainties propagate.

## Open questions / follow-ups

- **Direct molecular abundance constraints** require additional G395H or panchromatic visits; multiple visits also needed to break the cloud-top vs metallicity degeneracy.
- **Confirm sub-solar C/O** via dedicated MIRI/LRS observations to access the 7–10 μm SO₂ and water bands.
- **Mass-loss diagnosis**: ground-based high-resolution He I follow-up to constrain outflow temperature and break the metallicity vs mass-loss-rate degeneracy.
- **Stellar contamination floor**: methodology development (panchromatic NIRISS + NIRSpec scheduled within one rotation period) needed for robust sub-Neptune transmission spectra around active M-dwarfs.

# Insufficient evidence for DMS and DMDS in the atmosphere of K2-18 b: From a joint analysis of JWST NIRISS, NIRSpec, and MIRI observations

**Authors:** Luque, Piaulet-Ghorayeb, Radica, Xue, Zhang, Bean, Samra, Steinrueck (2025, A&A Letter; arXiv:2505.13407)
**Targets:** [[K2-18b]] · **Instruments:** [[JWST-NIRISS]] SOSS + [[JWST-NIRSpec]] G395H + [[JWST-MIRI]] LRS · **Program:** [[JWST-GO-2722]] (PI: Madhusudhan)

## TL;DR

The first **joint panchromatic (0.6–12 μm) retrieval** of K2-18 b combining NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI under [[JWST-GO-2722]]. Three independent data reductions ([[exoTEDRF]] for all instruments; [[Eureka]] + [[SPARTA]] cross-checks for NIRSpec/MIRI; original [[JExoRES]] from M23/M25) feed two retrieval frameworks ([[PLATON]] and [[SCARLET]]) testing baseline (CH₄/CO₂/H₂O/NH₃/HCN/CO), +DMS/DMDS, +C₂H₆, and combined-molecule cases. **Result:** including DMS/DMDS over baseline yields Δln Z ranging −0.3 to +2.8 across data reductions — **never reaching the Δln Z ≥ 5 threshold for a detection claim**. Ethane (C₂H₆), with a similar C–H stretching mode at 3.4 μm and H–C–H bending mode at 6.9 μm, fits the data equally well or better. The MIRI-only photospheric temperature inferred by [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] (T_p ≈ 422 K) is inconsistent with the panchromatic-retrieved value (~175–250 K) and with the zero-albedo equilibrium temperature (~380 K). **The "MIRI features" are too large to be planetary in origin** under self-consistent scale-height energetics. Approximately **26 MIRI transits** would be needed for a 3σ rejection of a flat line vs the DMS/DMDS-favored model — with only ~4 observable transits per year.

## Key findings

- **DMS/DMDS not detected** in panchromatic JExoRES retrieval (Δln Z = −0.3 vs baseline). In exoTEDRF and exoTEDRF+SPARTA cross-reductions, DMS/DMDS gives Δln Z = 2.3–2.8 (weakly–moderately favored) — driven by small reduction-dependent shifts at 7 μm (DMS preferred for exoTEDRF+SPARTA) and 10 μm (DMDS preferred for exoTEDRF-only). **None reach Δln Z ≥ 5.**
- **Ethane is an equally good explanation.** C₂H₆ shares the methyl-group C–H stretching (3.4 μm) and H–C–H bending (6.9 μm) absorptions with [[DMS]] and [[DMDS]]. Across JExoRES and exoTEDRF data, +C₂H₆ retrievals slightly favor ethane over the baseline (Δln Z = 0.7 for JExoRES; 0.4 for exoTEDRF) — comparable to DMS/DMDS preferences. **Spectroscopically the molecule cannot be identified uniquely** at this S/N.
- **[[CH4]] robustly detected** at high abundance across all four reductions (log VMR ≈ −1.6 to −0.9 in SCARLET; −0.85 to −0.94 in PLATON). Median ~1 dex higher than the "two-offsets" case of Madhusudhan 2023 (not ingested).
- **[[CO2]] reduction-dependent.** SPARTA + JExoRES yield two-sided posteriors (log VMR ≈ −1.66 to −2.13); exoTEDRF yields broader posteriors. Consistent within uncertainties with previous estimates.
- **No evidence for H₂O, NH₃, HCN, CO.** All upper limits; ranges consistent with [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] and [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]].
- **MIRI features implausibly large for a planetary signal.** The retrieved photospheric temperature T_p = 175⁺⁵⁰₋₄₀ K from the panchromatic spectrum is 2σ-consistent with T_eq ≈ 255 K, but the M25 MIRI-only T_p = 422⁺¹⁴¹₋₁₃₃ K is required to match the *amplitude* of the MIRI features under self-consistent scale-height physics. **Energy balance forbids T_p > T_eff√(R⋆/a) = 380 K** for a zero-albedo atmosphere. The MIRI features therefore cannot be planetary at face value; they may reflect uncorrected systematics, incomplete line lists, or genuine but smaller signals.
- **TLSe retrievals are degenerate with no-TLSe.** Including the [[stellar-contamination]] transit light source effect (TLSe) does not change the molecular abundance posteriors — confirming the [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]] finding.
- **~26 more MIRI transits needed.** Simulations using the best-fit DMS/DMDS PLATON model as ground truth show that ~26 stacked MIRI transits (σ = 13) would reach 3σ rejection of a flat-line model. With ~4 observable transits per year, this is **not** an achievable program in the present cycle.
- **DMS cross-sections are pressure-limited.** No DMS/DMDS line lists below 1 bar exist; the Sharpe et al. 2004 (HITRAN) cross-sections at 278 K / 1 bar are extrapolated. K2-18 b's photosphere is at ~1 mbar — **the retrievals likely overestimate DMS absorption and underestimate its retrieved abundance.** Conversely, ethane's HITRAN line list is incomplete — likely underestimating its absorption and overestimating its abundance.

## Methods

Three independent pipelines reduce the same [[JWST-GO-2722]] dataset:
- [[JExoRES]] — as published in Madhusudhan et al. 2023 (NIRISS+NIRSpec; not ingested) and [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] (MIRI).
- [[exoTEDRF]] v2.3.0 — the **first publicly available pipeline supporting NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI in one framework** (introduced in this work). NIRISS+NIRSpec reductions from [[2025_Schmidt_K2-18b]]; MIRI reduction newly described (Stage 1 ramp fitting with DO_NOT_USE flagging of first 5 + last groups, EMI correction, time-domain 7σ cosmic-ray flagging, PCA detrending of detector drift in Stage 2, 6-pixel box extraction). MIRI light-curve fit with exoUPRF + batman.
- [[Eureka]] + [[SPARTA]] — independent MIRI/NIRSpec reductions matching the JExoRES choices in M25; SPARTA implements an end-to-end pipeline 27× faster than calwebb_detector1.

Two retrieval frameworks:
- [[PLATON]] (Zhang et al. 2019, 2020, 2025) — GPU-accelerated; native-resolution NIRISS/NIRSpec retrieval at R = 100,000 with DACE opacities; CH₄ from HITEMP 2020; C₂H₆ from ExoCross+HITRAN 2020; DMS/DMDS from Sharpe et al. 2004 (1 bar, 278 K). Isothermal + opaque cloud deck + TLSe (starspot temperature + filling factor). [[MultiNest]] (1000 live points, Importance Nested Sampling).
- [[SCARLET]] (Benneke & Seager 2012, 2013; Roy 2023; Piaulet-Ghorayeb 2024) — free-chemistry retrievals on independent reductions. Multi-ellipsoid nested sampling ([[nestle]]). Three instrument offsets between detector segments.

Bayesian model comparison via Δln Z (Trotta 2008 thresholds: <1 indistinguishable, 1–2.5 weak, 2.5–5 moderate, ≥5 strong — never reached for DMS/DMDS).

## Caveats & limitations

- **Baseline model is intentionally restricted.** Excludes propyne, diethyl sulfide, methacrylonitrile, and other methyl-bearing molecules explored in [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]] and Welbanks, Nixon et al. 2025 (not ingested). The Δln Z 2.3–2.8 preferences for DMS/DMDS may be inflated by this restriction — broadening the model space dilutes the preference further.
- **DMS/DMDS line lists are pressure-extrapolated.** No theoretical or experimental cross-sections exist below 1 bar; the assumed 1-bar Sharpe et al. 2004 cross-sections will be pressure-broadened relative to the true ~1 mbar photospheric environment — overestimating absorption and biasing abundance low.
- **C₂H₆ line list is incomplete.** HITRAN 2020 covers limited bands; absorption may be underestimated and retrieved abundance overestimated — but the *Bayes factor* preference for C₂H₆ vs DMS/DMDS is robust to this asymmetric bias (both directions favor "alternative hydrocarbon").
- **Single transit per instrument.** All conclusions rest on three single visits. With ~26 MIRI transits required for a 3σ detection of features over a flat line, current data are at the limit of what JWST can deliver in the M-dwarf habitable zone.
- **No exhaustive molecular search.** This paper tests ethane as the most chemically expected alternative; the broader systematic search is in [[2025_PicaCiamarra_K2-18b]].

## Open questions / follow-ups

- **What's the photochemical inventory of hydrocarbons at K2-18 b's pressures?** Wogan et al. 2024, Huang et al. 2024, and Welbanks 2025 (none ingested) all predict ethane is the dominant hydrocarbon byproduct of CH₄ photolysis. A predictive abundance from a self-consistent photochemical model + comparison to the retrieved 95% upper limit (log X(C₂H₆) < −1.05 in PLATON; unconstrained in SCARLET) would close the loop.
- **Are the MIRI features systematics or astrophysical?** The 422 K T_p retrieved by [[2025_Madhusudhan_K2-18b-MIRI]] is physically inconsistent with the planetary spectrum. Independent data reductions agree on the spectrum shape (Fig. 1) but disagree by ~5σ in individual MIRI bins. A multi-visit MIRI stack (or HWO-class follow-up) is the only firm test.
- **Does the DMS issue generalize?** The combination of "low-pressure cross-sections missing" + "structural degeneracy with photochemically expected hydrocarbons" is a generic problem for sulfur-organic biosignatures in temperate sub-Neptune atmospheres. The K2-18 b debate becomes the methodology benchmark.

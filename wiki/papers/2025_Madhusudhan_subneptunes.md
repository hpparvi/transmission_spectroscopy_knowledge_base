# Exploring the Sub-Neptune Frontier with JWST

**Authors:** Madhusudhan, Holmberg, Constantinou, Cooke (2025, PNAS, 24 Sept 2025; arXiv:2509.19247)
**Type:** **Review / perspective article**, not a single-target observation paper.
**Targets discussed:** [[K2-18b]], [[TOI-270d]], plus comparator references to GJ 9827 d, GJ 1214 b (not yet ingested) (referenced via [[2025_Gressier_HATP26b]] population), [[LHS1140c]] / [[LHS1140]] system, [[L98-59d]], GJ 3470 b, 55 Cnc e (last three not yet ingested) · **Instruments:** [[JWST-NIRSpec]], [[JWST-NIRISS]], [[JWST-MIRI]], [[JWST-NIRCam]]

## TL;DR

A community-survey perspective on the JWST-era characterization of sub-Neptunes — planets between Earth and Neptune in size with no solar-system analog. Reviews chemical detections on the most-studied targets (K2-18 b, TOI-270 d, GJ 9827 d, plus the warm exo-Neptune GJ 3470 b as size comparator), then proposes a **two-axis classification scheme** for volatile-rich sub-Neptunes — equilibrium temperature × atmospheric H₂ content — yielding six categories: **ice worlds, hycean worlds, mini-Neptunes, gas dwarfs, supercritical mini-Neptunes, and steam worlds**. The classification framework introduces a critical equilibrium temperature T_crit (estimated ≳ 350 K from current data) above which cold-trap H₂O condensation no longer operates and atmospheres transition from H₂-dominated with possible liquid surfaces (hycean) to H₂O-dominated (steam-world) regimes. Argues that JWST observations are starting to populate the parameter space at the level needed for a generalized classification of volatile-rich low-mass exoplanets.

## Key contributions

### 1. Observational survey of well-characterized JWST sub-Neptunes

- **[[K2-18b]]** (T_eq ≈ 258 K, R = 2.61 R⊕, M = 8.63 M⊕) — CH₄ at log VMR ≈ −1.74⁺⁰·⁵⁹₋₀·⁶⁹, CO₂ at log ≈ −2.09⁺⁰·⁵¹₋₀·⁹⁴ (Madhusudhan et al. 2023; not ingested); H₂O upper limit −3.06; tentative DMS (their position; contested per [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]]).
- **[[TOI-270d]]** (T_eq ≈ 352 K) — CH₄ at log ≈ −2.72⁺⁰·⁴¹₋₀·⁵⁰ (Holmberg & Madhusudhan 2024) or log ≈ −1.64⁺⁰·³⁸₋₀·³⁶ (Mikal-Evans/Benneke 2024); CO₂ similarly two readings; CS₂ at log ≈ −3.07⁺⁰·⁷⁴₋₀·⁹¹ (Holmberg) or −3.44⁺¹·⁰⁹₋₀·⁶⁷ (Mikal-Evans). H₂O detected; NH₃ and CO non-detected.
- **GJ 9827 d** (T_eq ≈ 600 K) — H₂O dominated atmosphere at log ≈ −0.5 (lower limit on volume mixing ratio > 31.6%); steam-world classification; Piaulet-Ghorayeb et al. 2024 (not ingested).
- **GJ 3470 b** (warm exo-Neptune; T_eq ≈ 634 K) — H₂O, CH₄, CO₂, SO₂ at high significance (Beatty et al. 2024; not ingested) plus tentative CO; size comparator at the sub-Neptune / Neptune boundary.
- Several other sub-Neptunes briefly discussed: GJ 1214 b (not yet ingested) (cloudy); [[LHS1140c]] (referenced via wiki, but the discussion centers on LHS 1140 b not yet ingested); [[L98-59d]] (sulfur-rich; cited via Banerjee 2024, Gressier 2024).

### 2. Classification scheme: six volatile-rich sub-Neptune types

The two-axis space of **T_eq** vs **H₂ atmospheric extent**:

- **Ice worlds (cold + thin H₂):** H₂O frozen in deep ice layer; thin H₂-dominated atmosphere atop. Solar-system Neptune analog, but at much smaller mass.
- **Hycean worlds (warm + intermediate H₂):** Liquid-water surface (or "dark hycean" — liquid only on the night side) under H₂-rich atmosphere. T_eq ≲ T_crit (~350 K). Habitable zone candidates.
- **Mini-Neptunes (warm + thick H₂):** H₂-dominated atmosphere over a thick water-ice envelope; no liquid water.
- **Gas dwarfs (warm + H₂ over rock):** H₂-rich atmosphere over a rocky core; little or no water.
- **Supercritical mini-Neptunes (hot + intermediate H₂):** Above T_crit; mixed H₂-H₂O envelope where H₂ dissolves into supercritical H₂O.
- **Steam worlds (hot + H₂O-dominated):** Atmosphere dominated by H₂O vapor; little or no H₂ envelope above. T_eq > T_crit.

### 3. T_crit empirical estimate

Critical equilibrium temperature for cold-trap operation **T_crit ≳ 350 K** based on:
- TOI-270 d (T_eq = 352 K): mid-amount H₂O between cold-trap-active K2-18 b and cold-trap-inactive GJ 9827 d.
- The exact T_crit depends on host star spectrum, atmospheric metallicity, and pressure profile; a global value remains theoretically uncertain.

### 4. Mass-metallicity relation in sub-Neptunes

- Solar-system trend: CH₄ VMR rises with decreasing planet mass. Earth/Mars/Venus → Jupiter/Saturn → Uranus/Neptune.
- Sub-Neptune extrapolation: the K2-18 b and TOI-270 d carbon abundances are higher than the trend extrapolation would predict.
- Argues for a population-level mass-metallicity test as JWST sample grows.

### 5. Biosignatures on hycean candidates

Reviews potential biosignature gases for sub-Neptune atmospheres (motivated by the K2-18 b DMS debate):
- **DMS, CS₂, OCS, CH₃Cl, N₂O** — secondary molecules abundant on early Earth that may be detectable on hycean worlds at JWST precision.
- O₂ and O₃ unlikely to be abundant in H₂-rich atmospheres.
- CH₄ has abiotic false positives.
- Concludes that DMS and similar molecules represent the most promising sub-Neptune biosignature targets, **but require multi-instrument confirmation**.

## Caveats & limitations

- **Single-paper-derived classifications are fragile.** The K2-18 b DMS detection is contested; the TOI-270 d MMW is split between two readings; many "single visit, single retrieval" results dominate the present sample.
- **T_crit is an empirical estimate.** With only ~3 well-characterized warm sub-Neptunes, the precise T_crit value (350 K? 400 K? 500 K?) is poorly constrained.
- **Mass-metallicity trend** built on 2–3 carbon-detection points only.
- **Hycean criterion = atmosphere + albedo + bulk parameters.** All three must align for a target to qualify, but each has individual uncertainty.
- **Biosignature candidate list is theoretical.** No firm sub-Neptune biosignature detection in this wiki to date; DMS is the closest and is currently retracted by independent reanalyses.

## Open questions / follow-ups

- **What is T_crit precisely?** Multiple sub-Neptunes between T_eq = 300–500 K with measured H₂O abundance would refine.
- **Population-level mass-metallicity relation.** With current data the trend is uncertain; needs ~10+ characterized sub-Neptunes.
- **JWST observational survey of cooler M-dwarf hycean candidates** (e.g., LHS 1140 b, GJ 12 b, GJ 1132 system) would populate the lower-T_eq end.
- **Habitability assessment requires atmosphere + albedo + bulk-density triangulation** — none of these is currently constrained to better than ~30–50% for any wiki target except K2-18 b.
- **Are biosignature gases like DMS, CS₂ detectable in practice at sub-Neptune precision and DMS opacity-database limitations?** [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] tests CS₂ and finds it only as one of four indistinguishable degeneracies.

## Note on framing

This is the lead author's perspective paper. As of the wiki's current state, several claims (DMS detection on K2-18 b; specific MMW for TOI-270 d) are **contested** by [[2025_FernandezRodriguez_K2-18b]] and [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] respectively. The classification scheme remains useful as an organizing framework regardless of whether specific targets are correctly placed within it.

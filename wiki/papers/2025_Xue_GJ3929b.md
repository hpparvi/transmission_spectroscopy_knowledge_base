# The JWST Rocky Worlds DDT Program Reveals GJ 3929 b to Likely Be a Bare Rock

**Authors:** Xue, Q., Zhang, M., Park Coy, B., Brady, M., Ji, X., Bean, J. L., et al. (2025, ApJL 995:L52)
**Targets:** [[GJ3929b]] · **Instrument:** [[JWST-MIRI]] (F1500W photometric imaging, 15 μm) · **Program:** [[JWST-DD-9235]]

## TL;DR
Two MIRI F1500W (15 μm) **photometric** secondary eclipses of the near-Earth-sized planet GJ 3929 b yield a combined eclipse depth of 160 ± 26 ppm (6σ), corresponding to a dayside temperature T_day = 782 ± 79 K (SPHINX stellar models) and a temperature ratio R = 1.07 ± 0.10 — consistent with a bare rock. CO₂-dominated atmospheres thicker than ~100 mbar are ruled out at >3σ. New MAROON-X RVs refine the planet mass by 3.5× (1.13 ± 0.09 M⊕) and reveal two additional planet candidates in the system. Note: this paper uses MIRI **photometric imaging** with the F1500W filter (a single 15 μm broadband channel), not MIRI/LRS slitless spectroscopy.

## Key findings
- Eclipse depths: Visit 1 = 177 +47/−45 ppm (4σ), Visit 2 = 143 +34/−35 ppm (3σ), joint = **160 ± 26 ppm (6σ)**.
- Dayside temperature from SPHINX-model stellar calibration: **T_day = 782 ± 79 K**; from observed flux directly: 745 ± 76 K.
- Bare-rock limit: **T_max = 737 ± 14 K**. Temperature ratio **R = 1.07 ± 0.10** (SPHINX) → consistent with bare rock.
- **CO₂-rich atmospheres > 100 mbar ruled out at >3σ**; 50 mbar pure CO₂ ruled out at 3.3σ.
- Earth-like and Venus-like atmospheres disfavored.
- New MAROON-X radial velocities (this paper): planet mass refined from ~3σ to 1.13 ± 0.09 M⊕ — a 3.5× improvement. New planet candidates: GJ 3929 c (P = 14.994 days, confirmed Keplerian signal) and GJ 3929 d (P = 6.116 days, newly significant but unconfirmed).
- Combined with known radius 1.09 ± 0.04 R⊕, planet bulk density is consistent with a rocky (Earth-like) composition.

## Methods
Two secondary eclipses observed 2025 July 8 and July 31 with MIRI F1500W (photometric time-series, 15 μm single broadband channel). Reduction with [[SPARTA]] (updated for MIRI imaging mode). Thermal emission forward models with [[HELIOS]] 3.0 for bare-rock, CO₂-dominated, and analogue scenarios. Light-curve and RV fitting with [[juliet]]. Stellar atmospheric models from [[SPHINX]] (Iyer 2023). Interior composition not constrained here (smint modeling prose-only). MAROON-X spectrograph (not yet ingested) provides new radial velocities.

## Caveats & limitations
- Single photometric channel (15 μm) provides one temperature point, not a spectrum; cannot distinguish absorption features at specific wavelengths.
- T_day uncertainty dominated by stellar model calibration (SPHINX), not eclipse measurement precision.
- GJ 3929 d candidate not yet confirmed; planet c's non-transiting geometry limits characterization.
- The [[JWST-MIRI]] F1500W mode is a photometric imager, not the LRS slitless spectrograph used in other secondary-eclipse papers in this wiki (e.g., [[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]], [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]], [[2024_Wachiraphan_LTT1445Ab]]).

## Open questions / follow-ups
- Can MIRI/LRS follow-up spectrally resolve the emission from GJ 3929 b and test for specific atmospheric features?
- Does the ~3σ improvement in mass precision with MAROON-X clarify the bulk composition ambiguity between Earth-like and higher-volatile compositions?
- Will GJ 3929 c or d be accessible to atmospheric characterization?

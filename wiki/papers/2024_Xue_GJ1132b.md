# JWST Thermal Emission of the Terrestrial Exoplanet GJ 1132 b

**Authors:** Xue, Bean, Zhang, Mahajan, Ih, Eastman, Lunine, Weiner Mansfield, Coy, Kempton, Koll, Kite (2024, ApJL 973:L8) · [doi:10.3847/2041-8213/ad72e9]
**Target:** [[GJ1132b]] · **Host:** [[GJ1132]] · **Instrument:** [[JWST-MIRI]] (LRS, 5–12 μm)
**Program:** [[JWST-GTO-1274]]

## TL;DR
A single MIRI/LRS secondary eclipse of the rocky super-Earth GJ 1132 b yields a **featureless thermal emission spectrum** from 5–12 μm. The white-light eclipse depth is **140 ± 17 ppm**, corresponding to a dayside temperature **T_day = 709 ± 31 K** and temperature ratio **R = T_day / T_max = 0.95 ± 0.04**. The emission spectrum is consistent with a blackbody and incompatible with any Earth-like, Venus-like, or thick CO₂ atmosphere. Combined with the transmission non-detection from [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]], the conclusion is that **GJ 1132 b is a bare rock**.

## Key findings
- White-light secondary-eclipse depth: **140 ± 17 ppm**; **T_day = 709 ± 31 K** (zero-albedo bare-rock T_max = 746 K).
- **R = 0.95 ± 0.04** — consistent with an airless body with negligible heat redistribution.
- Featureless 5–12 μm emission spectrum; no CO₂, H₂O, or silicate emission features.
- **Earth-like atmosphere (1 bar, ≥1% H₂O) excluded**; **Venus-like atmosphere (P > ~100 bar, ≥1 ppm CO₂ or H₂O) excluded**.
- Pure CO₂ atmosphere excluded above 0.006 bar; pure H₂O atmosphere excluded above 0.16 bar.
- Two independent reductions ([[SPARTA]] primary, [[Eureka]] cross-check) consistent.
- [[HELIOS]] forward models for bare-rock, CO₂, H₂O, and Earth/Venus-analogue scenarios all fail to fit the data better than a blackbody.

## Methods
- **Observations:** single MIRI/LRS secondary eclipse on 2023-07-01; 5–12 μm; ~5 hr observation under JWST Cycle 1 GTO program [[JWST-GTO-1274]] (PI: Lunine).
- **Reduction:** [[SPARTA]] (primary) + [[Eureka]] (cross-check).
- **System parameters:** from [[EXOFASTv2]] (Eastman et al. 2019; not yet ingested as standalone paper).
- **Forward modeling:** [[HELIOS]] (Malik et al. 2017, 2019; not yet ingested) computes 1D emission spectra and dayside temperature structures for a grid of atmospheric scenarios. Comparison anchored to the Koll (2022) bare-rock T_max formula.
- **Exclusion criterion:** atmospheric models predicting T_day significantly below 709 K or spectral features inconsistent with the observed spectrum at >2σ are excluded.

## Caveats & limitations
- Single secondary eclipse; two-epoch confirmation (as in [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]]) not yet done for this target.
- A thin, low-pressure CO₂ atmosphere (< 0.006 bar) is formally consistent with the emission data.
- The 5–12 μm range only clips the edge of the 15 μm CO₂ band; a longer-wavelength MIRI observation would improve CO₂ constraints.

## Open questions / follow-ups
- Is a thin volcanic outgassing atmosphere (< 0.006 bar CO₂) consistent with the combined transmission + emission + stellar-age constraints?
- Can a joint analysis of the Bennett et al. transmission spectrum + this emission spectrum close the remaining thin-steam atmosphere window below 0.16 bar?

## Tensions
- **vs. thin-steam window ([[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]):** Bennett et al. note a thin H₂O steam atmosphere (< 10 mbar) cannot be ruled out by transmission alone. This emission measurement extends the H₂O exclusion to pressures > 0.16 bar, consistent with bare-rock but not independently closing the sub-0.16-bar window.

# A Detailed Investigation of HD 209458 b HST and JWST Transmission Spectra with SANSAR

**Authors:** Verma, Goyal, Avarsekar, Shukla 2025, AJ 170:69 · [arXiv:2507.19820]
**Targets:** [[HD-209458b]] · **Instruments:** [[HST-STIS]], [[HST-WFC3]], [[JWST-NIRCam]]

## TL;DR
Introduces **SANSAR** (Suite of Adaptable plaNetary atmoSphere model And Retrieval), a Python-based atmospheric forward and retrieval framework supporting opacity-sampling and correlated-k radiative transfer, free / equilibrium / self-consistent grid retrievals, [[FastChem]] / PyFastChem coupling for chemistry, and [[MultiNest]]/PyMultiNest for nested sampling. Benchmarked against [[POSEIDON]], [[ATMO]], [[CHIMERA]], and [[Aurora]] on WASP-96 b for forward-model and retrieval consistency (within 1σ). Then applied to [[HD-209458b]] across three instrument combinations: (i) [[JWST-NIRCam]] only (Xue et al. 2024 reduction; not ingested), (ii) [[HST-WFC3]] + NIRCam, (iii) [[HST-STIS]] + WFC3 + NIRCam (full 0.3–5 μm). The full STIS+WFC3+NIRCam free-chemistry retrieval gives [[H2O]] log VMR = −4.53 (12.6σ), [[CO2]] = −7.52 (7.8σ), [[Na]] = −5.67 (6.9σ), [[K]] = −6.93 (4.9σ); equilibrium-chemistry gives metallicity ~0.8–2.1× solar and C/O ≈ 0.54 (consistent with stellar). Demonstrates that **NIRCam-only retrievals overestimate H₂O and CO₂ abundances by ~3 orders of magnitude** relative to STIS+WFC3+NIRCam — the optical baseline anchors temperature and cloud properties, breaking the radius–abundance–cloud degeneracy.

## Key findings
- **SANSAR retrieval framework** introduced: opacity sampling at R~20,000 + correlated-k at R~1000 (using Exo_k k-tables) both validated against POSEIDON (~6 ppm mean offset), ATMO (~10 ppm), CHIMERA (~4 ppm), and Aurora across WASP-96 b benchmarks. Bicubic interpolation on a 20×40 (T, P) opacity grid; supports Madhusudhan-Seager and Guillot P-T parameterizations; isothermal P-T also available. PyMultiNest at 2000 live points + tolerance 0.5.
- **Wavelength-coverage matters more than model choice.** With HD 209458 b free-chemistry retrieval:
  - NIRCam-only: log H₂O = −1.62⁺⁰·³⁹⁻¹·²⁹, log CO₂ = −5.31⁺⁰·³⁷⁻¹·¹² — supersolar and weakly constrained
  - WFC3 + NIRCam: log H₂O = −3.02⁺⁰·⁸⁶⁻¹·¹⁷, log CO₂ = −6.08⁺⁰·⁸⁵⁻¹·¹⁰ — improved
  - STIS + WFC3 + NIRCam: log H₂O = **−4.53⁺⁰·³²⁻⁰·²⁹**, log CO₂ = **−7.52⁺⁰·³⁰⁻⁰·²⁶** — converged near solar
  - The 3 dex shift between NIRCam-only and full coverage is interpretable as overfitting in the NIRCam-only data: the strong CO₂ feature alone, without optical Na/K and slope constraints, drives unphysically high H₂O and CO₂.
- **NIRCam-only equilibrium-chemistry constrains supersolar metallicity** (2.2–10.7× solar) when C/H is varied to control C/O. **STIS+WFC3+NIRCam constrains solar/subsolar metallicity** (0.8–2.1× solar).
- **C/O = 0.54⁺⁰·⁰⁹⁻⁰·⁰⁹** (equilibrium chemistry) and **0.46⁺⁰·⁰⁹⁻⁰·⁰⁹** (grid retrieval) with the full dataset; consistent within 1σ with the host-star C/O = 0.42 ± 0.08 (Polanski 2022; not ingested).
- **No detection of CO, NH₃, C₂H₂, CH₄, H₂S, HCN** (3σ upper limits; rejects MacDonald & Madhusudhan 2017's NH₃ tentative claim and Giacobbe et al. 2021's CH₄/HCN/NH₃/C₂H₂/CO ground-based claims). 
- **Reduced χ² lower for NIRCam-only retrievals** (0.57 free, < 0.6 equilibrium, < 0.6 grid) than for STIS+WFC3+NIRCam (1.21 free, 1.27 eq, 1.30 grid) — a sign of overfitting in the narrow-band data.
- **Implication for cooler instrument modes:** NIRSpec G395H and G235H observations alone may overestimate molecular abundances by similar magnitudes; UV/optical baseline is essential for robust retrievals on hot Jupiters.

## Methods
SANSAR atmospheric model: 100 layers, hydrostatic equilibrium, H₂+He bulk gas (84%/15%); 12 chemical species considered (H₂O, CO, CO₂, Na, K, CH₄, NH₃, C₂H₂, H₂S, HCN, FeH, etc.); CIA from H₂–H₂ and H₂–He; 2D MacDonald & Madhusudhan 2017 cloud + Rayleigh-haze parameterization. Opacity database from Goyal et al. 2018, 2020 ([[ATMO]] line list); LBL precomputed at 0.001 cm⁻¹. Three retrieval flavors:
1. **Free-chemistry** — 18 parameters; NIRCam-only / WFC3+NIRCam / STIS+WFC3+NIRCam; Madhusudhan-Seager 3-region P-T; isothermal also tested.
2. **Equilibrium chemistry** — 9 parameters; C/O and [M/H] free; PyFastChem solves on-the-fly; varying C/H or O/H tested separately.
3. **Self-consistent grid** — 2.4-million-spectrum precomputed RCTE grid with chemistry, interpolated via RegularGridInterpolator + RBFInterpolator; 4 free parameters (rcf, [M/H], C/O, T_int).

## Caveats & limitations
- 1D, plane-parallel, no terminator inhomogeneity (could underestimate cloud-fraction effects highlighted by [[2025_Fu_limb-asymmetry]] for hot Jupiters).
- No disequilibrium chemistry (vertical mixing, photochemistry); excluded by SANSAR's current chemistry module.
- 3D effects (limb asymmetry, day-night gradients) explicitly not modeled — would require future 2D extension.
- Stellar contamination not modeled; HD 209458 is quiescent so this is a less critical limitation than for active hosts.
- NIRSpec G395M/H is hypothesized to behave similarly to NIRCam (overestimate without optical baseline), but not directly tested with HD 209458 b data.
- Retrieved offset between HST and JWST observations is large (~205 ppm) for free retrieval but small (~39 ppm) for equilibrium retrieval; the physical origin is unclear and treated as a fitting parameter.

## Open questions / follow-ups
- Does the same wavelength-coverage bias apply to NIRSpec G395H or G235H observations of cooler/lower-S/N planets? Direct test on COMPASS NIRSpec G395H targets (e.g., [[TOI-836b]]) would be informative.
- 2D atmosphere extension of SANSAR — the framework already accounts for patchy clouds; full terminator inhomogeneity would extend it to limb-asymmetry retrievals.
- How robust is the C/O = 0.54 ± 0.09 result to disequilibrium chemistry? Drummond et al. 2016 (not ingested) showed quenching can shift inferred C/O by ~0.1 dex.

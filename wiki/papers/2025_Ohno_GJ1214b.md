# A Possible Metal-dominated Atmosphere below the Thick Aerosols of GJ 1214 b Suggested by Its JWST Panchromatic Transmission Spectrum

**Authors:** Ohno, Schlawin, Bell, Murphy, Beatty, Welbanks, Greene, Fortney, Parmentier, Edelman, Mehta, Rieke (2025, ApJL 979:L7) · [arXiv:2412.02681]
**Targets:** [[GJ1214b]] · **Instruments:** [[HST-WFC3]], [[JWST-NIRSpec]] (G395H), [[JWST-MIRI]] (LRS)

## TL;DR
Companion study to Schlawin et al. 2024a (not ingested) on the same JWST/NIRSpec G395H + HST/WFC3 + JWST/MIRI/LRS panchromatic transmission spectrum of the archetype thick-aerosol sub-Neptune **[[GJ1214b]]** (R_p = 2.73 R_⊕, M_p = 8.17 M_⊕). Constructs a **photochemical-microphysical (PM) haze grid** coupling EGP (radiative-convective equilibrium) + [[VULCAN]] (photochemistry) + Ohno-Okuzumi/Ohno-Kawashima two-moment haze microphysics + [[CHIMERA]] radiative transfer, retrieved with PyMultiNest over 2184 haze profiles. The 1.1–12 μm panchromatic spectrum is **well explained by an extremely high metallicity [M/H] ≈ 3.5–3.8** (i.e. ~3000–6000× solar) with **column-integrated haze production rate F_haze ≈ 10⁻⁸–10⁻⁷ g cm⁻² s⁻¹**. The inference is robust across four tested haze refractive indices (water-rich tholin, Titan tholin, CG22 tholin, hydrogenated diamond) and against a chemically-consistent retrieval cross-check ([M/H] = 3.48⁺⁰·⁴²₋₀·³⁶). The conclusion is striking: GJ 1214 b likely possesses a **metal-dominated atmosphere where H₂ is no longer the main constituent** — joining a growing class of extremely metal-rich super-Earth / sub-Neptune atmospheres alongside [[L98-59d]], GJ 9827 d, and LTT 9779b.

## Key findings

- **Extreme metallicity inference is robust.** Six retrieval configurations (water-rich tholin / Titan tholin / CG22 tholin C/O=1.0 / hydrogenated diamond / photolysis-based F_haze / chemically-consistent) all converge on **[M/H] ≈ 3.48–3.77** (median values), with χ²_ν = 1.28–1.53 for 88–89 degrees of freedom. The metallicity result is **agnostic** to assumed haze optical constants and F_haze prescription.
- **[[CO2]] is the dominant spectral discriminant in NIRSpec.** The 4.3 μm CO₂ feature is the largest unmasked-by-haze molecular feature in the panchromatic spectrum. Its strength relative to the ~3 μm haze feature and the 3.3 μm CH₄ band drives the metallicity inference upward — Hu & Seager 2014 (not ingested) predicted abundant CO₂ only under hydrogen-depleted conditions for GJ 1214 b.
- **CH₄ and CO₂ relative strengths constrain disequilibrium / chemistry.** Schlawin 2024a (not ingested) reports CO₂ at 2.4σ and CH₄ at 2.0σ on the same NIRSpec data. The PM haze grid spectra at low metallicity [M/H] ≤ 2.0 over-predict the 3.2 μm CH₄ and 4.3 μm CO₂ relative depths.
- **The MIRI/LRS flatness sets a small scale height — independent metallicity evidence.** Kempton et al. 2023 (not ingested) MIRI phase curve flatness, jointly with the transmission spectrum, requires [M/H] ≥ 3.0 to suppress the prominent ~6 μm C–H / C–N organic haze feature that would otherwise emerge at moderate metallicities.
- **C/O is degenerate with the haze-production prescription.**
  - Free F_haze: **C/O = 0.43⁺⁰·³⁶₋₀·¹⁰** (sub-solar).
  - Photolysis-based F_haze (rate = sum of photolysis rates of CH₄+C₂H₂+C₆H₆+HCN+CO+CO₂+OCS+CS+CS₂): **C/O = 1.02⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀·⁰⁷** (super-solar; sulfur-photolysis-driven haze regime).
  - Higher metallicity transitions haze formation from hydrocarbon/nitrile-based (low-metallicity Titan-like) to CO/CO₂/sulfur-based (high-metallicity oxidized regime).
- **Haze production rate F_haze ≈ 10⁻⁹·⁵ to 10⁻⁷ g cm⁻² s⁻¹.** This is **5–6 orders of magnitude greater** than Solar System haze-producing atmospheres (Titan 3×10⁻¹⁴, Pluto 1.2×10⁻¹⁴, Triton 2–8×10⁻¹⁵ g cm⁻² s⁻¹). Even with all photolytic-loss budgets pooled (CH₄+C₂H₂+C₆H₆+HCN+CO+CO₂+OCS+CS+CS₂), the calculated photolysis rate (~10⁻¹⁰ g cm⁻² s⁻¹) **falls 2 dex short of the retrieved F_haze**, suggesting either an undiscovered haze-formation channel or that the photolysis budget is an under-estimate.
- **Hydrogenated-diamond aerosol is statistically preferred** (χ²_ν = 1.28; lowest among the tested refractive indices) thanks to a narrow C–H stretching bump at 3.53 μm that matches the data. Note: not unique — other refractive indices are also consistent.
- **Robust against chemically-consistent retrieval.** Re-running with CHIMERA's chemically-consistent retrieval framework using power-law aerosol (κ_aer ∝ (λ/λ₀)^−α) gives [M/H] = **3.48⁺⁰·⁴²₋₀·³⁶**, in agreement with the PM grid result despite different aerosol parameterization.
- **Implications for the GJ 1214 b interior.** A CO₂-dominated atmosphere is in **tension with the mass–radius relation**, which requires substantial H₂ envelope on a rocky core to match the observed low density. The high atmospheric metallicity could be reconciled via:
  - Planetesimal/pebble accretion (limited to ~10–30× solar; insufficient alone),
  - Post-formation processes: H₂–magma reaction (Kite & Fegley 2020 — not ingested), atmosphere–core dredge-up from giant impacts (Kurosaki & Inutsuka 2023 not ingested),
  - "Ices" in the interior dominated by CO/CO₂ rather than H₂O (McClure 2023 — not ingested) if GJ 1214 b formed beyond CO/CO₂ snow lines.
- **Joins growing class of extremely metal-rich super-Earth/sub-Neptune atmospheres** alongside [[L98-59d]] (Banerjee 2024 not ingested), GJ 9827 d (Piaulet-Ghorayeb 2024; not ingested), LTT 9779 b (Hoyer 2023; not ingested), and [[2025_BelloArufe_L98-59b]].

## Methods

- **Photochemical-microphysical (PM) haze grid.**
  - **TP profiles.** EGP radiative-convective code (Marley & McKay 1999; Fortney 2005; Morley 2013; Marley & Robinson 2015) for [M/H] = 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, 3.5, 4.0 with full heat redistribution and T_int = 40 K. C/O treated separately downstream.
  - **Chemistry.** [[VULCAN]] (Tsai 2017, 2021; SNCHO network — 571 forward reactions + 1142 reversed + 69 species photodissociation) with the MUSCLES survey stellar UV.
  - **Haze microphysics.** Ohno & Okuzumi 2018 + Ohno, Kawashima & Fortney 2020 two-moment haze model — calculates vertical particle number-and-mass density via collision growth + eddy diffusion + gravitational settling. K_zz(P) = K_{zz,1bar} × (P/1 bar)^{−0.4} (motivated by Charnay 2015a GCM).
  - **Grid coverage.** [M/H] ∈ [2.0, 4.0] × C/O ∈ [0.057, 1.457] × K_{zz,1bar} ∈ [10⁵, 10⁹] cm²/s (0.2 dex steps) × F_haze ∈ [10⁻⁷, 10⁻¹²] g cm⁻² s⁻¹. Total 2184 haze profiles.
  - **Transmission spectra.** [[CHIMERA]] (Line & Mai 2019) for radiative transfer; H₂O (POKAZATEL), CO, CO₂ (UCL-4000), NH₃, HCN, H₂S, CH₄, C₂H₂, C₂H₄, C₂H₆, CH₃Cl, CS, SO, SO₂, OCS line lists; H₂-H₂ + H₂-He CIA; H₂/He Rayleigh; PyMieScatt (Sumlin 2018) Mie theory with assumed spherical particles + ρ_p = 1 g cm⁻³.
  - **Retrieval.** Grid-based via PyMultiNest (Buchner 2014); free parameters [M/H], C/O, log F_haze, log K_{zz,1bar}, and per-instrument transit-depth offsets.
- **Cross-checks.**
  - Four haze refractive-index variants tested (water-rich tholin, Titan tholin, CG22 tholin for C/O=1.0, hydrogenated diamond).
  - Photolysis-based F_haze variant: F_haze tied to photolysis rate sum.
  - Chemically-consistent retrieval cross-check (FastChem2 + power-law aerosol opacity).

## Caveats & limitations

- **TP profile doesn't include radiative feedback from haze and chemistry.** Self-consistent climate-photochemistry-haze coupling is left for future studies. The TP grid is therefore an external prior on retrieval.
- **NRS1 vs NRS2 detector offset uncertainty.** The paper notes that the unknown offset between NRS1 and NRS2 detectors prevents conclusive choice between subsolar (C/O = 0.43) vs super-solar (C/O ≈ 1.0) C/O regimes.
- **NIRSpec CO₂ + CH₄ detection significances are modest** at 2.4σ and 2.0σ in Schlawin 2024a — high-precision follow-up needed for definitive metallicity confirmation.
- **Single-particle-radius haze.** PyMieScatt with spherical particles; fractal aggregates (Adams 2019; Ohno & Kawashima 2020) likely affect spectral shape and inferred parameters but are not modeled.
- **F_haze 2-dex larger than the calculated photolysis budget** — implies an unidentified haze-production mechanism, or that haze persists from a different formation channel; this is a real puzzle (the haze production-rate ~10⁻⁸ g cm⁻² s⁻¹ may be 5–6 orders of magnitude above Solar System hazes).
- **Aerosol identity not definitively determined.** Hydrogenated diamond gives lowest χ², but other refractive indices are statistically consistent within 0.2 in χ²_ν.
- **Mass–radius tension with CO₂-dominated atmosphere on a rocky core.** Reconciling requires post-formation enrichment processes (H₂-magma reaction, CO/CO₂ ices in the interior) — i.e. processes beyond standard planetesimal/pebble accretion (which caps at ~10–30× solar).

## Open questions / follow-ups

- **High-precision MIRI follow-up** to confirm the small scale height implied by MIRI flatness and to directly detect the ~6 μm C–H/C–N organic haze feature at expected amplitude ~50 ppm. Authors flag this as the most direct test.
- **Atmospheric C/O.** Resolving the sub-vs-super-solar C/O degeneracy requires better NRS1↔NRS2 offset calibration plus higher-precision NIRSpec follow-up.
- **Direct aerosol-type discrimination.** Different refractive indices produce spectrally distinct features at 3–4 μm and ~6 μm; precise transmission + dayside emission could distinguish water-rich tholin, hydrogenated diamond, KCl clouds (Morley 2013), and Na₂S clouds (Huang 2024 not ingested).
- **Bulk-density / mass–radius reconciliation.** GJ 1214 b under a CO₂-dominated metal-dominated atmosphere needs a re-derivation of interior models that fold in: post-formation enrichment, energy release by metal rainout (Vazan 2024 not ingested), atmospheric opacity slowing thermal evolution (Burrows 2007 not ingested), and radiative feedback from aerosols (Poser & Redmer 2024 not ingested).
- **Lavvas et al. 2024 (not ingested) contemporaneous study** reaches the same conclusion: [M/H] ~2000–3000× solar on GJ 1214 b based on HST + MIRI transmission and emission spectra. Convergent finding via independent methodology.

# A Disintegrating Rocky World Shrouded in Dust and Gas: Mid-infrared Observations of K2-22 b Using JWST

**Authors:** Tusay, N., Wright, J. T., Beatty, T. G., Desch, S., et al. (2025, ApJL 987:L6)
**Targets:** [[K2-22b]] · **Instrument:** [[JWST-MIRI]] (LRS, 4.4–11.8 μm) + [[CHEOPS]] (optical) · **Program:** [[JWST-GO-3315]]

## TL;DR
Four JWST MIRI/LRS transit windows of the [[disintegrating-planet]] K2-22 b were observed in April 2024 (program GO 3315). Transit depth is highly variable — characteristic of the escaping dust/gas tail — and only the fourth observation yields a clear detection (9.7σ in the combined 4.4–11.8 μm light curve; 1627 ± 168 ppm in the 4.4–5.3 μm sub-band). The fourth-transit spectrum shows a significant absorption feature at ~5 μm tentatively attributed to NO gas. Dust mineralogy modeling using Mie scattering theory favors **iron-rich silicate** composition with effective grain size ~1 μm. This is the first mid-infrared characterization of the escaping material from a disintegrating rocky planet, probing the interior composition through its vaporizing products.

## Key findings
- Four transit windows observed: Obs 1 (2024 Apr 24), Obs 2 (Apr 26), Obs 3 (Apr 27 08:15 UTC), Obs 4 (Apr 27 14:21 UTC). The short orbital period (9.1457 hr) permits multiple transits in a few days.
- **Variable transit depths** across all four MIRI observations — consistent with stochastic mass-loss activity of a disintegrating planet. Depths in the 4.4–5.3 μm band: 52 ± 253 ppm (Obs 1), 727 ± 247 ppm (Obs 2), 446 ± 174 ppm (Obs 3), 1627 ± 168 ppm (Obs 4).
- **Obs 4 unambiguous transit: 9.7σ** detection in the combined 4.4–11.8 μm MIRI/LRS light curve; depth 1506 ± 540 ppm broadband.
- **~5 μm absorption feature** in the Obs 4 spectrum: a narrow feature consistent with **[[NO]] (nitrogen monoxide)** at ~5.1 μm. CO₂ considered but provides a poor fit.
- **Iron-rich silicate dust**: Mie scattering fits to the spectral shape favor iron-bearing mineral composition with effective grain size ~1 μm (power-law size distribution, p = 0.875). MgO and SiO predicted by theoretical models but not detected as dominant components.
- CHEOPS simultaneous optical observations: low-significance or non-detections in most epochs; marginally consistent with Obs 4 transit detection.
- The non-detections in Obs 1–3 are physically meaningful: disintegrating planet mass-loss is episodic and stochastic, and null transits are expected.

## Methods
Primary MIRI/LRS reduction with [[Eureka]] (T. J. Bell et al. 2024); independent verification with [[Pegasus]] (T. G. Beatty et al. 2024; not yet ingested). CHEOPS reduction with PIPE photometric extraction and Chexoplanet (Egger et al. 2024; not yet ingested) for coherent variance removal. Dust mineralogy modeled via Mie scattering theory (Mie 1908) using optical constants from van Lieshout et al. 2016 (not yet ingested) and mineral spectra from the JPL ECOSTRESS spectral library. Gas species opacity from the DACE database (Grimm et al. 2021; not yet ingested) at T = 2100 K, P = 10⁻⁸ bar. Model selection via Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC).

## Caveats & limitations
- Only one of four transit windows shows a definitive detection; the non-detections are physically interpretable but limit the per-transit spectral precision.
- The NO identification is tentative: the ~5.1 μm feature is narrow and at the spectral resolution limit of MIRI/LRS (R ≈ 17); alternative absorbers cannot be definitively excluded.
- Long-wavelength MIRI channels (8–11.8 μm) have large uncertainties and are unreliable for spectral feature identification in this dataset.
- Spectral folding contamination at 3.9 μm from the LRS double-prism assembly affects the shortest wavelength channels.
- Physical origin of NO is uncertain: could be direct vaporization of nitrogen-bearing minerals, photodissociation of N₂, or secondary chemistry in the escaping gas.

## Open questions / follow-ups
- Can additional MIRI/LRS transits during high-activity epochs confirm the NO feature and constrain the gas column density?
- What mineral abundances (Fe/Mg ratio) can be derived if higher-SNR spectral observations are obtained?
- Does the episodic activity follow a quasi-periodic pattern, and can it be predicted for scheduling future JWST observations?
- BD+05 4868 Ab (Rappaport et al. 2024; not yet ingested) is a similar but brighter disintegrating-planet system and may be more suitable for follow-up characterization.

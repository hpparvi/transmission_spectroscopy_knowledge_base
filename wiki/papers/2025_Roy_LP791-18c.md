# Diversity in the haziness and chemistry of temperate sub-Neptunes

**Authors:** Roy, Benneke, Fournier-Tondreau, Coulombe, Piaulet-Ghorayeb, Lafrenière, Allart, Cowan, et al. (2025, Nature Astronomy) · DOI: 10.1038/s41550-025-02723-3
**Primary target:** [[LP-791-18c]] · **Host:** [[LP-791-18]] (M6) · **Instrument:** [[JWST-NIRSpec]] (PRISM, 0.7–5.2 μm)
**Program:** [[JWST-GTO-1201]] (NEAT survey)

## TL;DR

JWST/NIRSpec PRISM transmission spectrum of the temperate sub-Neptune [[LP-791-18c]] (T_eq ≈ 355 K) reveals a **strong Rayleigh-scattering haze continuum (5.4σ)** overlaid with [[CH4]] absorption (4.5σ) and **no detectable [[CO2]]** (CH₄/CO₂ > 12 at 2σ). The haze enhancement is 2–3 orders of magnitude greater than on comparable temperate sub-Neptunes [[K2-18b]] and [[TOI-270d]] — the paper's headline framing is that intrinsic atmospheric **diversity** persists even among targets with similar bulk properties and equilibrium temperatures. Inferred metallicity 250–400× solar; bulk inferred to be richer in H₂ than H₂O, hinting at formation inside the water ice line.

## Key findings

- **CH₄ detected at 4.5σ** (2.3 and 3.3 μm bands); log X(CH₄) > −2.89 (2σ).
- **Strong Rayleigh-scattering haze slope at 5.4σ** (log c_Haze = 5.16) — 2–3 orders of magnitude greater enhancement than K2-18 b or TOI-270 d.
- **No CO₂ detection;** log X(CO₂) < −1.97 (2σ); CH₄/CO₂ > 12 at 2σ.
- **Metallicity 250–400× solar** (chemically-consistent retrieval); atmosphere consistent with chemical equilibrium — no disequilibrium signatures required.
- **Bulk composition richer in H₂ than H₂O,** hinting at formation **inside** the water ice line.
- **Hazes likely CH₄-derived tholins** (Titan-like) or sulfur hazes; specific precursor cross-sections (HCN, C₂H₂, C₂H₄, C₂H₆, SO₂, SO) **not detected**.
- **Comparative claims:** [[K2-18b]] (T_eq ≈ 255 K) and [[TOI-270d]] (T_eq ≈ 384 K) show clear CH₄ + CO₂; LP 791-18 c is anomalously hazy by comparison.
- **HST-derived clouds-vs-T_eq trend broken:** 4 of 9 JWST sub-Neptunes inconsistent with the older "low-T sub-Neptunes mostly clear" expectation.
- **Tentative ordering:** at low T_eq the sub-Neptune sample appears to organize by incoming **XUV flux**, not equilibrium temperature — though stellar age uncertainties limit the conclusion.

## Methods

- **Observation:** Single JWST NIRSpec PRISM BOTS transit on 2023 Jun 17, NRSRAPID, SUB512, S1600A1 slit. Detector intentionally saturated 1.0–1.8 μm to boost SNR longward — that wavelength region was rejected from the final analysis.
- **Reduction:** Custom Stage-1 built on the STScI pipeline + [[Eureka]] Stages 2/3, with group-level 1/f background subtraction and a new **nonuniform-illumination-nonlinearity (NIN) correction** (Benneke et al., under review; not yet ingested). Cross-checked with [[exoTEDRF]].
- **Light curves:** [[ExoTEP]] (Benneke et al.; not yet ingested). Spot-crossing event modeled with a Gaussian-spot model and [[spotrod]].
- **Retrievals:** Free-chemistry plus chemically-consistent (metallicity + C/O parameterization) including hazes and Mie clouds; tested out-of-equilibrium H₂S explicitly.
- **Forward modeling:** [[SCARLET]] radiative-convective grid + [[VULCAN]] (K_zz = 10⁴ cm² s⁻¹) for disequilibrium-chemistry exploration; tracks CH₄/CO₂ vs metallicity and Bond albedo.
- **Tested haze precursor opacities:** HCN, C₂H₂, C₂H₄, C₂H₆, SO₂, SO — none required.
- **TLS / unocculted-spot stellar contamination** tested and found not to affect the conclusions.

## Caveats & limitations

- **1.0–1.8 μm rejected** due to saturation systematics (motivated by an analogous WASP-39 b benchmark issue with overlapping NIRISS/SOSS).
- **Single transit only.**
- **Spot-crossing event** in the light curve required modeling; included via [[spotrod]].
- **H₂O abundance unconstrained** (hidden under CH₄ and haze opacities).
- **Haze precursor identification inconclusive** — many candidates compatible with the Rayleigh slope.
- **High-energy-flux trend with haziness** suggestive but uncertain due to stellar age uncertainties.
- **CO₂ detection on K2-18 b** remains contested in the literature (referenced only).

## Open questions / follow-ups

- What sets the wide diversity in haziness across nearly-identical-T_eq sub-Neptunes? Is XUV-driven photochemistry the dominant control?
- Confirm the LP 791-18 c haze interpretation with G395H spectroscopy (resolving CH₄ band shapes) or higher-order resolution.
- Does the K2-18 b CO₂ tension survive Hu et al. (in prep; not yet ingested) reanalysis with GO 2372 data?

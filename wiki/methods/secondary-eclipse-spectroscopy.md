# Secondary-eclipse spectroscopy

Measurement of a transiting planet's thermal-emission spectrum by observing the decrement in total system flux when the planet passes behind its host star (secondary eclipse). The eclipse depth at each wavelength yields the planet-to-star flux ratio F_p(λ)/F_⋆(λ), from which a dayside brightness temperature T_day(λ) can be inferred given a stellar model. For rocky M-dwarf planets, MIRI/LRS (5–12 μm) probes the blackbody peak and places constraints on atmospheric retention via the ratio R = T_day/T_max, where T_max is the zero-albedo, zero-heat-redistribution "bare-rock" limit (Koll 2022). R ≈ 1 indicates a bare rock or tenuous atmosphere; R ≪ 1 implies either high albedo or efficient heat redistribution, typical of thick atmospheres. Complementary to [[transmission-spectroscopy]].

## Papers
- [[2025_Sikora_HD80606b]] — JWST NIRSpec G395H 20.9-hr **partial phase curve** centered on the eclipse + periapse of highly eccentric hot Jupiter HD 80606 b; CH₄/H₂O/CO₂ post-periapse; T_bright 892 → 1313 K → 1077 K; first wiki paper demonstrating phase-resolved spectroscopic eclipse + periapse capture on an eccentric gas giant.
- [[2026_Holmberg_GJ3473b]] — Hot Rocks Survey V; four MIRI F1500W eclipses of GJ 3473 b; joint depth 186 ± 45 ppm, R = 0.82 ± 0.12; both bare-rock and atmosphere consistent.
- [[2025_Connors_MIRI-15um]] — uniform FN-PCA reanalysis of 17 MIRI F1500W eclipses across 5 rocky planets (TRAPPIST-1 b/c, LHS 1478 b, TOI-1468 b, LHS 1140 c); methodology paper introducing [[Erebus]].
- [[2025_LustigYaeger_WASP17b]] — JWST NIRSpec G395H transit + secondary eclipse of WASP-17 b; first application of iESR-PIE (see [[planetary-infrared-excess]]) for nightside emission.
- [[2025_Gressier_WASP17b]] — JWST NIRISS/SOSS dayside secondary eclipse of WASP-17 b; H₂O at 6.4σ; supersolar dayside metallicity.
- [[2026_Coy_HD3167b]] — single MIRI/LRS eclipse of the USP super-Earth HD 3167 b; R = 0.57; ~5σ atmospheric evidence (either high-albedo or heat-redistributing thick atmosphere).
- [[2025_Teske_TOI561b]] — four NIRSpec G395H secondary eclipses of the USP super-Earth TOI-561 b; T_day = 1800–2150 K vs. bare-rock ~2950 K; significant heat redistribution or high albedo; first detection consistent with thick volatile-rich atmosphere on a rocky exoplanet.
- [[2025_Xue_GJ3929b]] — two MIRI **F1500W photometric imaging** secondary eclipses of GJ 3929 b (15 μm single channel; not LRS spectroscopy); T_day = 782 ± 79 K, R = 1.07 ± 0.10; bare-rock; CO₂ > 100 mbar ruled out.
- [[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]] — single MIRI/LRS eclipse of GJ 1132 b; T_day = 709 ± 31 K, R = 0.95 ± 0.04; featureless blackbody spectrum consistent with an airless body; rules out Earth-like and Venus-like atmospheres.
- [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]] — two MIRI/LRS eclipses of Gl 486 b; R = 0.97 ± 0.01, bare-rock consistent; rules out Earth-/Venus-like atmospheres.
- [[2024_Wachiraphan_LTT1445Ab]] — three MIRI/LRS eclipses of LTT 1445 A b; R = 0.95 ± 0.06, bare-rock consistent; rules out 100 bar CO₂ atmospheres at >6σ.

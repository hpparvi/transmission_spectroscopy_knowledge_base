# JWST-NIRCam

The Near-Infrared Camera on JWST (Rieke et al. 2023; not yet ingested), with grism time-series modes (F322W2 covering ~2.4–4.0 μm and F444W covering ~4.0–5.0 μm) for transit and eclipse spectroscopy of bright targets. Less commonly used than [[JWST-NIRSpec]] for small-planet transmission, but valuable for hot/warm-Jupiter transmission and emission, particularly in combination with NIRSpec and MIRI for panchromatic coverage.

## Papers

- [[2025_AcunaAguirre_WASP80b]] — archival JWST NIRCam transmission (2.5–4.0 μm) and emission (F322W2 + F444W) on WASP-80 b combined with HST and JWST/MIRI in a joint interior–atmosphere retrieval.
- [[2025_Murphy_WASP107b]] — NIRCam F322W2 + F444W limb-resolved transit fits on WASP-107 b are the **clean reference instrument** in the panchromatic 1–12 μm analysis; no occulted-spot crossings during either NIRCam visit, used to anchor the spot-crossing correction applied to NIRISS and NIRSpec data.
- [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — re-analyzes Xue et al. 2024 (not ingested) NIRCam F322W2 + F444W transmission of HD 209458 b in combination with HST/STIS + WFC3, demonstrating that NIRCam-only retrievals overestimate H₂O and CO₂ abundances by ~3 dex without an optical baseline.
- [[2025_Mukherjee_GJ436b]] — **3× NIRCam F322W2 + 3× NIRCam F444W eclipses of warm-Neptune GJ 436 b** under [[JWST-GO-1177]] (MANATEE; PI Schlawin); combined with 2× MIRI/LRS for the first panchromatic 2.4–12 μm thermal emission spectrum on a warm Neptune. First wiki **emission spectroscopy** demonstration on a Neptune-mass M-dwarf planet; JWST 3.6 μm flux significantly fainter than Spitzer.

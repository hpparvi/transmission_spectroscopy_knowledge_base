# JWST-NIRSpec

The Near-Infrared Spectrograph on JWST, providing 0.6–5.3 μm spectroscopy. For transiting exoplanet work the most-used modes are the bright-object time-series (BOTS) configurations with the G395H grating (2.8–5.2 μm, R ≈ 2700, two detectors NRS1 and NRS2 separated by a physical gap), its lower-resolution sibling G395M, and the **PRISM** mode (0.6–5.3 μm single-dispersion, R ≈ 100, single detector) used for ultracool-dwarf hosts where photon SNR is the limit. Reference papers: Birkmann et al. 2022 (instrument), Jakobsen et al. 2022 (overview).

## Papers
- [[2025_Teske_TOI561b]] — four G395H secondary eclipses of TOI-561 b (USP super-Earth, K-dwarf, ~10 Gyr); T_day = 1800–2150 K vs. bare-rock ~2950 K; first detection consistent with thick atmosphere on a rocky planet; challenges cosmic shoreline.
- [[2025_PiauletGhorayeb_TRAPPIST1d]] — two PRISM transits of TRAPPIST-1 d; flat ±100–150 ppm; H₂ excluded >3σ; cloud-free Venus/early Mars/modern Earth excluded >95%; N₂-rich consistent.
- [[2025_Fisher_TOI1685b]] — five G395H transits of TOI-1685 b (M2.5V host); flat; MMW ≳ 10 at ~5σ; CO₂, SO₂, H₂O ruled out; bare rock.
- [[2025_Espinoza_TRAPPIST1e]] — four PRISM transits of TRAPPIST-1 e; GP marginalization over stellar contamination; cloudy H₂-dominated atmospheres ruled out >3σ.
- [[2025_Glidden_TRAPPIST1e]] — four PRISM transits of TRAPPIST-1 e (1–5 μm); visits 3–4 stellar-activity-contaminated; combined GP-corrected spectrum featureless; μ > 8.6 u; thick CO₂ ruled out at 6.9σ.
- [[2025_Rathcke_TRAPPIST1bc]] — back-to-back NIRSpec PRISM transits of TRAPPIST-1 b and c on 2024 July 9; single-shot 0.87–4.37 μm white-light coverage with 6 groups/integration on an ultracool host, partially saturating a few pixels at the SED peak.
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — two G395M transits of GJ 1132 b combined with the earlier G395H transits; first demonstration of combining G395H + G395M on a single target, with G395M filling the NRS1/NRS2 gap.
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — three G395H transits of L 168-9 b combined with a MIRI/LRS transit; first broadband NIR + MIR (3–12 μm) transmission spectrum of a rocky exoplanet.
- [[2025_Alderson_TOI-776b]] — two G395H transits of TOI-776 b; flat spectrum after NRS1/NRS2 detector-offset correction; adds to the evidence that detector offsets are a recurring G395H systematic on small planets.
- [[2024_Scarsdale_L98-59c]] — two G395H transits of L 98-59 c under COMPASS; featureless at 22/36 ppm precision in R~200.
- [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] — single G395H transit of L 98-59 d from GTO 1224; atmospheric feature 3.3–4.8 μm preferred at 2.6σ–5.6σ.
- [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]] — retrievals on the same GTO 1224 G395H transit of L 98-59 d; favors sulfur-species atmosphere.
- [[2024_Alderson_TOI-836b]] — two G395H transits of the super-Earth TOI-836 b; flat spectrum, no H₂-dominated atmosphere.
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — two G395H transits of GJ 1132 b; discusses the NRS1/NRS2 detector-offset issue and argues for G395M on faint M-dwarf hosts.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — two G395H transits of GJ 486 b; identifies NRS2 Transit-1 superbias offset; feature detection degenerate between water-rich atmosphere and starspot contamination.
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — two G395H transits of the Earth-sized LHS 475 b; demonstrates no NIRSpec noise floor above 5 ppm.

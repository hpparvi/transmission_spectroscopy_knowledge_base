# HST-STIS

The Space Telescope Imaging Spectrograph on Hubble (Woodgate et al. 1998; not yet ingested). The G430L (0.29–0.57 μm) and G750L (0.52–1.02 μm) spectroscopic modes are the workhorse for HST optical transmission spectroscopy of exoplanets — providing Na, K, and Rayleigh-slope coverage that complements JWST near- and mid-infrared modes.

## Papers

- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — five STIS G430L (290–570 nm) + medium-resolution G750M (581–637 nm) transits of [[HD-189733b]]; the 0.3–0.6 μm STIS coverage anchors the long-baseline UV-to-IR Rayleigh slope used to argue for a panchromatic dust haze.
- [[2026_Heinke_HATP12b]] — archival HST STIS G430L+G750L (0.36–1.01 μm) data combined with JWST NIRISS+NIRSpec+MIRI for panchromatic information-content analysis of HAT-P-12 b.
- [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — archival HST STIS data (Sing et al. 2016; not ingested) on HD 209458 b combined with HST/WFC3 + JWST/NIRCam; the 0.3–1.0 μm STIS coverage anchors temperature and cloud properties and reduces inferred H₂O / CO₂ abundances by ~3 dex relative to NIRCam-only retrieval.

# TESS

NASA Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (Ricker et al. 2015; not ingested). All-sky optical photometric monitor (600–1000 nm, R ~ 1) with four 24° × 24° wide-field CCD cameras, operating in 2- and 10-minute and 200-second cadences across 27-day sectors. Primary mission is detecting transiting exoplanets via short-cadence photometry; secondary use in this wiki is as a **transit-timing baseline** for JWST observations of planets in active multi-planet systems (e.g. the 2:1 MMR pair in [[TOI-1130]] requires careful TTV-aware JWST scheduling) and as an **optical-baseline anchor** in panchromatic transmission spectra (e.g. [[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]] combines TESS + CHEOPS + NIRISS SOSS + NIRSpec G395H). The TESS optical band is also occasionally used to anchor reflected-light constraints (e.g. [[2025_Splinter_WASP121b]] compares TESS A_g to its NIRISS Order 2 reflected-light measurement on WASP-121 b).

## Papers
- [[2026_Barat_TOI1130b]] — 20 TESS sectors + 12 CHEOPS transits used to refine transit-timing solution for the 2:1 MMR TTV-active TOI-1130 b.
- [[2025_Splinter_WASP121b]] — TESS 0.6–0.95 μm geometric albedo (Daylan 2021; not ingested) compared with NIRISS Order 2 reflected-light measurement (consistent within 1σ).
- (Implicit in most wiki targets — transit discovery and timing solutions for almost all JWST exoplanet targets ultimately trace to TESS or to Kepler/K2.)

# HST-WFC3

The Wide Field Camera 3 on the Hubble Space Telescope, providing near-infrared spectroscopy via its G141 grism (1.075–1.70 μm, R ≈ 130) and G102 grism (0.8–1.15 μm, R ≈ 210) in spatial-scanning mode for time-series observations of transiting exoplanets. The workhorse instrument for transmission spectroscopy in the pre-JWST era; sensitivity to the 1.4 μm water band made it the primary tool for detecting H₂O in exoplanet atmospheres. Standard reduction pipeline: [[Iraclis]]. Standard retrieval code: [[TauREX3]] (ARES survey) or other codes.

## Papers
- [[2014_McCullough_HD189733b]] — single G141 spatial-scan transit of [[HD-189733b]]; first detection of H₂O on this target (200 ± 47 ppm at 1.4 μm + 83 ± 53 ppm at 1.15 μm); reinterprets Pont et al.'s panchromatic Rayleigh slope as TLS contamination on the active K-dwarf host.
- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — two G141 transits of HD 189733 b combined with the panchromatic STIS+ACS+NICMOS+Spitzer dataset; flat 1.10–1.69 μm transmission consistent with muted (≤ 1 scale height) molecular features.
- [[2021_Mugnai_GJ1132b]] — five G141 transits of GJ 1132 b (2017 Apr–Nov); featureless; rules out H₂-dominated atmospheres; directly rebuts Swain et al. 2021 claim.
- [[2026_Heinke_HATP12b]] — archival G141 1.12–1.64 μm transit included in the HAT-P-12 b panchromatic information-content study (combined with new JWST observations).
- [[2025_Verma_HD209458b]] — archival G141 transit of HD 209458 b (Deming et al. 2013; not ingested) combined with HST/STIS and JWST/NIRCam; the 1.4 μm water-band coverage anchors temperature in the SANSAR retrievals.
- [[2025_Barat_V1298Tau-b]] — archival G141 transit of the young sub-Neptune progenitor V1298 Tau b (Barat et al. 2024b; not ingested); offset by 400 ppm to match JWST visit due to stellar variability between epochs; combined with JWST/NIRSpec G395H for panchromatic 1.0–5.2 μm transmission.

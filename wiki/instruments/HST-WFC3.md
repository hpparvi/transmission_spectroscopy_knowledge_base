# HST-WFC3

The Wide Field Camera 3 on the Hubble Space Telescope, providing near-infrared spectroscopy via its G141 grism (1.075–1.70 μm, R ≈ 130) and G102 grism (0.8–1.15 μm, R ≈ 210) in spatial-scanning mode for time-series observations of transiting exoplanets. The workhorse instrument for transmission spectroscopy in the pre-JWST era; sensitivity to the 1.4 μm water band made it the primary tool for detecting H₂O in exoplanet atmospheres. Standard reduction pipeline: [[Iraclis]]. Standard retrieval code: [[TauREX3]] (ARES survey) or other codes.

## Papers
- [[2021_Mugnai_GJ1132b]] — five G141 transits of GJ 1132 b (2017 Apr–Nov); featureless; rules out H₂-dominated atmospheres; directly rebuts Swain et al. 2021 claim.
- [[2026_Heinke_HATP12b]] — archival G141 1.12–1.64 μm transit included in the HAT-P-12 b panchromatic information-content study (combined with new JWST observations).

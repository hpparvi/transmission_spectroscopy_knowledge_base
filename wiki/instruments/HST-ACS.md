# HST-ACS

The Advanced Camera for Surveys on Hubble. The G800L grism (550–1050 nm, R ≈ 70 in slitless mode at the bright-target spatial-scan / staring configuration) provided an early-generation HST optical spectrograph for transit transmission spectroscopy in the 2006–2009 era, prior to STIS post-COSTAR repair operations becoming routine and prior to WFC3 spatial-scanning availability. Best known for the Pont et al. HD 189733 b campaigns.

## Papers

- [[2008_Pont_HD189733b]] — three G800L transits of [[HD-189733b]] (550–1050 nm); first conclusive haze claim in an exoplanet atmosphere.
- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — same G800L data combined with STIS + WFC3 + NICMOS + Spitzer for the panchromatic 0.3–24 μm spectrum.

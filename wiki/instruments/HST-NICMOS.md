# HST-NICMOS

The Near Infrared Camera and Multi-Object Spectrometer on Hubble (1.0–2.5 μm), used in two distinct modes for transit transmission spectroscopy:

- **Spectroscopic** (G206 grism, 1.4–2.5 μm, R ≈ 200) — the Swain, Vasisht & Tinetti 2008 (not yet ingested) HD 189733 b spectroscopic measurements; reduction systematics later challenged by Gibson, Pont & Aigrain 2011/2012a (not yet ingested), with Gaussian-process re-analysis yielding much larger uncertainties. **Considered unreliable** for high-precision atmospheric inference by [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]].
- **Photometric** (F166N, F187N filters near 1.66 / 1.87 μm; F237M and others) — band-integrated photometry less sensitive to the spectroscopic systematics above; retained in panchromatic combined fits.

Superseded for transit transmission by HST/WFC3 (post-2010) and JWST/NIRSpec/NIRISS (post-2022).

## Papers

- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — F166N + F187N photometric points retained, G206 spectroscopy not used; covers 1.4–2.5 μm in the panchromatic spectrum of [[HD189733b]].

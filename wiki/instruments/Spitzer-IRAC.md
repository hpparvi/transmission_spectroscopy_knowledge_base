# Spitzer-IRAC

The Infrared Array Camera on the Spitzer Space Telescope, providing photometry in four bandpasses (3.6, 4.5, 5.8, 8.0 μm) — the workhorse mid-IR instrument for hot-Jupiter transmission, secondary-eclipse, and phase-curve photometry from 2003 to the post-cryogenic Spitzer mission ending in 2020. Plagued by detector "ramp" and "pixel-phase" effects; reduction approaches converged to BLISS / pixel-level decorrelation in the late 2010s. Largely superseded by [[JWST-MIRI]] and [[JWST-NIRCam]] for hot-Jupiter work.

## Papers

- [[2013_Pont_HD189733b]] — 3.6 / 4.5 / 5.8 / 8 μm photometric transit depths on [[HD189733b]] from Désert et al. 2009/2011, Knutson et al. 2007/2009/2012, Agol et al. 2010 (none yet ingested); the 8 μm/4.5 μm opacity inversion + 3.6 μm phase-curve amplitude provide key constraints supporting the dusty-atmosphere interpretation.

# JWST-MIRI

The Mid-Infrared Instrument on JWST (Rieke et al. 2015; Kendrew et al. 2015; Bouwman et al. 2023), covering 5–28 μm. The Low-Resolution Spectrometer (**LRS**) slitless mode is the primary configuration for transiting exoplanet time-series, probing ~5–12 μm with resolving power R ~ 50–100. MIRI can also be used in **photometric imaging** mode with broadband filters (e.g., F1500W at 15 μm), providing a single temperature point rather than a spectrum. Complementary to [[JWST-NIRSpec]] for small-planet atmospheres: MIRI enables detection of CO₂ (15 μm), O₃, and high-MMW signatures inaccessible to NIRSpec.

## Papers
- [[2025_Xue_GJ3929b]] — two MIRI **F1500W photometric imaging** secondary eclipses of GJ 3929 b (NOT LRS spectroscopy); joint eclipse depth 160 ± 26 ppm; T_day = 782 ± 79 K, R = 1.07 ± 0.10; bare-rock; CO₂ > 100 mbar excluded.
- [[2025_Alam_L168-9b]] — single MIRI/LRS transit of L 168-9 b (commissioning program COM 1033), giving the first NIR + MIR (3–12 μm) transmission spectrum of a rocky exoplanet; featureless across both wavelength ranges.
- [[2024_Xue_GJ1132b]] — single MIRI/LRS secondary eclipse of GJ 1132 b; T_day = 709 ± 31 K, R = 0.95 ± 0.04; bare-rock consistent.
- [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]] — two MIRI/LRS secondary eclipses of Gl 486 b; dayside T = 865±14 K, temperature ratio R = 0.97±0.01, consistent with an airless body.
- [[2024_Wachiraphan_LTT1445Ab]] — three MIRI/LRS secondary eclipses of LTT 1445 A b; rules out thick atmospheres (100 bar CO₂ at >6σ).

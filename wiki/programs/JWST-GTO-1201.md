# JWST GTO 1201

JWST Cycle 1 Guaranteed Time Observations program 1201 (PI: D. Lafrenière), part of the **NEAT** survey (Near-infrared Exploration of the Atmospheric diversity of Transiting exoplanets). Uses both [[JWST-NIRISS]] SOSS (0.85–2.8 μm) and [[JWST-NIRSpec]] PRISM (0.6–5.2 μm) to observe transiting rocky and sub-Neptune planets across a range of host spectral types. Multi-target survey; confirmed targets include [[GJ357b]], TRAPPIST-1 d, and L 98-59 b/d (Damiano et al. 2024 for L 98-59 b; not yet ingested), with L 98-59 d NIRISS follow-up also referenced in [[2024_Gressier_L98-59d]] and [[2024_Banerjee_L98-59d]]. NIRISS reductions use the [[exoTEDRF]] pipeline; NIRSpec reductions use [[Eureka]] and [[stctm]].

## Papers
- [[2025_PiauletGhorayeb_TRAPPIST1d]] — two NIRSpec PRISM transits of TRAPPIST-1 d (Nov 5+9 2022); flat spectrum; H₂ excluded >3σ; cloud-free terrestrial analogs excluded >95%; N₂-rich consistent.
- [[2025_Taylor_GJ357b]] — single NIRISS/SOSS transit of GJ 357 b on 2023-11-22; featureless spectrum.

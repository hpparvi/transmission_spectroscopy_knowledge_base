# JWST GO 3838 — BOWIE-ALIGN

JWST Cycle 2 GO program 3838 (PIs: J. Kirk & E.-M. Ahrer), the BOWIE-ALIGN survey: a comparative atmospheric study of **aligned vs misaligned hot Jupiters** orbiting F-type stars (above the Kraft break, T_eff ≳ 6100 K, where stellar spin is undamped). The premise: aligned hot Jupiters are presumed to have undergone **disc migration** (preserving the protoplanetary disc's alignment), while misaligned ones underwent **high-eccentricity migration** (post-disc dynamical perturbations). If the two populations have systematically different atmospheric C/O ratios and metallicities, those abundances trace the formation pathway. Comparison to a sample of 4–6 planets is the survey deliverable; KELT-7 b is the third target.

## Targets

- **WASP-15 b** (misaligned) — Kirk et al. 2025 (not yet ingested): super-solar metallicity, solar C/O. Consistent with planetesimal accretion.
- **TrES-4 b** (aligned) — Meech et al. 2025 (not yet ingested): sub-solar metallicity, sub-solar C/O. Consistent with oxygen-rich gas accretion.
- **[[KELT-7b]]** (aligned) — [[2025_Ahrer_KELT7b]]: weak features; high cloud OR low metallicity; broad C/O range 0.25–0.9.
- Three more targets pending publication.

## Methodology

- Single JWST NIRSpec G395H transit per target (R ≈ 2700 native, R ≈ 100–400 binned).
- Three-pipeline cross-check: [[Eureka]] + [[ExoTiC-JEDI]] + [[Tiberius]].
- Two retrieval frameworks: [[POSEIDON]] + [[petitRADTRANS]] (free + equilibrium chemistry).
- Stellar contamination not a primary concern (F-star hosts above Kraft break).

## Papers

- [[2025_Ahrer_KELT7b]] — KELT-7 b NIRSpec G395H transit; weak H₂O + CO₂; cloud-or-low-metallicity ambiguity; C/O = 0.43–0.74, metallicity 1–16× solar.

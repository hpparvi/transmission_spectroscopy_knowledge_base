# JWST GO 4098

JWST Cycle 2 GO program 4098 (PIs: B. Benneke & D. Evans-Soma); NIRSpec G395H + NIRISS SOSS multi-instrument transit observations of the temperate sub-Neptune [[TOI-270d]] in the [[TOI-270]] system. Designed to probe the atmospheric composition of a relatively cold sub-Neptune to test the hycean-vs-metal-rich-envelope dichotomy, paralleling the analogous [[K2-18b]] debate. The data have been analyzed three times — Benneke et al. 2024 (fiducial; not yet ingested), Holmberg & Madhusudhan 2024 (hycean; not yet ingested), and [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] — with two of three converging on a metal-rich miscible-envelope reading. The G395H visit also fortuitously captured a simultaneous transit of [[TOI-270b]] (analyzed in [[2025_Coulombe_TOI-270b]]), enabling a methodological-first stellar-contamination cross-check of the inner super-Earth using the larger sibling.

## Papers

- [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] — independent Eureka! + BeAR reanalysis of both NIRSpec G395H (2023-10-04) and NIRISS SOSS (2024-02-06) transits; reproduces Benneke 2024 but not Holmberg & Madhusudhan 2024; CH₄ + CO₂ decisive; tentative CS₂; SO₂ retracted.
- [[2025_Coulombe_TOI-270b]] — focused analysis of the simultaneous TOI-270 b transit from the same G395H visit; updated bulk density (3.7 ± 0.5 g cm⁻³, 4.4σ underdense vs. Earth-like); tentative H₂O feature at lnB = 0.3–3.2; TOI-270 d transit used as TLS control to rule out stellar contamination.

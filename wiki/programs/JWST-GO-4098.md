# JWST GO 4098

JWST Cycle 2 GO program 4098 (PIs: B. Benneke & T. Evans-Soma); a **sub-Neptune survey** combining NIRSpec/G395H and NIRISS/SOSS transmission observations across multiple targets, motivated by the goal of "exploring the existence and diversity of volatile-rich water worlds" — testing the Hycean / steam-atmosphere / metal-rich-envelope continuum across warm sub-Neptunes around late-type stars. The G395H + NIRISS combination is intended to break the aerosol-vs-MMW degeneracy via panchromatic coverage, though [[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]] notes that NIRISS/SOSS spectra of active M-dwarf hosts can be dominated by the [[stellar-contamination]] (TLS) effect with visit-to-visit variability when scheduled months apart — a methodological lesson for the program.

## Papers

- [[2025_Felix_TOI-270d]] — independent Eureka! + BeAR reanalysis of both NIRSpec G395H (2023-10-04) and NIRISS SOSS (2024-02-06) [[TOI-270d]] transits; reproduces Benneke 2024 but not Holmberg & Madhusudhan 2024; CH₄ + CO₂ decisive; tentative CS₂; SO₂ retracted.
- [[2025_Coulombe_TOI-270b]] — focused analysis of the simultaneous TOI-270 b transit from the same G395H visit; updated bulk density (3.7 ± 0.5 g cm⁻³, 4.4σ underdense vs. Earth-like); tentative H₂O feature; TOI-270 d transit used as TLS control.
- [[2025_Ahrer_GJ3090b]] — first JWST sub-Neptune helium detection (5.5σ at 1083 nm) on the 693 K [[GJ3090b]]; muted features → high-metallicity (>100× solar at 3σ; >700× solar at 2σ regardless of clouds) consistent with prior CRIRES+ ground-based non-detection; demonstrates TLS-dominated NIRISS/SOSS spectra and a 100 ppm offset to NIRSpec/G395H driven by cross-instrument visit timing.

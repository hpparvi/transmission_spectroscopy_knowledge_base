# JWST GO 2571

JWST Cycle 1 GO program 2571 (PI: J. Libby-Roberts); NIRSpec/PRISM Bright Object Time Series transmission observation of the [[super-puff]] [[Kepler-51d]] across 0.6–5.3 μm. Single ∼14 hr visit on 2023-06-26 (8.5 hr transit) using the SUB512 subarray. Primary goal: test photochemical-haze, dusty-outflow, and tilted-ring hypotheses for the planet's extreme low density and inflated radius.

## Papers

- [[2025_LibbyRoberts_Kepler51d]] — sloped near-featureless 0.6–5.3 μm spectrum; favors high-altitude photochemical haze (1–100 μbar, sub-micron particles) on top of an extended H/He envelope; tilted-ring alternative ruled out as steady state on Pointing–Robertson lifetime grounds.

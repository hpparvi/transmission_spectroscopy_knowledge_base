# JWST Rocky Worlds DDT

JWST Director's Discretionary Time program (PI: J. Lustig-Yaeger, K. B. Stevenson; not yet ingested as a standalone proposal) collecting **MIRI/F1500W secondary-eclipse photometry** at 15 μm of a small sample of M-dwarf rocky planets selected for high community interest along the [[cosmic-shoreline]]. The 15 μm filter is chosen to bracket the CO₂ ν₂ band — a primary atmosphere-vs-bare-rock discriminator at low S/N. Most planets in the program are also being observed in transmission via NIRSpec G395H or NIRISS SOSS through GO programs.

The program is referenced in this wiki as the planned F1500W follow-up for [[LTT1445Ab]] ([[2026_Wogan_LTT1445Ab]] forecasts ≥ 4 visits at 20 ppm precision would detect the thickest LRS-allowed atmosphere) and for tying together transmission + emission constraints on the COMPASS sample more broadly. Distinct from the separate **Hot Rocks Survey** ([[JWST-GO-3730]], PI Diamond-Lowe) which used a Cycle 2 GO allocation rather than DDT time.

# JWST GO 1981 — CHAMPs

JWST Cycle 1 GO program 1981 (PIs: K. Stevenson & J. Lustig-Yaeger), observing five rocky M-dwarf planets with orbital and planetary properties close to the solar system's cosmic shoreline, aiming to assess atmospheric survivability. Published targets include LHS 475 b ([[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]]), GJ 486 b ([[2023_Moran_GJ486b]], [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]]), and GJ 1132 b ([[2023_May_GJ1132b]], [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]]).

## Papers
- [[2025_Bennett_GJ1132b]] — two additional NIRSpec G395M transits of GJ 1132 b; coadded with the earlier G395H data the spectrum is flat, consistent with a bare rock.
- [[2023_May_GJ1132b]] — two NIRSpec G395H transits of GJ 1132 b from the program.
- [[2023_Moran_GJ486b]] — two NIRSpec G395H transits of GJ 486 b; transmission spectrum shows 2.2σ–3.3σ deviation from flat, with H₂O-rich atmosphere and unocculted starspots as two equally-viable scenarios (later resolved in favor of starspots by [[2024_WeinerMansfield_GJ486b]]).
- [[2023_Lustig-Yaeger_LHS475b]] — two NIRSpec G395H transits of the Earth-sized rocky planet LHS 475 b; featureless spectrum rules out H₂-dominated atmospheres at >10σ.

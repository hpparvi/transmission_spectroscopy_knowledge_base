# JWST GO 2488

JWST Cycle 1 GO program 2488 (PI: J. T. Sikora); single 20.9-hour partial phase curve of the highly eccentric hot Jupiter [[HD80606b]] using NIRSpec G395H/F290LP, centered on the secondary eclipse and periapse passage on 2022 November 1. Designed to capture the transient atmospheric response to the rapid (~17-hour) ~860× increase in stellar irradiation as the planet sweeps through periapse — testing GCM + photochemistry predictions for thermal inversion, CH₄ → CO conversion, and cloud responses.

## Papers

- [[2025_Sikora_HD80606b]] — first atmospheric characterization of HD 80606 b with JWST; CH₄, H₂O, CO₂ detected post-periapse; T_bright rises 892 K → 1313 K then decays; **rules out strong temperature inversion** despite GCM predictions; chemical-equilibrium retrievals preferred over free-chemistry.
